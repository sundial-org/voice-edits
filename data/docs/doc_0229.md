# Hospital IT Resilience Lecture Notes

## Session Overview
These notes cover the March 14, 2024 infrastructure review led by Dr. Elena Vasquez for the hospital IT group. Our primary objective was to assess the resilience of clinical systems after the recent network segmentation project completed on February 28. The session included representatives from network engineering, clinical informatics, and cybersecurity.

## Incident Timeline
On March 12, 2024, at 03:17, automated monitoring flagged latency spikes in the east-wing VLAN. By 04:05, the EHR database mirror at Site B showed replication lag of 47 minutes. Clinical staff reported intermittent login failures through the Citrix gateway, though no patient data was lost. The root cause was traced to an unpatched firmware release on switch cluster SW-09 that conflicted with the new VLAN tagging rules.

## Remediation Plan
The group agreed on a phased recovery approach to avoid disrupting the 06:00 nursing shift change. Immediate actions included rolling back the firmware on SW-09 and re-establishing synchronous replication. Long-term, we will implement:

- Quarterly firmware audits starting April 1, 2024
- Redundant replication paths with a 15-minute failover target
- Mandatory change-advisory board sign-off for all VLAN modifications

## Metrics and Targets
Current system availability sits at 99.97% over the trailing 90 days. Our target is 99.99% by Q3, which allows for no more than 52 minutes of unplanned downtime per year. Dr. Vasquez emphasized that clinical uptime is a patient-safety metric, not just an IT benchmark.

## Follow-Up Actions
Network engineering will deliver the updated topology diagram by March 18, 2024. Cybersecurity will complete the penetration-test review of the segmented zones by March 25. All staff should review the revised incident-response playbook before the April 2 drill.
