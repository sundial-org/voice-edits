# Internal FAQ — Resonance Collective

## Who runs this collective, and when do we meet?
Resonance Collective is co-led by Marcus Chen and producer duo Vega & Slate. We gather every Tuesday at 19:00 in Studio B, and our shared drive is maintained by Juno Park.

## What gear and software do we share?
Our collective pool includes:
- Two Universal Audio Apollo x8p interfaces
- A 1978 Roland Juno-60, restored in March 2024
- One pair of Neumann KH 310 monitors
- Shared licenses for Ableton Live 12 Suite and Pro Tools 2023
- A communal patch-bay labeled with color tape only—do not reorder cables

## How are royalties and session fees split?
For releases under the Resonance label, net revenue splits 60/30/10 among primary artist, featured collaborator, and collective maintenance fund. Session engineers charge $45/hour, but in-house members log hours against the pool instead of invoicing.

## What's the booking protocol for Studio B?
Reserve the room via the shared calendar at least 48 hours before your session. Blockouts longer than six hours require approval from Marcus. If you cancel within 12 hours, you forfeit your slot for the following week.

## Communication rules and conflict resolution
All project updates go in the #production-log thread; personal messages for mix feedback are discouraged. Disputes over masters go to a three-person review board: Chen, Park, and one rotating member. Decisions are final within 72 hours.

## Archive and backup policy
Every finished multitrack must be uploaded to the NAS by the first Friday of the month. We maintain three redundant backups, with the oldest rotation dated January 2022. If your project is missing, check the cold-storage drive in the equipment closet before asking in chat.
