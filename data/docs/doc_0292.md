# PRD: Summit Roast Direct-to-Consumer Web Platform

## Overview
Summit Roast, founded in 2018 in Portland, needs a direct-to-consumer web platform to replace its aging wholesale portal. By March 15, 2025, we will launch a responsive storefront that supports subscription management, single-origin checkout, and wholesale account portals in one unified experience.

## Goals and Success Metrics
We aim to increase direct revenue by 35% within the first two quarters of launch. Key performance indicators include reducing checkout abandonment from 42% to under 18% and growing the subscriber base to 5,000 active members by June 2025.

## Target Users
- Home enthusiasts purchasing 12 oz bags of single-origin beans
- Cafe partners placing weekly wholesale orders exceeding 25 lbs
- Gift buyers seeking curated seasonal boxes delivered monthly

## Functional Requirements
The platform must support:
- One-click reorder for past wholesale invoices
- Real-time inventory visibility for the Ethiopia Yirgacheffe and Colombia Huila lots
- Subscription pause, skip, and swap functionality with a 48-hour cutoff before roast day
- Integration with ShipStation for domestic and international logistics

## Technical Architecture
The stack will use Next.js 14 on Vercel, backed by a PostgreSQL database via Supabase. Payment processing runs through Stripe. All user data must comply with GDPR and CCPA standards, with encryption at rest using AES-256.

## Timeline and Milestones
- February 1, 2025: Design approval and API contract finalized
- February 28, 2025: Beta release for 50 internal testers and two partner cafes
- March 15, 2025: Public launch with marketing campaign led by Creative Director Mara Olsen

## Risks and Mitigations
Supply chain volatility for green coffee could delay product page updates if inventory sync fails. We will implement a manual override dashboard and maintain a 10% buffer stock alert threshold to prevent overselling during peak harvest releases.
