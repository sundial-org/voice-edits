# BrightFuture Learning: 2025 Digital Equity Summit Event Plan

## Overview and Objectives
BrightFuture Learning, an edtech nonprofit serving under-resourced districts, will host the 2025 Digital Equity Summit on March 14, 2025, at the Riverside Convention Center. The single-day convening aims to equip 180 educators and 40 district technology leaders with practical strategies for closing connectivity gaps. Our primary objective is to finalize three pilot partnerships—specifically with Oakland Unified, Riverside County, and the Delta Collaborative—before the close of Q2 2025.

## Program Design and Agenda
The summit runs from 8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. Keynote speaker Dr. Amina Okonkwo will open with data on broadband adoption in rural classrooms. Morning breakout sessions will cover device lifecycle management, student data privacy, and community Wi-Fi mapping. Afternoon workshops will shift to hands-on curriculum integration using open-source platforms.

- Registration and coffee: 8:00 a.m. – 8:30 a.m.
- Keynote and panel: 9:00 a.m. – 10:30 a.m.
- Breakout rotation A: 11:00 a.m. – 12:15 p.m.
- Lunch and networking: 12:15 p.m. – 1:15 p.m.
- Breakout rotation B: 1:30 p.m. – 3:00 p.m.
- Closing commitments and regional planning: 3:15 p.m. – 4:00 p.m.

## Logistics and Partnerships
We have secured in-kind venue sponsorship from Riverside Convention Center and equipment loans from TechAccess Foundation. Catering will be provided by GreenPlate Catering for $18 per attendee, with a confirmed headcount deadline of February 28, 2025. Accessibility services—including ASL interpretation and Spanish-language materials—are contracted through LanguageBridge Services. Volunteer coordination will be managed by Programs Director Marcus Chen, who will recruit 22 classroom teachers and 8 technology coaches.

## Budget and Impact Metrics
The event operates on a $42,500 budget, with 60 percent already covered by the Hewlett Foundation grant awarded in January 2025. We anticipate reaching 220 direct participants and distributing 350 take-home toolkits containing hotspot guides, accessibility checklists, and open-license lesson plans. Post-event surveys will measure confidence gains in digital equity planning using a 5-point Likert scale. Success will be defined as 75 percent of attendees completing a 90-day action plan with their home districts.

## Next Steps
By February 10, 2025, the communications team will release the registration portal. By February 28, we will confirm catering numbers and print materials. The evaluation report, led by Research Associate Priya Nair, is due by April 30, 2025, and will inform our fall 2025 regional training series. All partner districts will receive a preliminary memorandum of understanding by March 7, 2025, ensuring alignment before the summit convenes.
