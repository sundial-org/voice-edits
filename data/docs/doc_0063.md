# Fintech App Research Notes — Q3 Discovery

## Project Context
This document captures findings from the discovery sprint for the Meridian Pay mobile wallet redesign, conducted between August 12 and September 3, 2024. The goal was to identify friction points in the onboarding flow and evaluate competitor positioning in the European neobank space.

## User Interviews
We spoke with 14 active users and 6 churned customers across Berlin and Madrid. The feedback was consistent: users valued speed over feature density.

- 9 out of 14 users abandoned onboarding at the KYC verification step
- 4 participants specifically mentioned Revolut as their backup account
- 3 respondents cited the 14-day trial limit as a reason for not upgrading

The qualitative data suggests we need to reduce identity verification to under 90 seconds.

## Competitive Analysis
We benchmarked three direct competitors: N26, Monzo, and Revolut. N26 launched its new savings tier on July 22, which may shift user expectations around interest rates. Monzo continues to dominate in UK user satisfaction scores, but its EU rollout remains limited.

Meridian Pay currently processes approximately 2.3 million transactions monthly. To remain competitive, we should prioritize instant SEPA transfers and a transparent fee dashboard.

## Quantitative Metrics
Our analytics review covered the period from June 1 to August 31. Key observations:

- Daily active users grew by 18 percent quarter-over-quarter
- Average session duration dropped from 4.2 minutes to 3.1 minutes
- Support tickets related to card freezing increased 34 percent in August

These figures indicate that while acquisition is healthy, retention mechanics require immediate attention.

## Next Steps
By October 15, the product team will finalize a revised onboarding prototype. We will run a second round of usability testing with 20 participants and compare conversion rates against the baseline established in August. All changes must align with PSD2 compliance requirements before release.
