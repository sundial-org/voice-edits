# Solar Installer Onboarding Guide

Welcome to Helios Residential Solar. This guide covers your first 30 days on the crew, from safety certification through your first independent site assignment.

## Week One: Safety and Certification

Before you step onto a rooftop, you must complete the OSHA 30-Hour Construction Safety course and receive your NABCEP Associate credential packet by March 15. All installers report to Lead Supervisor Marcus Chen for their morning briefing at 6:30 AM sharp. You will be issued a hard hat, Class 0 rubber gloves, and a personal fall-arrest harness rated for 310 lbs. Do not share harnesses; each unit is serialized to its owner.

## Tools and Equipment

Your standard kit includes a Milwaukee M18 drill driver, a Fluke 393 FC clamp meter, and a torque wrench calibrated to 18 Nm for rail attachments. Keep your inverter commissioning tablet charged above 40 percent at all times. The warehouse opens at 5:45 AM; arrive 15 minutes early to inspect your ladder straps and anchor bolts.

## Field Protocol

Every residential array follows this order:
- Verify roof pitch with a digital inclinometer before unpacking
- Mark rafter locations with chalk lines every 48 inches
- Install flashing under every bracket foot
- Torque all bolts to 18 Nm and photograph each rail joint
- Complete the digital commissioning form before leaving the driveway

Remember: no live DC connections until your journeyman partner gives verbal clearance.

## First Solo Assignment

By April 10, you should be ready for a 6.8 kW residential system in the Westfield district. The project uses 18 REC Alpha Pure panels and a SolarEdge SE6000H inverter. Marcus will shadow you for the first two arrays, then observe from ground level only. Expect a two-day install with a 12-panel south-facing roof and 6 panels on the garage. Submit your final inspection photos through the HelioPortal by 4:00 PM on the second day.

## Questions and Contacts

If you encounter damaged modules, missing flashings, or weather delays, contact Dispatch at extension 442. For payroll or benefits issues, reach HR coordinator Dana Reeves directly. Welcome to the team—build it clean, build it safe, and build it to last.
