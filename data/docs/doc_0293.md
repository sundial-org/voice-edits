# Welcome to the Meridian Stargazers

Welcome to the Meridian Stargazers, founded in 1987 by Dr. Elena Vasquez. Our club connects local observers with the night sky through guided viewing, education, and community outreach. Whether you own a telescope or are just learning the constellations, this guide will help you settle in quickly.

## Getting Started

Your first month is designed to be low-pressure and informative. Attend the orientation on the first Saturday at 7:00 PM in the community observatory. You will receive a membership card, a star chart for the current season, and a welcome packet that includes our safety protocols. New members are paired with a mentor for their first three observing sessions.

## Membership and Dues

Annual dues are $45 for individuals and $70 for families, due by March 15 each year. These fees cover equipment maintenance, insurance for our Dark Sky Site near Granite Ridge, and guest speaker honorariums. Payment can be made online or at any general meeting. Members in good standing may reserve the club's 8-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope up to twice per month.

## Observing Schedule

We meet every Friday and Saturday from dusk until 11:00 PM, weather permitting. The club hosts three major events annually:
- The Perseid Meteor Watch on August 12
- The Autumn Deep-Sky Marathon on October 3
- The Winter Solstice Star Party on December 21

Check the weather cancellation line after 5:00 PM on meeting days.

## Equipment and Resources

Members have access to a shared library of eyepieces, filters, and binoculars. Before using any club instrument, complete the 20-minute equipment orientation led by James Park. Personal telescopes are welcome at all events; please bring a red flashlight and a notebook.

## Code of Conduct

Respect dark adaptation, announce movements near telescopes, and keep voices low after 9:30 PM. Alcohol and smoking are prohibited at the observing field. If you need to leave early, notify the site coordinator so we can assist with parking in the unlit lot.

We are excited to have you. Clear skies!
