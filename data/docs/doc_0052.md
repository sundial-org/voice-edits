# Metro Transit Authority — Weekly Status Update (Week of October 14, 2024)

## Executive Summary
This week, the Metro Transit Authority (MTA) completed critical infrastructure inspections across the Blue Line corridor and advanced the Riverside Bus Rapid Transit pilot. Ridership on the Green Line reached 42,300 average weekday passengers, up 4.2 percent from September. All revenue service remains on schedule through October 18.

## Service Performance & Operations
On-time performance for fixed-route bus service held at 91.7 percent despite detours near Central Station for the Meridian Bridge project. Rail operations maintained 98.1 percent reliability with only two minor delays exceeding five minutes.

Key service adjustments effective October 21 include:
- Extended evening frequency on Route 14 to every 12 minutes until 11:00 p.m.
- Temporary suspension of the Northgate Express stop for ADA platform reconstruction, estimated through November 8.
- Addition of three supplemental trips on Route 82 during the 7:00 a.m. peak.

## Safety & Security Updates
The Safety Division reported 14 near-miss incidents, down from 22 the prior week. Officer Denise Alvarez led refresher training for 68 operators on emergency evacuation protocols. All 24 station CCTV nodes at Parkside and Harbor Junction passed quarterly diagnostics.

## Capital Projects & Maintenance
Engineering crews replaced 380 feet of third-rail insulation along the Blue Line between Harbor Junction and Eastlake. The Riverside BRT pilot, budgeted at $4.8 million, entered Phase 2 with signal priority installation at six intersections. Project manager Samuel Okonkwo confirmed the October 30 milestone remains achievable.

## Community & Communications
Public outreach staff hosted two open houses at the Civic Center on October 12 and October 16, collecting 217 feedback forms regarding proposed fare adjustments for January 2025. Customer service handled 3,841 inquiries with an average resolution time of 4.3 minutes.

## Next Week Outlook
Focus shifts to winter weather readiness drills scheduled for October 23. The Board of Directors will review the revised capital plan on October 25 at 2:00 p.m. in the Chambers Room. All staff should submit maintenance logs by close of business Friday, October 18.
