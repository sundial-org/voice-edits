# Orion Astronomy Club — Weekly Status Update (Oct 14)

## Observatory Maintenance & Upgrades
This past Tuesday, facilities lead Dr. Elena Vasquez completed the collimation check on the 14-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain. The primary mirror alignment was off by 0.4 millimeters, which we corrected before Wednesday's public viewing. The dome rotation motor also received fresh lubrication, reducing the noise level during tracking by roughly 30 percent.

## Upcoming Events
Our next dark-sky outing is scheduled for October 21 at Pine Ridge State Park. We will meet at the east parking lot by 7:30 PM. Please bring red flashlights, star charts, and signed liability waivers. The forecast currently shows 10 percent cloud cover and a low of 42°F, so dress in layers.

## Member Highlights
- Marcus Chen captured an exceptional 4-hour mosaic of the Andromeda Galaxy using the club's cooled CMOS camera.
- New member Sarah Okonkwo completed her Messier Marathon checklist, logging all 110 objects.
- Veteran observer James Whitfield donated three vintage eyepieces (12 mm, 25 mm, and 40 mm) to the lending library.

## Education Corner
This Thursday at 6:00 PM, Professor David Liu will present "Gravitational Lensing in the Local Group" in Room 302. The talk will cover recent Hubble data and simulations run on the campus cluster. Attendance is free for members and $5 for guests.

We currently have 87 active members, up from 81 in August. Annual dues for 2024 remain at $45. Treasurer Amara Okafor reports that the telescope repair fund stands at $1,240, putting us on track to replace the guide-scope mount by November 15.
