# Metro Transit Weekly Status Update — Week of October 14

## Service Performance
Rider volume held steady at 1.24 million trips across the bus and rail network, matching projections for mid-October. On-time performance improved to 91.3 percent following schedule adjustments on the Blue and Green lines. The most significant delay event occurred Tuesday, October 15, when a signal fault near Central Station added 18 minutes to southbound service during the morning peak.

## Infrastructure & Maintenance
Track crews completed rail grinding along the 4.2-mile Red Line corridor between Maplewood and Riverside, reducing wheel noise and extending rail life. Station maintenance teams replaced 340 platform tiles at Union Terminal and installed new LED wayfinding signage. The underground ventilation upgrade at Harbor Tunnel remains on schedule for completion by November 8.

## Fleet & Operations
Bus division 3 received 12 new 40-foot electric vehicles, bringing the zero-emission fleet to 87 units. Mechanics resolved the recurring door fault on coach 2219, returning it to revenue service Wednesday. The weekend shuttle supporting the Riverfront Arts Festival carried 8,400 passengers over three days, operating every 10 minutes from 10 a.m. to midnight.

## Upcoming Changes
- Express Route 42 will resume full weekday service on Monday, October 21, with stops added at Market and 5th Street.
- Eastside Park-and-Ride will open its expanded 210-space lot on November 1, including 30 EV charging ports.
- Fare capping for low-income riders enrolled in the Horizon Pass program takes effect November 4.

## Community Engagement
Transit ambassadors distributed 2,100 safety guides at schools along the Orange Line corridor. The October board meeting is set for Thursday, October 24, at 6:00 p.m. in the Civic Center auditorium, with public comment opening at 6:30 p.m. Deputy Director Elena Vasquez will present the proposed 2025 capital budget.
