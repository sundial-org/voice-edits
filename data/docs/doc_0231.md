# Lecture Notes: Roast Dynamics at Black Mountain Roastery

## Introduction and Origin
Founded in 2014 by Elena Vasquez, Black Mountain Roastery began as a single-drum operation in Portland’s Pearl District. Today we process over 14,000 pounds of green coffee monthly, but the core philosophy remains unchanged: heat is a language, not a weapon. These notes summarize the lecture delivered on March 12, 2024, for our apprentice cohort.

## Green Coffee Fundamentals
Before flame touches steel, we must understand density and moisture. Vasquez emphasizes that Ethiopian Yirgacheffe and Guatemalan Huehuetenango behave differently under identical profiles.
- Moisture content should sit between 10.5% and 11.2% at intake
- Screen size 18/64 is our standard for single-origin batches
- Water activity below 0.55 prevents mold without stalling Maillard reactions
- Bag markings must include harvest date, not just import date

Neglecting these baseline metrics turns a 12-minute roast into a 9-minute disaster.

## Roast Curve Management
The lecture focused on three phases: drying, Maillard, and development. Our Probat P25 operates with a drum speed of 52 RPM, and we charge at 385 °F for 85-pound batches. Development time ratio (DTR) targets 22% for filter roasts and 18% for espresso.

Key markers include first crack at approximately 401 °F, though altitude and barometric pressure shift this by up to 6 degrees. Vasquez demonstrated a 2023 Colombian Huila lot that peaked at 418 °F with a 1:48 development window, yielding 84.3 cupping points.

## Sensory Calibration
Roasters must taste, not just read thermocouples. The group cupped three roast levels of the same Kenyan AA.
- Light: bright citric acidity, tea-like body
- Medium: caramelized sugar, balanced stone fruit
- Dark: carbon bitterness dominates, origin character erased

The consensus selected the medium profile for our April wholesale release.

## Conclusion and Next Steps
Mastery requires repetition. Each apprentice will log 30 supervised roasts before solo authorization. Vasquez will review curves on April 8, 2024. Bring your notebooks, your skepticism, and a calibrated palate.
