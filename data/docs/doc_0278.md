# Aurora Robotics Team — Engineering Changelog

## Sprint 42: Actuator Calibration (March 4 – March 10)
We finalized torque mapping for the shoulder assembly. Marcus Chen led the update to the PID loops on Joint 3, reducing overshoot from 12% to 4%. The new calibration sheet is pinned in the workshop.

## Hardware Retrofit: Gripper v2.1
The aluminum fingers cracked during stress testing on March 8. We switched to carbon-fiber composite and added a rubber inlay.
- Re-machined palm plate to 3.2 mm tolerance
- Replaced servo model HS-7950TH with Savox SV-0235MG
- Added strain gauge at base for real-time load monitoring
- Tested lift capacity at 8.4 kg sustained

## Software Milestones
Elena Vasquez merged the navigation stack on March 9. Path planning now avoids dynamic obstacles within a 0.45 m radius. We also fixed the LiDAR dropout bug that stalled autonomous mode since February 22.

## Field Trials: Warehouse Run (March 11)
The robot completed 14 pick-and-place cycles in 22 minutes without human intervention. Battery draw averaged 18.2 A, well within the 25 A safety ceiling. Team Lead Dr. Amina Okonkwo signed off for the regional qualifier on April 3.

## Upcoming Focus
We need to validate the vision pipeline under fluorescent flicker and finalize emergency-stop latency testing before the qualifier.
