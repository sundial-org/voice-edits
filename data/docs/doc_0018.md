# Internal Solar Ops FAQ — Helios Ridge Installers

## Safety & PPE Requirements
Every crew member must wear ANSI-rated hard hats and Class 0 insulated gloves when working within 10 feet of live DC conductors. In March 2023, technician Mara Delgado sustained an arc-flash injury after skipping the glove check; that incident triggered our current zero-tolerance policy. Lockout-tagout tags must be signed by lead electrician James Okoro before any inverter servicing begins.

## Pre-Install Site Survey Checklist
Arrive with the SunPath Pro 7.2 app loaded and a 25-foot measuring tape. Confirm:
- Roof pitch is under 45 degrees or flagged for engineering review
- Main service panel has at least 125 amps of busbar capacity
- Shading analysis shows no more than 14% annual loss
If the property has a clay tile roof, call warehouse for the TileHook v4 brackets—do not substitute standard L-feet.

## Equipment & Inventory Updates
As of June 2024, we stock only Enphase IQ8HC microinverters for residential jobs; string inverters require VP approval. The warehouse on 440 Industrial Drive holds 380 panels in reserve. If a job needs more than 32 modules, submit Form 109B by Thursday at 14:00 to secure weekend delivery.

## Crew Scheduling & Weather Holds
Shifts start at 06:30. If forecasted winds exceed 35 mph or lightning is within 8 miles, site lead must call the delay line by 05:45. Overtime is capped at 14 hours per week per installer. All timecards are submitted through the Helios app by Sunday 22:00.

## Warranty & Hand-Off Protocol
Customer-facing documentation uses the 25-year production guarantee for panels and 10 years for workmanship. Before leaving, photograph the inverter serial number—format HX-2024-XXXX—and upload it with the final inspection report. Any callbacks within 90 days route to the original crew first.
