# Collective Lecture Notes — Hybrid Mixing & Modular Routing

## Session Overview and Objectives

These notes cover our March 14 workshop led by engineer Mara Okonkwo. We focused on integrating analog summing with in-the-box automation, specifically targeting the 32-track session format used in our studio. The goal was to reduce latency below 6 ms while preserving the harmonic saturation of the Neve 1073 preamps.

## Signal Path Architecture

Mara walked us through a revised routing matrix that splits the drum bus into four parallel stems before hitting the API 2500 compressor. Key takeaways included maintaining headroom at -18 dBFS and avoiding digital clipping on the 2-buss.

- Drum parallel: routed through Chandler TG2
- Synth submix: sent to Strymon BigSky via aux 4
- Vocal chain: UAD 1176 into EMI REDD console
- Master return: captured at 96 kHz / 24-bit

## Calibration and Monitoring

We calibrated the Genelec 8351 monitors using GLM 4 software, setting the listening position at 1.2 meters from the sweet spot. The room correction curve revealed a 120 Hz buildup that we tamed with a narrow -3 dB cut rather than broad shelving.
