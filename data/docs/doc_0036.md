# Lecture Notes: Scaling Access Through Open Curriculum

## Course Context and Mission
These notes accompany the Fall 2024 seminar series hosted by BrightPath Learning, an edtech nonprofit founded in 2018 to close the digital divide in secondary education. Our cohort this semester includes 42 educators from 14 districts. The goal is not merely to distribute software licenses, but to build sustainable pedagogical frameworks that outlast any single funding cycle. Dr. Aisha Patel, our research director, emphasized during the August 14 orientation that equity must be designed into the architecture of every lesson plan, not added as an afterthought.

## Core Principles of Open Design
Open curriculum does not mean unstructured. It requires rigorous scaffolding paired with adaptable assets. Key tenets include:
- Modular lesson units that teachers can reorder without losing coherence
- Multilingual glossaries embedded at the point of need rather than appended
- Assessment rubrics that privilege growth over normative ranking
- Local context windows that invite student-generated primary sources
- Accessibility checks completed before publication, not during pilot testing

## Case Study: The Meridian Pilot
In March 2023, Meridian High School in Detroit adopted our open mathematics pathway. Within one academic year, course-completion rates rose from 61% to 79%. The turnaround was not driven by hardware alone—each classroom received only four refurbished devices—but by restructuring peer tutoring schedules and aligning assessments to state standards released in June 2022. Principal Robert Chen noted that teacher autonomy increased when centralized mandates decreased.

## Implementation Roadmap
For nonprofits considering replication, we recommend a phased rollout. Phase one involves stakeholder mapping and a 90-day listening tour. Phase two focuses on curriculum adaptation with local advisory boards. Phase three launches with a 20-student pilot before any district-wide commitment. Funding should be diversified; relying solely on a single grant from the Department of Education in 2021 nearly stalled our expansion.

## Closing Reflections
Technology amplifies existing practice. If the underlying pedagogy is weak, digital tools will only scale that weakness faster. Our mandate is to remain lean—operating with a team of 11 full-time staff—while supporting over 3,200 learners annually.
