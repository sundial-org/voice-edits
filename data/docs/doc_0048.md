# Meridian Roastworks: Scaling Sustainable Small-Batch Coffee for the Riverside Corridor

## Executive Summary
Meridian Roastworks requests $85,000 from the Green Bean Innovation Fund to expand our solar-powered roasting facility at 442 Millbrook Lane. Founded by Elena Vasquez in 2019, we currently supply 14 regional cafés with single-origin beans sourced directly from the Antioquia Cooperative in Colombia. This grant will fund a second Loring S15 Falcon roaster, upgrade our packaging line, and establish a compost-return program with partner farms by March 2026.

## Project Background and Need
The Riverside Corridor has seen a 34% increase in independent coffee shops since 2022, yet local roasteries remain reliant on out-of-state distributors. Meridian fills this gap by roasting in 28-pound batches within eight hours of order, preserving volatile aromatics that industrial operations lose. Our direct-trade model returns 18% above market price to growers. Without expanded capacity, we will turn away an estimated $120,000 in wholesale contracts next quarter.

## Implementation Plan
Over twelve months, we will execute three phases:
- Q1 2026: Install the Loring S15 Falcon and complete electrical upgrades certified for 480V solar integration.
- Q2 2026: Launch the Compost-Back Loop, delivering 600 pounds of spent chaff monthly to Antioquia partner plots.
- Q3 2026: Certify the expanded line under the Specialty Coffee Association’s Golden Cup standard and hire two apprentice roasters from the local apprenticeship program.

## Budget and Impact Metrics
The $85,000 award breaks down as follows: $52,000 for equipment, $18,000 for facility retrofitting, $9,000 for compost logistics, and $6,000 for staff training. We project roasting 4,200 pounds of coffee per month by December 2026, reducing per-pound carbon footprint by 22%. All financial reports will be audited quarterly by the firm Whitmore & Associates.

## Sustainability and Community Engagement
Meridian operates on 100% renewable electricity through our 24-kilowatt rooftop array. We host free cupping workshops every third Saturday, serving over 200 residents annually. Our proposed expansion includes a public tasting room designed in partnership with Riverside Community Architects, ensuring the facility remains an accessible educational hub rather than an isolated production site.

## Conclusion
With support from the Green Bean Innovation Fund, Meridian Roastworks will strengthen regional supply chains, deepen ethical sourcing commitments, and create three skilled jobs. We invite reviewers to visit our Millbrook Lane facility or contact Elena Vasquez directly at elena@meridianroastworks.org to discuss this proposal further.
