# Project Chimera — 2024 Robotics Team README

## Overview
Project Chimera is the 2024 competitive entry for Team Helios, developed for the International Robotics Challenge in Boston. Our autonomous ground vehicle, designated HLV-09, integrates custom vision pipelines with a reinforced carbon-fiber chassis designed to survive the 3.2-kilometer endurance course.

## Team & Timeline
Lead engineer Dr. Amara Okonkwo assembled the core group in January 2024. Mechanical lead Marcus Chen finalized the drivetrain by March 14, 2024, while software integration concluded on August 22, 2024. The team currently operates out of Lab 7B with a budget capped at $42,000.

## Hardware Specifications
- 4-wheel independent suspension with 280 mm ground clearance
- NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano running at 40 TOPS for real-time inference
- 48 V lithium-polymer battery pack delivering 6 hours of continuous operation
- Custom LiDAR mount rated for 150 kg of shock load
- Dual 1080p global-shutter cameras synchronized at 60 fps

## Software Architecture
The navigation stack runs ROS 2 Humble on Ubuntu 22.04. Perception uses a YOLOv8-seg model trained on 14,000 annotated frames collected at the testing field near Lake Meridian. Path planning relies on a hybrid A* algorithm optimized for uneven terrain, with obstacle avoidance updated at 50 Hz.

## Competition Goals
We aim to complete the endurance course in under 18 minutes with zero manual interventions. Secondary objectives include mapping 90% of the hidden sector and logging environmental data for the post-event analysis report due September 30, 2024.
