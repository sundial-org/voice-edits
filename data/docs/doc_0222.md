# Solar Installer Field Lecture Notes

## Safety and Personal Protective Equipment
Before touching any module, remember the hierarchy of controls established in our March 14, 2025 onboarding update. Every installer must wear Class 0 rubber insulating gloves rated for 1,000 V when working within reach of energized DC conductors. Arc-flash face shields are mandatory once system voltage exceeds 600 V DC, which applies to every commercial string we are building this quarter. Maria Chen, our lead safety officer, reported that 80% of near-misses last year involved skipped lockout-tagout steps. Verify zero energy with a calibrated multimeter—never assume a breaker label tells the full story.

## Module Handling and Array Layout
We are deploying REC Alpha Pure-R 430 W modules on a flush-mount rail system. Each rail splice requires a minimum 6-inch overlap and two stainless-steel clamps per joint. Layout begins at the southwest ridge and moves northeast to maintain consistent string lengths of 14 modules per circuit.

Key layout checks include:
- Confirm roof penetrations align with structural rafters marked on the 2024 permit set
- Maintain 3-inch clearance between module frames and rail ends
- Torque all mid-clamps to 18 N·m using a calibrated driver
- Label each string with weatherproof tags before leaving the roof

Remember that shading from the HVAC curb will reduce the southwest string by roughly 4% in winter; account for that in your string sizing worksheet.

## DC Wiring and Combiner Boxes
Run 10 AWG PV wire in continuous conduit from the array to Combiner Box A, located on the north parapet wall. Keep DC and AC raceways separated by at least 1 inch, per NEC Article 690.31. Each combiner accepts four strings fused at 15 A; do not mix 430 W and older 370 W modules on the same fuse.

Grounding electrode conductors must be bare copper, sized per Table 250.66, and bonded to the rail system with listed lay-in lugs. Double-check that rapid shutdown devices are within 1 foot of the array boundary, as required by the 2023 NEC update adopted in this jurisdiction on January 1, 2024.

## Commissioning and Handoff
Once wiring is complete, perform an insulation resistance test at 1,000 V DC on each string. Acceptable readings are above 40 MΩ; anything lower indicates moisture intrusion or pinched jacket. After energizing, log open-circuit voltage and operating current into the project portal within 24 hours.

Your final punch list should include photo documentation of every roof penetration, torque verification stamps, and signed PPE compliance cards. These notes cover the core sequence—execute slowly, check twice, and ask the foreman if a detail conflicts with your plan.
