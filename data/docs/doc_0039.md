# Meridian Coffee Roasters — Changelog

## 2024 Harvest: Ethiopian Yirgacheffe Release
On March 14, we released our single-origin Ethiopian Yirgacheffe, lot #4471, roasted in 15-kilogram batches. This washed-process coffee replaced the outgoing Guatemala Huehuetenango, which retired after 18 months in rotation. Cupping notes highlight bergamot and black tea; the profile is lighter than our usual house blend.

## Facility Upgrade: Loring S70 Installation
We installed a Loring S70 in the main roast lab on April 2. The upgrade reduces gas consumption by 22 percent compared to our 2019 Probat, and it allows us to hit development times within 0.4 seconds of target. All espresso blends now pass through the new machine.

## Packaging and Shipping Adjustments
Starting May 20, every 12-ounce bag ships with a one-way degassing valve and a compostable inner liner. We also added a 5-pound bulk option for wholesale accounts. Tracking updates now arrive via SMS for orders placed after 9:00 a.m. Eastern.

- Updated roast profiles for Sumatra Mandheling and Colombia Huila
- Introduced a 3-pack sampler with 8-ounce tins
- Extended wholesale ordering to 48-hour turnaround by June 1
- Added nitrogen-flushed storage for green inventory exceeding 500 pounds

## Team and Training
Head roaster Elena Vasquez completed the Q Grader calibration course in Seattle on July 8. Two apprentice roasters joined the program in August, bringing the production team to seven people total.

## Looking Ahead: Winter Preview
We plan to debut a dark roast named Obsidian on November 11, followed by a holiday blend roasted to 428 °F drop temperature. Warehouse capacity will increase by 800 square feet before December 15.
