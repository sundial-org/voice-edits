# Product Requirements Document: Epic Integration Module v2.4 — Mercy General Hospital IT

## Overview
Mercy General Hospital IT is deploying Epic Integration Module v2.4 to unify clinical data exchange across cardiology, radiology, and emergency departments. The initiative is sponsored by Chief Medical Informatics Officer Dr. Elena Vasquez and targets full production release by August 15 2025. The system must support 450 workstations and maintain 99.99% uptime during the 90-day stabilization window.

## Scope & Requirements
The module will replace legacy HL7 v2 interfaces with FHIR R4 APIs, enabling real-time patient record synchronization between Epic Hyperspace and the hospital's internal data lake. Core deliverables include:

- Bi-directional FHIR R4 endpoints for admission, discharge, and transfer (ADT) messages with sub-200ms latency
- Role-based access controls aligned with HIPAA Security Rule §164.312, audited quarterly by the compliance office
- Automated failover to the secondary data center in Phoenix within 30 seconds of primary node degradation
- Integration testing against Epic's 2024 certification sandbox prior to user acceptance testing

Non-functional requirements mandate AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. The solution must scale horizontally to accommodate peak census loads exceeding 1,200 concurrent sessions.

## Timeline & Milestones
Development begins June 2 2025 and proceeds through three sprints. Sprint 1 (June 2 – June 27) covers API architecture and security baseline. Sprint 2 (June 30 – July 25) delivers integration builds and performance tuning. Sprint 3 (July 28 – August 15) executes UAT, staff training, and go-live. A soft launch is scheduled for August 8 2025 in the emergency department, followed by hospital-wide activation on August 15 2025.

Dr. Elena Vasquez will chair the clinical validation board, meeting every Thursday at 10:00 AM in Conference Room B. IT Project Manager Marcus Chen holds daily stand-ups at 8:30 AM to track blockers against the critical path.

## Risk & Compliance
Primary risks include vendor delay in Epic certification, data migration corruption during the legacy transition, and staff resistance to workflow changes. Mitigation strategies include maintaining parallel HL7 feeds for 60 days post-go-live, running automated checksum validation on all migrated records, and deploying change champions in each clinical unit.

Compliance obligations extend beyond HIPAA to include Joint Commission information management standards and state-level breach notification laws. All audit logs must be retained for six years and stored in WORM-compliant object storage.

## Success Metrics
Post-deployment, the module will be evaluated against quantifiable targets measured at 30, 60, and 90 days. Key performance indicators include interface error rates below 0.1%, average query response times under 150ms, and user satisfaction scores above 4.2 out of 5.0 on the standardized IT adoption survey distributed by Mercy General's Quality Improvement office.

If these metrics are not achieved by November 13 2025, the steering committee will trigger a remediation plan including vendor escalation and potential architecture revision.
