# Research Notes — Echo Bloom Studio

## Project Context
We are three weeks into pre-production for *Velvet Hollow*, a narrative puzzle-platformer scheduled for vertical slice delivery on March 14, 2026. After the team retreat in Portland on October 2, our art director Mara Chen proposed abandoning the hand-painted pipeline in favor of a hybrid shader workflow. This document captures the technical and creative findings from October 9 through October 23.

## Art Pipeline Experiments
Mara's shader tests on the test environment "Moss Quarter" show a 34% reduction in texture memory compared to the July build. The trade-off is a slight loss of brush-stroke granularity at 4K resolution. We need to decide by November 5 whether to commit studio-wide.

Key findings:
- Hybrid shader reduces draw calls by roughly 18 per frame in dense foliage scenes
- Hand-painted assets still outperform in close-up dialogue portraits
- Tooling requires an update to Blender 4.2 LTS plugins before December
- Junior environment artist Leo Park completed three benchmark scenes using both methods

## Narrative Design Updates
Writer Juno Reeves delivered the revised Act II outline on October 14. The new structure introduces the character Silas Vane two chapters earlier, which solves the pacing gap identified in the August playtest. Feedback from the seven external testers showed a 22% increase in emotional engagement scores after the change.

## Technical Constraints
Our target hardware remains the Steam Deck and mid-tier laptops. Frame-time budgets are tight: 8.3 ms for gameplay, 11 ms for cinematic transitions. The shader pipeline must not exceed 2.1 ms GPU time on the Deck's APU. We have reserved January for optimization, but base architecture needs to be locked by January 15.
