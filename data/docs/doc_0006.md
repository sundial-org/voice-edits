# OpenLearn Foundation — Product & Program Changelog

## Q3 Platform Release (August 2024)
We are thrilled to ship version 3.2 of the OpenLearn Platform, our largest stability-focused update since the January 2023 redesign. Led by engineering director Aisha Patel, the release addresses 147 reported accessibility issues and reduces average page load time by 34 percent for users on 2G connections. The nonprofit board approved the $210,000 infrastructure budget in June, allowing us to migrate core services to energy-efficient data centers in Nairobi and São Paulo.

Key improvements include:
- Full WCAG 2.2 AA compliance across all 42 course templates
- Offline-first lesson caching for the mobile app (Android and iOS)
- Real-time collaborative whiteboards for live tutoring sessions
- Automated transcript generation in Swahili, Portuguese, and Hindi
- A redesigned dashboard for educators tracking cohort progress

## New Partnerships and Curriculum Expansion
In July, OpenLearn signed a three-year memorandum of understanding with the Ministry of Education in Rwanda to integrate digital literacy modules into 180 rural secondary schools by March 2025. Program officer Marcus Chen negotiated the agreement, which includes training for 600 teachers and the donation of 4,500 refurbished tablets through our hardware recycling partners. Separately, we launched the "Data for Good" fellowship, selecting 22 graduate students from universities in Ghana, Vietnam, and Colombia to build open-source assessment tools.

## Community and Funding Updates
Our annual fundraising gala on September 14 exceeded its $500,000 target, reaching $612,400 in commitments from 340 individual donors and 18 institutional sponsors. These funds will directly support the expansion of our free tutoring network, which served 14,200 learners in the past quarter alone. We also welcomed Dr. Elena Vasquez to the advisory board; her research on equitable AI in classrooms will guide our 2025 product roadmap.

## Looking Ahead to Q4
As we move into October, priorities include stress-testing the new tutoring queue system ahead of exams in November, publishing our first open-data report on learner outcomes, and preparing grant applications for the Global EdTech Equity Fund. We remain committed to transparency, accessibility, and building technology that serves every student—regardless of geography or income.
