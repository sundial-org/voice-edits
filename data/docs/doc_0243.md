# Roastery R&D Log — Q3 Cupping Trials

## Batch Overview
Trials run 14 Aug through 22 Aug 2024 at the Main St. facility. Lead cupper: Elena Vargas. We evaluated four incoming lots, focusing on solubility adjustments and first-crack consistency across the Diedrich IR-12. Target moisture for green storage stayed at 10.5%.

## Ethiopian Yirgacheffe — Gedeb
Washed lot, Grade 1, 1,840 kg received 02 Aug. Profile: 8:45 total roast, drop at 204 °C. Cupping 18 Aug showed jasmine, bergamot, and a clean tea-like body. We pushed development ratio to 22% to highlight acidity without flattening the finish.

- Grind settings tested: 7.2, 8.1, 9.4
- Brew ratios: 1:15.5, 1:16, 1:16.75
- TDS readings: 1.38%, 1.42%, 1.35%

The 1:16 brew at 8.1 grind yielded the best clarity. Recommended for single-origin drip release on 05 Sept.

## Guatemalan Huehuetenango — Finca El Mirador
Bourbon and Caturra blend, harvested Mar 2024. Roast profile aimed for 11:15 with first crack at 6:40. Cupping notes: milk chocolate, red apple, and a soft caramel finish. The lot responded well to a slightly faster rate of rise after first crack, reducing baked notes seen in earlier runs.

We will bag 60% for retail and reserve 40% for the seasonal espresso blend. Bag codes start with HUE-2403.

## Colombian Huila — Pink Bourbon
Micro-lot of 420 kg from producer Carlos Mendizábal, arrived 10 Aug. This was the most variable lot—moisture ranged 10.2% to 11.1%. We separated into two roast tiers: a lighter 9:00 profile and a medium 10:30 profile.

The lighter roast preserved the guava and rose notes but lacked structure. The medium tier at 10:30, dropped at 208 °C, delivered the best balance. We will roast 180 kg next week for a limited release.

## Next Steps
