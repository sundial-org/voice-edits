# Solar Installer Onboarding Guide

Welcome to Helios Energy Solutions. This guide covers your first 90 days as a field installer, outlining safety protocols, installation standards, and reporting expectations for residential and commercial arrays.

## Pre-Deployment Requirements

Before joining a crew, complete OSHA 30 certification and the Helios internal fall-protection course led by Marcus Chen. You must also pass a background check and provide proof of a valid driver's license by March 15. All new hires receive a branded hard hat, high-visibility vest, and insulated tool bag on day one.

## Safety and Site Protocol

Every morning begins with a 15-minute toolbox talk. Review the day's roof pitch, weather conditions, and electrical isolation points. Never bypass lockout-tagout procedures. In 2023, our zero-incident target was achieved across 142 installations; maintaining that record depends on strict adherence to these rules.

## Installation Workflow

A standard 8 kW residential system takes two working days. Your responsibilities include:
- Mounting racking with 5-degree tilt adjustments for optimal exposure
- Running 10 AWG PV wire through weather-rated conduit
- Testing open-circuit voltage with a calibrated multimeter
- Sealing all roof penetrations with flashing rated for 25 years

## Quality Assurance and Handoff

After commissioning, photograph each array section and upload files to the project portal within 24 hours. The lead technician will verify torque settings on all 3/8-inch bolts and confirm inverter pairing codes. Customer walkthroughs are scheduled by Sarah Delgado, who coordinates final utility inspections.

## Career Progression

Within six months, installers can pursue NABCEP certification and advance to crew lead. We encourage feedback through monthly reviews with site manager James Ortiz. Your growth here is measured not only in panels installed, but in the reliability you bring to every rooftop.
