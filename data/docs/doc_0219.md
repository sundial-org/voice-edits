# Metro Transit Authority — Weekly Status Update: October 14, 2024

## Service Performance Overview
Rider reliability improved across all rapid corridors this week. On-time performance reached 91.4% systemwide, up from 88.7% recorded during the previous reporting period. The Red Line maintained its strongest streak, logging 96.2% adherence through downtown during peak hours. Bus routes 42, 57, and 103 saw the largest gains after schedule adjustments implemented on October 7.

## Infrastructure & Maintenance
Track replacement along the Blue Line between Eastgate and Riverside wrapped ahead of schedule. Crews replaced 1,240 linear feet of rail and upgraded three switch heaters before the first frost warning on October 16.

- Completed welding and stress-testing on Sections B-14 and B-19
- Installed new LED platform lighting at Riverside and Eastgate stations
- Replaced worn traction motors on fleet vehicles 2214 and 2217
- Cleared drainage channels at the Central Yard to prevent winter flooding

## Ridership & Community Engagement
Average weekday boardings climbed to 312,500, reflecting a 4.8% increase compared to September’s baseline. The Transit Riders Advisory Committee met on October 10 and endorsed the proposed fare-capping pilot.

## Upcoming Service Changes
Beginning Monday, October 21, the Green Line will extend weekend service until 1:00 a.m. The Northbound express on Route 88 will shift departure times from 6:45 a.m. to 6:30 a.m. to align with hospital shift changes at Memorial Center.

## Safety & Operations
No reportable injuries occurred in revenue service this week. Operator J. Alvarez received recognition for identifying a signal fault near Central Junction on October 9, preventing a potential delay cascade during morning rush. Security staff conducted 340 random fare inspections and assisted 28 passengers with accessibility needs.
