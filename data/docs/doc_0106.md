# BioNova Therapeutics — New Lab Member Onboarding Guide

Welcome to BioNova Therapeutics. This guide outlines the essential protocols, timelines, and cultural expectations for all researchers joining our Cambridge facility.

## Facility Access and Security

Your access badge activates on Monday, 14 October 2024. Report to Building 7, Suite 400, by 08:30 for orientation with Dr. Elena Vasquez, our Laboratory Safety Officer. You must complete the online biosafety module before receiving keycard credentials for BSL-2 zones.

## Training Schedule

Onboarding spans ten business days. During this window you will shadow a senior scientist, attend the weekly lab meeting every Wednesday at 10:00, and complete instrument certification. The sequence is designed to minimize disruption while ensuring competency.

Key milestones include:
- Day 1: Safety walkthrough and PPE fitting
- Day 3: Introduction to the LIMS database and sample tracking
- Day 7: Independent operation of the Thermo Fisher QuantStudio 5 under supervision
- Day 10: Final competency sign-off with your principal investigator

## Core Laboratory Protocols

All experiments using primary human cell lines require documented approval. Maintain a bound laboratory notebook with consecutive page numbering; electronic backups must sync to our secure server every 24 hours. If you observe contamination in incubator B-12, quarantine the unit immediately and notify facilities.

Required daily practices:
- Verify CO2 levels in all tissue-culture incubators before 09:00
- Log reagent lot numbers for every PCR master mix prepared
- Wear nitrile gloves and eye protection when handling acetonitrile or DMSO
- Dispose of sharps in the red biohazard containers, never in standard waste

## Equipment and Resource Allocation

Book shared instruments through the Calendly portal at least 48 hours in advance. The Agilent 2100 Bioanalyzer and the Beckman Coulter Avanti J-26 are reserved for trained users only. Consumable budgets are tracked per project code; discuss ordering with Marcus Chen, our Operations Manager, if supplies fall below two weeks of stock.

## Culture and Communication

We value rigorous data, transparent failure reporting, and cross-functional collaboration. Weekly journal clubs occur in Conference Room B every Friday at 15:30. Attend your first session within thirty days of joining. Questions about authorship or data ownership should be directed to Dr. Vasquez before initiating any external collaboration.

We are glad you are here. Follow these guidelines carefully, ask questions early, and contribute to the high standard of science at BioNova.
