# Team Helios Robotics — Q4 Changelog

## Hardware Updates
We finalized the drivetrain overhaul ahead of the winter scrimmage. Marcus Chen led the swap from the legacy 6-inch pneumatic tires to 8-inch Mecanum wheels, reducing turning scrub by an estimated 18 percent. The new aluminum chassis, fabricated by Dr. Elena Vasquez on October 14, 2024, weighs 12.4 kg—down from 14.1 kg—while retaining full torsional rigidity through the central spine. We also replaced the old 775 Pro motors with Falcon 500s running at a capped 340 RPM on the arm joint to prevent gearbox stripping under load.

- Installed dual 4-inch compliant wheels for intake stability
- Re-routed the CAN bus harness away from the elevator chain path
- Added a 3D-printed PETG guard around the LiDAR mount
- Recalibrated the IMU after the chassis swap

## Software Release v2.1
The autonomy stack received its largest update since Regionals. Path planning now uses a hybrid A* and pure-pursuit controller that reduced average cycle time from 42 ms to 19 ms. Vision targets are processed via a custom AprilTag pipeline running on the Jetson Orin at 30 FPS. Marcus Chen merged the feature branch on Tuesday after resolving a race condition in the swerve odometry thread.

## Competition Prep
With the November 2 qualifier approaching, we ran twelve full-field practice matches this week. The drive team—Sarah, Dev, and Juno—logged 6.5 hours of stick time and refined the endgame climb sequence to a consistent 14-second cycle. We also printed three backup intake flaps in TPU 95A after the primary set showed stress whitening at the hinge.

## Safety & Compliance
Dr. Elena Vasquez audited our lockout-tagout procedure and updated the lithium-polymer charging station signage. All batteries are now stored in individual fire-retardant pouches, and the team completed the updated OSHA robotics module by October 10, 2024. No incidents reported during the high-voltage testing session on Friday.
