# Changelog — Project Aegis Robotics Team

## Firmware v2.4.1 Deployment — March 14
Lead engineer Dr. Elena Vasquez finalized the v2.4.1 firmware roll-out across all six drive units. The update resolves the 42 Hz oscillation detected during the February 28 stress tests and reduces latency from 18 ms to 9 ms. Every unit passed the 150-cycle endurance benchmark before clearance.

## Mechanical Revisions — March 16
The chassis team replaced the aluminum shoulder brackets with titanium alloy equivalents rated for 340 N·m. Torque sensors on Joint 3 and Joint 7 were recalibrated using the Mark IV protocol. We logged zero structural failures across 72 continuous hours of load testing.

## Sensor Integration Milestones
The vision pipeline now processes 120 frames per second through the new LIDAR fusion module. Key updates include:
- Replacement of the legacy ToF sensor with the Velodyne VLP-32C
- Integration of thermal imaging for low-visibility navigation
- Calibration of IMU drift to within 0.03 degrees per minute
- Validation of obstacle detection at ranges up to 45 meters

## Control System Updates — March 20
Marcus Chen rewrote the inverse kinematics solver in C++17, cutting computation time by 34%. The autonomous path planner now supports dynamic replanning every 50 milliseconds, allowing Aegis-7 to navigate the warehouse maze without human intervention.

## Upcoming Sprint — March 25
We are targeting full system integration by April 3. Priorities include sealing the IP67-rated enclosure and running the final safety audit with Dr. Vasquez and the certification board. All members must submit calibration logs by March 29.
