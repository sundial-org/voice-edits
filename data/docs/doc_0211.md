# Technical Design: Meridian Grid-Scale Thermal Storage

## Context and Goals
Meridian Climate Systems is building long-duration thermal storage for industrial decarbonization. The objective is to deliver 48-hour continuous heat at 550 °C using a modular ceramic brick architecture. By Q3 2026, we must validate a 10 MWh pilot with our anchor partner, Riverside Cement, reducing their natural gas consumption by 35 percent.

## System Architecture
The storage train uses electrically resistive heating elements embedded in high-alumina refractory bricks. A closed-loop argon circuit transfers thermal energy to the industrial steam generator during discharge phases.

- Ceramic modules rated for 600 °C with 92% round-trip efficiency
- Argon heat-transfer loop with stainless-steel 316L piping
- 10 MWh pilot capacity scalable to 100 MWh per site
- Integrated predictive controller (Meridian OS v2.1)

## Performance Targets
We will demonstrate sustained output at 8.5 MW thermal for 48 hours. Insulation targets specify heat loss below 0.4% per 24 hours. The pilot must survive 5,000 charge-discharge cycles without brick degradation exceeding 2% volumetric change.

## Risk Mitigation
Material supply constraints on alumina feedstock require a dual-source strategy from Imerys and Alcoa by January 2025. Failure modes include thermal shock cracking during rapid discharge; we will mitigate with staged valve sequencing and real-time fiber-optic temperature mapping at 2-second resolution.

## Milestones
Pilot commissioning is targeted for 15 August 2026. Engineering validation begins in March 2025, followed by factory acceptance testing in October 2025. Full commercial rollout to five European sites is projected for 2028, contingent on achieving the 92% efficiency threshold.
