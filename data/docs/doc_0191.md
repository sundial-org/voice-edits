# Grant Proposal: Autonomous Navigation Upgrade for Team Helix Robotics

## Executive Summary
Team Helix Robotics, founded in 2019 at Lincoln High School, requests $18,500 to modernize our autonomous navigation stack ahead of the 2025 FIRST Robotics Competition regional in Detroit on March 14. Our current vision pipeline runs on 2018-era Jetson TX2 modules that fail in low-light warehouse conditions. Without this upgrade, our 2024 season win rate of 64% will likely drop below competitive thresholds.

## Team Background and Track Record
Under mentor Dr. Elena Vasquez, our 22-member student team has placed in the top eight at three consecutive state qualifiers. In 2023 we secured second place at the Midwest Invitational, demonstrating robust mechanical design but exposing latency issues in LiDAR fusion. We operate out of the Lincoln Makerspace, maintaining a $4,200 annual parts budget funded by local sponsors.

## Proposed Technical Improvements
Funding will be allocated to three targeted upgrades:
- Replacement of four Jetson TX2 compute nodes with AGX Orin modules
- Integration of a Velodyne VLP-16 LiDAR unit with real-time point-cloud filtering
- Development of a custom ROS 2 navigation layer trained on 12,000 labeled warehouse frames

These changes should reduce path-planning latency from 340 ms to under 90 ms.

## Budget and Timeline
We seek $18,500, broken into $11,200 for compute and sensors, $4,300 for machining and mounting hardware, and $3,000 for travel and registration to the March 14 Detroit regional. Procurement begins January 6, 2025; integration testing runs through February 20; final calibration completes by March 1.

## Conclusion
This investment directly addresses the bottleneck that limited Team Helix in 2024. With modernized perception and a disciplined build schedule, we can return to championship contention and expand our outreach program to two additional middle schools by June 2025. We welcome the opportunity to discuss this proposal in detail.
