# Resonance Collective: Grant Proposal for Community Studio Expansion

## Executive Summary
Resonance Collective, founded by producer Marcus Okonkwo in 2019, is applying for $48,000 to expand our East London production hub. Over the past four years we have facilitated 312 recording sessions for underrepresented artists. This grant will fund analog hardware, accessibility renovations, and a six-month residency beginning March 2024.

## Project Description
We propose the "Analog Futures" initiative: a collaborative production cycle pairing emerging beatmakers with veteran engineers. Participants will record on a restored 1976 Trident TSM console, recently donated by the estate of engineer Lena Brooks. The program runs from March 15 to September 15, 2024, and targets twelve artists aged 18–25 who lack studio access.

Key activities include:
- Weekly four-hour open studio blocks every Tuesday and Thursday
- Monthly mastering workshops led by Grammy-nominated mixer Aisha Rahman
- A public listening session on August 22, 2024 at the Hackney Library
- Digital archiving of all works under Creative Commons licenses

## Budget and Impact
The $48,000 request breaks down as follows: $22,000 for acoustic treatment and wheelchair-accessible consoles; $14,000 for three resident producer stipends; $8,000 for tape stock and maintenance; and $4,000 for outreach. We project direct engagement with 45 artists and indirect reach to over 800 community members through broadcasts and zines.

## Conclusion
Resonance Collective believes sound infrastructure is cultural infrastructure. With support from the Arts Innovation Fund, we can preserve analog craft while democratizing access. We invite the committee to join us in building a studio that belongs to everyone.
