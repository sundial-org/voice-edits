# Collective Draft: Mid-Spring Sessions & Upcoming Cuts

The studio doors stay open later now. Over the past six weeks our core group—Mira, Jonah, and engineer Tariq—locked in a new workflow that finally matches the chaos we’ve been chasing since February.

## What Changed in March

We stopped treating the console like a museum piece. Jonah brought in a 1978 Trident board from a closing space in Brighton, and Tariq rewired the patch bay in three days flat. By March 14 we had signal flow mapped for every live room. The result? Less troubleshooting, more tracking.

- Live bass through the Trident preamp
- Drum bus running into Tariq’s custom 1176 clone
- Vocal chain: SM7B into an Empirical Labs Distressor
- Reference monitors calibrated to 83 dB SPL
- Backup drives rotating every 48 hours

## The Numbers So Far

We’ve cut 14 full takes and kept 9. Mira’s vocal on “Copper Wire” went from scratch idea to final comp in two afternoons. Jonah’s synth layers on the same track use patches he sketched back in November 2022—proof that old folders still pay rent. We’re tracking at 96 kHz/24-bit, which eats drive space but keeps transients honest.

## Next Month: Release Plan for May 3

The collective will drop a four-track EP on Bandcamp and limited cassette. Pre-orders open April 18. Mira is handling artwork; Jonah is mixing; Tariq is cutting lacquers by hand. We’re pressing 150 copies—no reprints once they’re gone. If you’re in the city, join us May 3 at The Cellar for the listening session at 8 p.m.

We’re not a label. We’re just five people who trust a loud snare and a quiet room. See you in the mix.
