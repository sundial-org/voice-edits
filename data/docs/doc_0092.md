# Lecture Notes: Scaling LedgerFlow — From MVP to Regulated Fintech

## Product Genesis
LedgerFlow began in March 2021 as a weekend project by co-founders Aisha Patel and Marcus Chen. Their initial thesis was simple: small merchants lost an average of $4,200 annually to fragmented invoicing tools. By September 2022, the team had released v1.0 with automated reconciliation and real-time settlement rails.

## Core Architecture
The backend runs on a modular Kubernetes cluster across three AWS regions. Key design choices include:
- Event sourcing for every transaction log, ensuring immutable audit trails required by SOC 2 Type II
- A rules engine written in Rust that processes 12,000 transactions per second with sub-50ms latency
- End-to-end encryption using AES-256-GCM, with key rotation every 90 days

## Regulatory Milestones
Operating in financial services demands rigorous compliance. In January 2023, LedgerFlow secured its Money Services Business license in 14 U.S. states. By August 2024, the platform completed its PCI DSS Level 1 certification, allowing direct card-network tokenization without intermediary gateways.

## User Growth Metrics
Since the public launch in March 2021, adoption has accelerated:
- 340,000 active merchant accounts as of Q2 2025
- $2.8 billion in gross settlement volume processed in the last fiscal year
- Net revenue retention of 118%, driven by premium analytics modules

## Risk & Fraud Systems
Real-time risk scoring remains critical. The model ingests 180 behavioral signals per session, combining device fingerprinting with graph-based entity resolution. In June 2024, this system blocked a coordinated $1.2 million synthetic-identity attack within 14 minutes of initiation.

## Roadmap for Q3 2025
Engineering priorities include embedded lending APIs and multi-currency settlement across the Eurozone. The team plans to hire 25 additional compliance engineers before the October 2025 audit cycle, ensuring readiness for the proposed EU Digital Finance Package.
