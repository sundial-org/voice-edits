# Roastery Lecture Notes: From Green to Cup

## The Origin Story
When Marco Delgado founded Verdant Roast in 2014, he insisted on direct trade with the Kintamani highlands of Bali. That single decision reshaped our supply chain. Today we process roughly 12,000 pounds of green coffee each quarter, maintaining lot separation from harvest to bag.

## Roast Profiles and Chemistry
Maillard reactions begin in earnest around 302°F, but we stop our Ethiopian Yirgacheffe at 398°F to preserve bergamot acidity. Key development markers include:
- First crack at 385°F (8 minutes, 15 seconds)
- Color change to cinnamon by 405°F
- Degassing peak at 72 hours post-roast
- Target moisture loss of 14 percent

We never blend before roasting; each origin receives its own 35-minute profile.

## Cupping Protocol
Every Tuesday at 9:00 AM, the quality team cups the previous day's production using SCA standards. We score fragrance, flavor, aftertaste, acidity, body, and balance on 6-to-10 scales. Last month, our Guatemala Huehuetenango scored 87.25, our highest seasonal result.

## Storage and Shelf Life
Green coffee remains stable for 18 months in climate-controlled warehouses at 60 percent humidity. Once roasted, aromatics degrade rapidly. We recommend consumption within 21 days, though sealed nitrogen-flushed bags extend viability to 45 days without noticeable staleness.

## Conclusion
Consistency requires documentation. By tracking batch IDs, roast dates—like our landmark March 3, 2023 pilot run—and sensory scores, we build reproducible excellence in every bag.
