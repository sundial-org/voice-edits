# Internal Lecture Notes: Shipping Small, Thinking Big

## The Philosophy Behind Our Scale

Maren established this studio in 2019 with a simple rule: never let ambition outpace the team. We are eight people, not eighty. That constraint is not a deficit; it is a design parameter. When we built *Elden Crest*, we targeted a 12-week vertical slice rather than a two-year roadmap. The result was a prototype that taught us more about player retention than any Gantt chart ever could. Small teams move faster, fail cheaper, and recover with identity intact.

## What the Data Actually Says

We often romanticize intuition, but our March 2024 playtest told a different story. Out of 340 anonymous sessions, 47% of players abandoned the build during the second biome transition. Not because of difficulty, but because of ambiguity. The environment art was beautiful, yet the lighting failed to guide the eye. Numbers do not hurt feelings; they direct craft. We redesigned the landmark system in four days.

## Practical Constraints We Chose

Every project needs boundaries that feel like creative rules rather than budgetary punishments. For our current title, we agreed on the following non-negotiables:

- A single hub world with no procedural generation
- A narrative arc resolved within 6 to 8 hours of play
- Hand-painted textures limited to a 2048x2048 atlas per character
- No live-service mechanics; the game ships complete on release day
- Audio composed in-house by Juno Reyes, using only analog synths

These limits keep our pipeline honest and our marketing promises realistic.

## The Human Cost of Polish

Crunch is not a badge; it is a debt that accrues interest in morale. During the *Elden Crest* final push, we worked three consecutive weekends. The build was stable, but two team members burned out and left within six months. We now enforce a hard stop at 7 p.m. and use the remaining hours for documentation, not production. Sustainable velocity beats heroic sprints. The game will still exist in ten years; your health determines whether you do.
