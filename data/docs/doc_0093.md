# Grant Proposal: Scaling Equitable Digital Literacy through the OpenClass Initiative

## Executive Summary
The Meridian Education Foundation requests $480,000 over two years to expand the OpenClass Initiative, a nonprofit edtech program that delivers adaptive literacy tools to underserved middle schools. Since our pilot in September 2022, we have reached 14,300 students across 47 districts, closing reading-gap metrics by an average of 18 percent.

## Organizational Background
Founded in 2018 by Dr. Amara Okonkwo and engineer Luis Delgado, Meridian operates as a 501(c)(3) with a fully distributed team of 32 staff members. Our mission is to eliminate the digital divide in K-8 education by providing open-source curricula paired with low-bandwidth platforms. In 2023, we received the EdTech Equity Award from the National Learning Consortium, validating our approach to culturally responsive design.

## Program Description
The OpenClass Initiative deploys three integrated components in partner schools:
- Adaptive reading modules localized in English, Spanish, and Haitian Creole
- Real-time teacher dashboards powered by privacy-first analytics
- Offline-capable tablets preloaded with 200+ hours of interactive content

Each component is developed in collaboration with classroom educators rather than outsourced vendors, ensuring pedagogical relevance.

## Implementation Plan
Phase one (January 2025–June 2025) will onboard 22 new schools in rural Mississippi and urban Detroit. Phase two (July 2025–December 2026) focuses on sustainability training for district technology coordinators. By March 2026, we project serving an additional 9,500 learners while reducing per-student technology costs to $34 annually.

## Budget and Sustainability
The requested $480,000 covers curriculum engineering ($210,000), hardware procurement ($140,000), and evaluation led by Dr. Priya Mehta at Vanderbilt University ($130,000). Matching support from the Carter Family Foundation brings total program funding to $620,000. After grant completion, recurring revenue from district licensing will cover 70 percent of operating expenses.

## Conclusion
With your partnership, Meridian can transform OpenClass from a regional pilot into a nationally replicable model. We invite the foundation to join us in ensuring every child has the digital literacy skills to thrive by 2026.
