# Aurora Robotics Team Onboarding Guide

Welcome to the Aurora Robotics collective. This guide covers the protocols, tools, and culture you will need during your first 90 days on the floor.

## Team Structure and Mentorship

Every new recruit is paired with a senior engineer. Your mentor this season is Dr. Lena Vasquez, who leads the manipulation subgroup. She will review your CAD submissions and sign off on safety checklists before any hardware activation. Direct questions about scheduling to Marcus Chen, our operations lead.

## Safety and Certification

No one operates the KUKA arm or enters Bay 3 without completing the workshop certification. The mandatory session runs on March 14 at 09:00 in Building C. You must also pass the lockout-tagout quiz with a score of 85 percent or higher.

## Tooling and Workspace Norms

We use GitHub for version control and SolidWorks 2024 for mechanical design. Keep your desks clear of loose lithium cells, and always log tool usage in the shared sheet.

Your first-week checklist:
- Attend the March 14 safety certification in Building C
- Meet with Dr. Vasquez to set your 30-day milestone plan
- Configure your SolidWorks license and clone the main repository
- Complete the lockout-tagout assessment with 85 percent accuracy
- Shadow Marcus Chen during the Tuesday inventory audit

## Project Timeline and Expectations

The competition build window opens on April 2 and closes on May 18. We expect weekly progress logs every Friday by 17:00. If you miss two consecutive deadlines, you will be moved to the support rotation until consistency improves.

## Culture and Communication

We value direct feedback over politeness. In design reviews, challenge assumptions with data, not volume. Our team grew from 8 members in 2019 to 42 this year, and that scale only works if everyone documents decisions in the shared wiki.

Read this guide twice, then sign the acknowledgment form in the onboarding folder. We are glad you are here.
