# HelixTrack LIMS Module v2.4 — Product Requirements Document

## Overview
This document defines the release criteria for the HelixTrack Laboratory Information Management System (LIMS) Module v2.4, developed for Meridian BioLabs. The update addresses sample throughput bottlenecks observed in Q1 2024 and introduces automated chain-of-custody logging for GMP-compliant workflows.

## Scope and Objectives
The primary objective is to reduce manual data entry by 40% while maintaining 21 CFR Part 11 compliance. Dr. Elena Vasquez, Head of Quality Systems, has signed off on the validation protocol effective March 15, 2025. The module must integrate with existing Thermo Fisher SpectraMax instruments without disrupting ongoing Phase II trials.

Key deliverables include:
- Real-time barcode scanning with 99.9% read accuracy
- Automated temperature deviation alerts within 30 seconds
- Audit trail exports in XML and PDF formats
- Role-based access for up to 150 concurrent users

## Functional Requirements
The system shall support batch ingestion of up to 10,000 sample records per hour. Each record must capture collector ID, timestamp, and geolocation hash. Workflow templates for nucleic acid extraction and protein quantification must be configurable by lab managers without engineering support.

- Sample lifecycle tracking from receipt through cryogenic storage at -80°C
- Integration with Meridian BioLabs’ existing SAP ERP via REST API
- Electronic signature capture with biometric fallback for Dr. Vasquez and designated QA officers
- Offline caching for up to 72 hours during network maintenance windows

## Non-Functional Requirements
Performance benchmarks require page load times below 1.2 seconds under 85% CPU load. Data retention policies align with a 7-year archival mandate. Security patches must deploy within 48 hours of vendor release.

## Timeline and Milestones
Development sprint begins April 1, 2025. User acceptance testing runs through May 20, 2025. Production deployment is scheduled for June 10, 2025, contingent on successful IQ/OQ validation at Meridian BioLabs’ Cambridge facility.

## Risk and Compliance
Failure to validate electronic records could delay the IND submission planned for July 2025. Risk mitigation includes parallel paper backup protocols and a rollback window of 4 hours post-deployment. All changes are tracked under change control ticket LIMS-4429.
