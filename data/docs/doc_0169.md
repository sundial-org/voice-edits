# Grant Proposal: Translational Genomic Therapeutics for Rare Mitochondrial Disorders

## Executive Summary
Dr. Elena Vasquez at the Helmholtz Institute for Molecular Biology requests $4.2 million over 36 months to advance AAV-based delivery of nuclear-encoded mitochondrial repair constructs. Our preliminary data, collected between January 2023 and June 2024, demonstrate 62% restoration of complex I activity in patient-derived fibroblasts.

## Research Objectives
We propose three integrated aims:
- Optimize dual-promoter cassettes for tissue-specific expression in skeletal muscle and neural tissue
- Conduct GLP-toxicology studies in non-human primates by Q2 2026
- Initiate Phase I/IIa clinical trials at St. Jude Children's Research Hospital in October 2026

## Methodology and Innovation
The Vasquez Lab employs directed evolution of synthetic mitochondrial targeting sequences combined with lipid nanoparticle encapsulation. Key technical milestones include:
- Validation of vector purity exceeding 99.4% via digital PCR
- Reduction of off-target nuclear integration to below 0.03 events per genome
- Completion of IND-enabling pharmacokinetic profiling by March 2025

## Budget and Timeline
Funding will support 14 full-time researchers, GMP manufacturing contracts with Lonza Biologics, and regulatory consulting through Charles River Laboratories. We anticipate first-in-human dosing within 28 months of award activation.

## Expected Impact
Successful completion will establish a platform technology applicable to over 340 known mitochondrial DNA depletion syndromes, bringing precision gene correction closer to clinical reality for patients currently lacking therapeutic options.
