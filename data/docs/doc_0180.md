# Draft: Spring Release — Ethiopia Yirgacheffe G1

## From the Highlands to the Roast

Every spring, we search for a coffee that reminds us why we started roasting in 2016. This year, we found it in the mist-covered hills of Gedio. Our new single-origin release, Ethiopia Yirgacheffe G1, arrived at our Portland roastery on March 22 after a 14-day journey from the Worka Cooperative.

We buy directly from producer Maria Lopez, who oversees 312 smallholder farms averaging just 2.4 hectares each. Her commitment to slow, shade-grown cherry maturation gives this lot its unmistakable clarity.

## Tasting Notes

In the cup, expect a vibrant, tea-like body with layered sweetness. We cupped 24 samples before selecting Lot 7 for this release.

- Bergamot and jasmine on the aroma
- Lemon zest and wild honey through the mid-palate
- A clean, lingering black-tea finish

The profile shines brightest between 195 °F and 202 °F, where the acidity stays crisp without turning sharp.

## Brewing Guide

We designed this roast for filter methods, though it performs beautifully as a delicate espresso.

- Pour-over: 1:16 ratio, 3-minute draw-down
- French press: 4-minute steep at a coarse grind
- AeroPress: 2-minute immersion with a paper filter

If you prefer milk, try it in a 6-ounce cappuccino; the floral notes hold up surprisingly well against steamed oat.
