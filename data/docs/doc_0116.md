# Mammalian Cell Culture: Passaging and Cryopreservation Protocol

## Introduction and Objectives
These notes cover the standard operating procedures established by Dr. Elena Vasquez for maintaining HEK293T and CHO-K1 lines in our BSL-2 suite. The primary objective is to preserve genetic stability through rigorous split ratios and to minimize mycoplasma contamination risk. All experiments logged in this session derive from the protocol revision dated October 14, 2023. Students should complete the pre-lab quiz on aseptic technique before entering the tissue culture room.

## Materials and Reagent Preparation
Prepare all media under the laminar flow hood using filter-sterilized components. The basal medium is DMEM high-glucose supplemented with 10% fetal bovine serum (FBS) and 1% penicillin-streptomycin. Pre-warm reagents to 37°C in the water bath for no longer than 30 minutes. For dissociation, use 0.25% trypsin-EDTA stored at -20°C in 5 mL aliquots. Cryopreservation medium consists of 90% FBS and 10% DMSO, prepared fresh on the day of freezing.

## Passaging Procedure
Monitor confluence daily using the inverted phase-contrast microscope. Once cultures reach 80-90% confluence—typically 48 to 72 hours after seeding—proceed with subculturing. Aspirate spent medium and wash once with phosphate-buffered saline lacking calcium and magnesium. Add 2 mL of trypsin per T-75 flask and incubate at 37°C with 5% CO2 for 3 to 5 minutes until cells detach. Neutralize with 8 mL of complete medium and transfer to a 15 mL conical tube.

## Cell Counting and Seeding
Centrifuge the suspension at 200 × g for 5 minutes. Resuspend the pellet in 5 mL of fresh medium and perform a viable count using trypan blue exclusion on the automated cell counter. Target a seeding density of 2.5 × 10^5 cells per mL for routine expansion. Calculate the split ratio carefully; HEK293T lines typically require a 1:6 split every 3 days.

- Use only certified serological pipettes calibrated within the last 90 days
- Label all flasks with cell line, passage number, date, and your initials
- Do not allow trypsin exposure to exceed 6 minutes to prevent clumping
- Maintain a dedicated waste beaker with 10% bleach for liquid disposal
- Document confluence percentages in the shared electronic lab notebook

## Cryopreservation and Recovery
Freeze cells at passage P12 or lower to ensure high post-thaw viability. Resuspend pellets at 1 × 10^7 cells per mL in ice-cold cryopreservation medium. Aliquot 1 mL per cryovial and place in a Mr. Frosty container at -80°C for 24 hours before transferring to liquid nitrogen storage. For recovery, thaw vials rapidly in a 37°C bath and dilute immediately into 10 mL of pre-warmed complete medium. Centrifuge to remove DMSO before plating in a T-25 flask.

## Quality Control and Conclusion
Perform mycoplasma PCR screening every 4 weeks and authenticate cell lines by STR profiling annually. Any culture showing abnormal morphology or growth rates deviating from the established doubling time of approximately 24 hours must be quarantined.
