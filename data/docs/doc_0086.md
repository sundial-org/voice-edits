# ClimateTech Team Meeting Notes — Q3 Operations Review

## Attendees and Context
Present: Dr. Elena Vasquez (CTO), Marcus Chen (Head of Engineering), Aisha Patel (Sustainability Lead), Tom Reed (Operations). The session focused on scaling our direct-air capture pilot ahead of the October 14 funding review with Meridian Capital.

## Pilot Metrics Update
The Dortmund facility captured 4,200 metric tons of CO2 in August, exceeding our 3,800-ton target by 10.5 percent. Energy consumption per ton dropped to 1.85 MWh, down from 2.1 MWh in July. Vasquez noted that membrane regeneration cycles are now running every 72 hours rather than every 96.

## Engineering Roadmap Adjustments
Chen proposed advancing the Module-7 rollout to September 22 instead of November. Key priorities include:
- Integrating the new sorbent blend tested by Patel's team on August 30
- Replacing the legacy compressor array at Site B before winter
- Running parallel stress tests for 48-hour continuous capture cycles
- Updating telemetry dashboards for real-time carbon-credit verification

## Funding and Partnerships
Reed confirmed that Meridian Capital requires audited lifecycle analyses by October 14. We must finalize the partnership memo with the Rhine Industrial Consortium by September 9. Patel will submit the revised water-usage audit, which shows a 22 percent reduction compared to Q2 baseline measurements.

## Action Items and Next Steps
Elena Vasquez will present the updated techno-economic model to the board on September 18. Marcus Chen owns the Module-7 timeline. Aisha Patel delivers the audit drafts by September 5. The next operations review is scheduled for September 25.
