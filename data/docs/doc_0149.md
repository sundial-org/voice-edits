# Product Requirements Document: Clinical Integration Engine v2.4 — Mercy General Hospital IT

## Overview and Scope
Mercy General Hospital IT is deploying Clinical Integration Engine (CIE) v2.4 to unify EHR, lab, and radiology feeds across the East Wing and outpatient clinics. The project, led by Senior Architect Dr. Elena Vasquez, targets full interoperability by March 15, 2026. The engine must process 14,000 HL7 messages per hour with 99.99% uptime.

## Functional Requirements
The platform shall support real-time ADT, ORU, and MDM message routing without manual intervention. Key capabilities include:
- Bidirectional interface with Epic Hyperspace 2024 and Cerner PowerChart
- Automated deduplication for duplicate patient records exceeding 0.3% variance
- Role-based access controls mapped to Active Directory groups managed by James Okonkwo
- Audit logging retained for 7 years per HIPAA guidelines

## Non-Functional and Security Requirements
Latency must remain below 120 milliseconds for critical alert pathways. All PHI in transit requires TLS 1.3 encryption; data at rest uses AES-256. The system will undergo penetration testing by RedShield Consulting every quarter, beginning Q2 2025.

## Implementation Timeline and Milestones
Phase 1 (Pilot — East Wing): January 6 to February 28, 2025
Phase 2 (Hospital-wide rollout): March 1 to May 30, 2025
Phase 3 (Outpatient integration): June 1, 2025 through March 15, 2026
Go-live approval requires sign-off from CIO Patricia Reed, CISO Marcus Li, and Chief Medical Informatics Officer Dr. Vasquez.

## Risk Management and Compliance
Failure to meet the 99.99% SLA triggers escalation to the Hospital Operations Board within 4 hours. Budget allocation is capped at $2.4 million, including vendor licensing for InterSystems HealthShare. All changes follow ITIL v4 change management protocols documented in ticket system MG-IT-8842.
