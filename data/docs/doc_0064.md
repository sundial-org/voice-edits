# MetroLink Transit Agency — Internal Research Notes: Corridor Capacity & Equity Review

## Executive Summary
Prepared for the Planning Committee on 14 October 2024 by Dr. Aisha Patel, Senior Transit Analyst. This document summarizes preliminary findings from the Q3 2024 ridership and infrastructure audit across the Eastbank, Riverside, and Northgate corridors. Total evaluated ridership exceeded 842,000 passenger trips per weekday.

## Data Sources & Methodology
Our analysis merged automated passenger counter data from August 2024 with census tract-level demographic files updated through 2023. We applied the weighted corridor stress index developed by Martinez and Liu (2022) to normalize load factors across varying bus and light-rail configurations.

Key inputs included:
- Onboard survey responses from 3,214 riders collected between 12 August and 27 September
- Signal-priority logs from 47 intersections along the Eastbank spine
- Maintenance delay reports filed between 1 January and 30 September 2024

## Corridor Performance Metrics
The Riverside Line recorded the steepest year-over-year growth at 11.3 percent, yet it also showed the highest coefficient of variation in on-time performance. Northgate Bus Route 44 maintained consistent headways but suffered from chronic overcrowding between 07:30 and 09:00.

Notable quantitative observations:
- Average peak load factor reached 1.42 passengers per available seat
- Dwell times at Central Station averaged 94 seconds, exceeding the agency target of 75 seconds
- Only 62 percent of accessible platforms met current ADA-grade slope specifications

## Equity & Accessibility Findings
Disparities remain pronounced in low-income census tracts served primarily by bus rather than rail. Residents in the Eastbank South zone—designated Equity Priority Area 7—experience an average commute duration 18 minutes longer than the system median.

Identified service gaps:
- Limited weekend frequency on Route 22 and Route 88, reducing employment access for shift workers
- Absence of real-time arrival displays at 23 of 41 high-ridership bus stops in Equity Priority Areas
- Fare-capping technology unavailable to unbanked riders using cash-only payment methods

## Next Steps & Recommendations
The Planning Committee should authorize Phase II corridor modeling by 15 November 2024. Priority capital investments include platform leveling at Riverside Station and signal prioritization along the Northgate spine. Dr. Patel will present detailed cost-benefit scenarios at the November board meeting, with final recommendations expected by 6 December 2024.
