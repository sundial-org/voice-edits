# Hospital IT Lecture Notes: Q4 Infrastructure & EHR Migration

## Overview
On October 14, 2024, Dr. Elena Vargas and the Clinical Informatics board approved the phased rollout of the Meridian EHR v4.2 platform across all three campuses. This migration replaces the legacy system that has handled approximately 14,000 active patient records since 2019. The primary objectives are to reduce latency in lab result delivery, strengthen HIPAA audit trails, and unify pharmacy ordering under a single interface.

## Migration Timeline
The deployment window runs from November 1 through December 15, 2024. Network Engineering, led by Marcus Chen, will execute server cutovers during low-census hours—specifically between 02:00 and 05:30—to minimize disruption to ICU telemetry feeds.

Key milestones include:
- November 8: Core database replication complete
- November 22: Pharmacy module live at Riverside campus
- December 3: Full integration testing with radiology PACS
- December 15: Legacy read-only archive locked

## Security Protocols
All endpoints must enforce AES-256 encryption at rest and TLS 1.3 in transit. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for anyone accessing protected health information outside the internal VLAN. The Security Operations Center will monitor anomalous login attempts in real time, with automated lockout triggered after three failed attempts within a five-minute window.

We are targeting 99.97% uptime for critical clinical services during the transition. Any unplanned downtime exceeding fifteen minutes requires immediate escalation to the Chief Medical Information Officer and documentation in the incident log within thirty minutes of restoration.

## Staff Training Requirements
Department heads must confirm that 100% of clinical staff complete the Meridian certification module by November 29, 2024. IT will host hands-on labs every Tuesday and Thursday in Conference Room B.

Training priorities:
- Navigating the unified patient timeline view
- Submitting electronic prior-authorization requests
- Recognizing and reporting phishing attempts targeting EHR credentials
- Using the emergency downtime procedure checklist

## Incident Response Plan
If data corruption or unauthorized access is detected, isolate the affected subnet immediately and notify Marcus Chen and the on-call security engineer. Do not attempt manual database repairs without authorization from the database administration team. Preserve all system logs for forensic review.

Post-incident reviews must occur within seventy-two hours and include representatives from IT, Compliance, and Clinical Operations.

## Closing Notes
This migration represents our most significant infrastructure change in five years. Success depends on precise execution, clear communication, and adherence to the timelines outlined above. Questions should be directed to the IT Service Desk or submitted through the internal ticketing portal before close of business on Friday.
