# Grant Proposal: Nexus Commerce Platform for Independent Retailers

## Executive Summary
This proposal requests $450,000 from the Meridian Digital Equity Fund to build Nexus, an open-source e-commerce backbone designed for independent retailers in underserved urban corridors. Led by Dr. Elena Vasquez at the Civic Tech Institute, the 18-month initiative will deploy by March 2026 and serve an initial cohort of 12,000 merchants across the Great Lakes region. Unlike proprietary marketplaces that extract 15-30% in fees, Nexus operates on a cooperative governance model with transparent, sliding-scale pricing.

## Problem Statement
Small retailers lost an estimated $28 billion in digital revenue during 2023-2024 because legacy platforms prioritize national brands. In the Eastside Commercial District alone, 340 family-owned stores lack affordable inventory-sync and fulfillment tools. High transaction costs and opaque algorithms have eroded local economic resilience, forcing closures that destabilize neighborhood tax bases.

## Proposed Solution
Nexus delivers a modular, API-first platform with three core pillars:
- Real-time inventory synchronization across physical and digital storefronts
- Community-owned logistics routing to reduce last-mile delivery costs by 40%
- Multilingual merchant dashboards localized for Spanish, Arabic, and Mandarin speakers

The architecture runs on federated cloud nodes hosted by participating universities, reducing vendor lock-in.

## Implementation Timeline
Phase 1 (Months 1-6): Core platform engineering and security audits with partners at Lakeview University.
Phase 2 (Months 7-12): Pilot rollout with 200 merchants in Detroit and Cleveland.
Phase 3 (Months 13-18): Regional expansion, cooperative board elections, and integration with municipal procurement systems.

## Budget and Sustainability
The $450,000 award covers engineering salaries ($210,000), pilot logistics subsidies ($95,000), and multilingual outreach ($45,000). By Q4 2026, platform transaction fees will cover 65% of operating costs, with remaining support from cooperative membership dues and regional development grants.

## Expected Impact
We project $3.2 million in retained local revenue within the first operational year. By empowering 12,000 merchants with data sovereignty and fair-market visibility, Nexus demonstrates that equitable digital infrastructure can strengthen both neighborhood economies and democratic participation in technology governance.
