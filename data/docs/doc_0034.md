# Grant Proposal: Horizon Commerce Platform

## Executive Summary
We are requesting $480,000 to accelerate development of Horizon Commerce, a merchant-first e-commerce infrastructure platform founded by Dr. Elena Vasquez in March 2022. Traditional marketplaces extract 18% in fees and restrict data ownership. Horizon replaces this with a modular stack that reduces transaction costs to 2.1% while returning full analytics control to vendors.

## Problem Statement
Small and medium retailers lost approximately 34% of digital revenue to platform monopolies during 2023. Existing solutions force merchants to choose between reach and autonomy. Horizon eliminates this trade-off by providing open APIs, embedded checkout, and real-time inventory synchronization across channels.

## Proposed Solution
Our architecture separates presentation from fulfillment. Key capabilities include:
- Headless storefront engine with sub-200ms latency globally
- Unified inventory API connecting warehouses, pop-ups, and social channels
- Automated tax-compliance module covering 47 jurisdictions
- Embedded financing tools that increase average cart value by 22%

## Implementation Timeline
Phase 1 (January 2025 – June 2025) completes the core engine and merchant onboarding portal. Phase 2 (July 2025 – December 2025) launches the partner marketplace and expands server clusters in Frankfurt and Singapore. All development will occur at our headquarters in Portland, Oregon, with remote engineering support from Toronto and Berlin.

## Budget Overview
The $480,000 grant will be allocated as follows: $210,000 for backend infrastructure and security audits, $135,000 for UX research and frontend development, $85,000 for regulatory compliance testing, and $50,000 for community onboarding workshops. We project breakeven by Q3 2026 with 1,200 active merchants generating $4.2M in annual transaction volume.

## Impact Metrics
Success will be measured by merchant adoption rate, reduction in average fee burden, and platform uptime exceeding 99.98%. Within 18 months of the grant period ending in December 2026, we aim to onboard 500 independent retailers and redirect $12M in revenue away from extractive marketplaces back into local economies.
