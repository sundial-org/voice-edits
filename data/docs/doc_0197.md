# Apex Commerce Platform — Product Requirements Document v2.4

## Overview & Objectives
Apex Commerce is a headless e-commerce platform targeting B2B and direct-to-consumer retailers. The Q3 2025 release must support real-time inventory synchronization, PCI-DSS Level 1 compliant payments, and sub-200ms API response times under peak loads of 50,000 concurrent users. Product owner Sarah Chen has prioritized checkout conversion and vendor onboarding as the two critical success metrics for the September 2025 launch window.

## Scope & Key Features
The release covers the core marketplace engine, merchant dashboard, and customer-facing storefront API. Out of scope are mobile native apps and AI-driven recommendation modules, which remain on the 2026 roadmap. Must-have capabilities include:

- Real-time inventory tracking across 12 regional warehouses with automatic stock-level alerts at 15 units remaining
- Multi-currency pricing engine supporting USD, EUR, and GBP with daily exchange rate updates pulled from the European Central Bank feed
- One-click reorder functionality tied to purchase history retained for a minimum of 24 months
- Admin approval workflows for new vendors, requiring verification by the Risk Operations team within 48 business hours

## Technical Architecture
Services will run on Kubernetes clusters in us-east-1 and eu-west-2, with PostgreSQL 16 for transactional data and Redis 7 for session caching. The storefront will consume GraphQL via Apollo Federation, while internal analytics will stream through Apache Kafka to Snowflake. All endpoints must enforce OAuth 2.0 and rate-limit unauthenticated requests to 100 calls per minute per IP.

## Milestones & Timeline
Discovery and design reviews conclude by July 15, 2025. Engineering sprints run from August 1 through September 15, followed by a two-week security audit conducted by external firm Veracode. The production rollout is targeted for October 3, 2025, with a phased canary release beginning in North America and expanding to EMEA by October 17, 2025.

## Success Metrics
We will measure checkout conversion rate improvement from the current baseline of 2.1% to a target of 3.4%, reduce average vendor onboarding time from 9 days to 3 days, and maintain 99.99% platform uptime during the Black Friday 2025 traffic event on November 28, 2025. Customer satisfaction scores collected via post-purchase surveys must reach or exceed 4.6 out of 5.0 within 60 days of launch.
