# MetroLink Transit Agency — Real-Time Fleet Monitoring PRD

## Overview
MetroLink Transit Agency requires a centralized Real-Time Fleet Monitoring platform to replace the legacy AVL system deployed in 2014. The new solution must support 842 active vehicles across bus, light rail, and paratransit divisions. Project sponsor Maria Delgado, Director of Operations, has set a hard launch target of March 15, 2026, to coincide with the Route 42 expansion.

## Objectives
The primary goal is to reduce average passenger wait-time variance by 18 percent within 90 days of go-live. Secondary objectives include integrating with the existing CAD/AVL backbone, providing dispatcher dashboards with sub-3-second latency, and meeting ADA compliance for real-time arrival announcements at all 312 station platforms.

## Functional Requirements
Core capabilities must cover the full operational lifecycle:

- Real-time GPS tracking with 5-second refresh intervals for all revenue vehicles
- Automated headway management alerts when bus spacing deviates by more than 4 minutes
- Predictive arrival algorithms using historical data from the Central Traffic Signal Network
- Dispatcher override controls for emergency rerouting during severe weather events
- Passenger-facing API feeding third-party apps and digital signage

## Non-Functional Requirements
System availability must reach 99.99 percent during service hours (05:00–01:30 daily). Data retention policies require raw telemetry storage for 7 years per FTA audit guidelines, with encrypted archives hosted in the Eastern Region Data Center. The platform must scale to accommodate 1,200 concurrent dispatcher sessions without performance degradation.

## Timeline & Milestones
Phase 1 discovery runs through August 2025. Vendor selection by October 30, 2025, followed by pilot deployment on the Blue Line corridor in January 2026. Full fleet rollout completes by March 15, 2026, with a 30-day hypercare window ending April 14, 2026.

## Stakeholders
Maria Delgado (Director of Operations) serves as executive sponsor. Engineering lead James Park oversees integration with the 2019 Signal Priority Module. Transit Operations Manager Sandra Lee manages dispatcher training and change control for the 312 affected staff members.
