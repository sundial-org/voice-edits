# NOCTURNE COLLECTIVE — Open Source Audio Archive

## Overview
Founded in March 2019 by producer Lena Vasquez and engineer Marcus Okonkwo, Nocturne Collective operates out of a converted textile warehouse in Rotterdam. We maintain a shared library of field recordings, analog synth patches, and collaborative stems available under Creative Commons licenses. Our mandate is transparency in process: every release ships with session notes, signal-chain diagrams, and raw multitracks.

## Catalog Release: Signal Drift (October 2023)
The collective's seventh full-length, *Signal Drift*, was mixed at Studio Meridian over fourteen consecutive nights. It features contributions from 11 members across 3 time zones, with over 400 individual audio files archived in our repository.

## Core Principles
We believe collective authorship requires disciplined documentation. Members adhere to the following workflow rules:
- All source files must include embedded metadata and a dated changelog
- Master buses are limited to 6 dB of dynamic range compression before delivery
- Collaborative stems are uploaded within 48 hours of session closure
- Every project folder contains a `README.md` and a `gear_list.txt`

## Technical Infrastructure
Our archive runs on a self-hosted Git LFS instance with 2.4 terabytes of redundant storage. We use a standardized 96 kHz / 24-bit format for all masters, with proxy files rendered at 48 kHz for preview. Marcus Okonkwo oversees the analog tape digitization pipeline, which has preserved 127 reels since 2021.

## Participation & Licensing
Anyone may download, remix, or perform works from the catalog. Commercial use requires attribution to the collective and notification via `contact@nocturne.audio`. We host quarterly review sessions—next on 14 January 2025—where contributors vote on repository maintenance priorities and release schedules.

Reach the collective through our public forum or visit the Rotterdam warehouse by appointment. Lena Vasquez and the editorial board respond to submissions within 72 hours.
