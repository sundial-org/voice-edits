# Grant Proposal: Modernizing Clinical Data Infrastructure at Meridian General Hospital

## Executive Summary
Meridian General Hospital’s Information Technology Division seeks $420,000 in funding to replace legacy clinical data pipelines and implement a unified interoperability framework. Led by Chief Information Officer Dr. Elena Vasquez, the initiative will connect radiology, pharmacy, and emergency systems through a secure FHIR-based exchange layer. Over the past 18 months, our IT group has documented a 40 percent increase in interface failures that delay critical care coordination. This proposal outlines a targeted upgrade to eliminate those bottlenecks, reduce manual reconciliation, and establish a scalable foundation for future telemedicine expansion across our three-county service area.

## Project Objectives
The grant will support four core deliverables designed to improve reliability and regulatory compliance by March 2026:

- Deploy a centralized health information exchange gateway integrating HL7 v2 and FHIR R4 standards across all inpatient units
- Migrate 2.4 million legacy patient records to an encrypted, audit-compliant cloud architecture hosted within U.S. jurisdiction
- Train 85 clinical and technical staff on new data governance protocols and incident response procedures
- Achieve 99.9 percent uptime for critical interfaces, measured against baseline metrics recorded in January 2025

## Implementation Timeline
Work will commence on July 1, 2025, following approval of vendor contracts. Phase One (July–September 2025) focuses on network segmentation and security hardening. Phase Two (October 2025–January 2026) covers data migration and interface testing with partner laboratories. Phase Three (February–March 2026) delivers staff certification, full production cutover, and independent audit verification. Dr. Vasquez will chair a biweekly steering committee including representatives from nursing informatics, biomedical engineering, and compliance.

## Budget and Impact
The requested $420,000 breaks down into $210,000 for software licensing and integration services, $135,000 for cloud infrastructure and encryption modules, and $75,000 for personnel training and change-management consulting. We project a 30 percent reduction in interface-related downtime within six months of go-live, translating to faster lab turnaround and fewer manual chart corrections. Long-term, the standardized architecture positions Meridian General to participate in statewide quality reporting networks without additional middleware investment.

## Conclusion
Hospital IT is not merely a support function; it is the backbone of safe, timely patient care. By funding this modernization effort, the grantor will directly strengthen clinical decision-making across every department we serve. We welcome the opportunity to discuss technical specifications, risk mitigation strategies, and milestones with the review committee at your earliest convenience.
