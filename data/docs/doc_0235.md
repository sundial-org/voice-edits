# Internal FAQ: Aurora Climate Systems — Onboarding & Operations

## Welcome and Company Context

Aurora Climate Systems builds direct-air capture infrastructure and verification software for heavy industry. Founded in 2019, we now operate four pilot arrays across the Pacific Northwest and employ 140 people between Seattle and Portland. Our internal culture emphasizes radical transparency: every employee receives full access to pilot telemetry, financial burn reports, and board-level risk assessments within their first week.

If you are joining the Engineering or Field Operations tracks, you should complete the safety certification module led by Dr. Elena Vasquez before accessing any live site. For Policy and Customer Success hires, the priority is the client confidentiality briefing scheduled every Tuesday at 10:00 a.m. Pacific.

## Carbon Accounting Protocol

We follow the 2024 Greenhouse Gas Protocol revision for all scope measurements. This means our internal dashboards treat biogenic carbon separately from fossil-derived streams, and we report net removal figures only after deducting energy-related lifecycle emissions.

Common questions about methodology:
- All pilot data is audited quarterly by an independent third party, with reports published internally before external release.
- We do not sell offset credits; every ton captured is retired against Aurora's own operational footprint or assigned to contracted industrial partners.
- The verification pipeline requires dual sign-off from both the data engineer on duty and the climate science lead.

If you discover an anomaly in telemetry that suggests reporting errors, escalate immediately through the integrity portal rather than Slack.

## Data Security and Access Levels

Customer contracts with firms like Meridian Steel and Pacific Cement contain strict non-disclosure clauses. Even anonymized performance metrics require legal review before they appear in marketing materials or conference presentations.

Your laptop and cloud credentials are provisioned with role-based permissions on day one. Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for the telemetry repository, and portable storage devices are prohibited on site premises. Remember that field tablets sync via encrypted satellite links; do not attempt to bypass the gateway for faster uploads.

## Internal Communication Norms

We use asynchronous documentation as the default. Meeting notes live in the shared wiki within thirty minutes of conclusion, and decisions without written records are considered provisional.

For urgent issues affecting pilot safety or data integrity, call the on-duty operations manager directly. For everything else, post in the relevant channel and allow four business hours for response before escalating. This protects deep-work blocks and respects the distributed schedules of our field teams.

We revisit these norms every quarter. If a process is slowing you down rather than protecting the mission, propose an amendment in the #policy-feedback channel.
