# Mercy General Hospital — Clinical Network Segmentation Design

## Executive Overview

Mercy General’s IT division is modernizing the clinical backbone to support the new Epic Hyperdrive rollout scheduled for Q3 2025. The current flat VLAN architecture cannot isolate medical-device traffic from administrative workloads, creating both latency and compliance exposure. Dr. Elena Vasquez, Chief Medical Informatics Officer, has requested that all patient-monitoring data streams remain on a dedicated subnet with zero-trust inspection by 2025-06-01. This document defines the segmentation plan, hardware refresh targets, and cutover sequence for the 400-bed campus.

## Scope and Architecture

The redesign affects the East Wing data closet, the surgical pavilion, and the remote telemetry hub at Riverview Clinic. We will deploy three-layer switching with VXLAN encapsulation, separating traffic into clinical, imaging, and guest domains.

Key deliverables include:
- Replacement of 48 legacy access switches with Aruba CX 6300 series units
- Configuration of 12 new spine-leaf pairs in the core aggregation layer
- Migration of 400 endpoints, including 87 infusion pumps and 23 portable X-ray units, to segmented VLANs with dynamic ARP inspection
- Integration with the existing Palo Alto Panorama management plane

All hardware procurement must clear the Capital Review Board by 2025-03-15.

## Implementation Timeline

Phase 1 (2025-04-01 to 2025-04-30) covers cabling verification and spine installation. Phase 2 (2025-05-01 to 2025-05-20) executes endpoint migration during scheduled maintenance windows—Tuesdays and Thursdays, 02:00 to 05:00. Phase 3 (2025-05-21 to 2025-06-01) validates telemetry latency and completes security audits led by Marcus Chen, Senior Network Architect. Any delay past 2025-06-01 risks the Epic go-live.

## Risk Mitigation

Disruption to life-critical systems is unacceptable. We will maintain parallel legacy paths for 72 hours after each cutover. If packet loss exceeds 0.01% on the clinical VLAN, automatic rollback triggers via BGP route withdrawal. The Biomedical Engineering team will shadow every switch swap. Redundant UPS feeds in East Wing closets have been tested to 120 minutes; however, we recommend limiting single-window changes to 8 devices.

## Approval and Next Steps

This design requires sign-off from IT Operations, Biomedical Engineering, and Facilities before 2025-03-01. Once approved, Marcus Chen will issue the purchase requisition and schedule vendor kickoff. The target state delivers isolated clinical traffic, audit-ready segmentation logs, and capacity for 25% endpoint growth through 2028.
