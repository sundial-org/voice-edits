# EdTech Equity Lab — Quarterly Research Notes

## Executive Summary
On March 14, 2024, the EdTech Equity Lab convened its advisory board to review pilot outcomes from the Spring literacy cohort. Preliminary data from the Oakland and Detroit districts suggest that adaptive tutoring platforms yield meaningful gains only when paired with structured facilitator support.

## Methodology & Cohort Design
We followed 1,247 students across grades 3–5 from January through May 2024. Dr. Amara Okonkwo led the evaluation design, using a matched-pair comparison against prior-year benchmarks. Key controls included:
- Baseline reading fluency scores (DIBELS 8)
- Weekly session duration logs
- Teacher-reported engagement ratings on a 4-point Likert scale

## Key Findings
Results exceeded our conservative projections in two of three metrics. Average weekly usage reached 4.2 hours, up from 2.1 in the fall pilot. Notably, students working with trained mentors at the Riverside Community Center improved comprehension scores by 12.3 percentile points.

However, access barriers remained acute:
- 34% of households reported inconsistent broadband access
- Device-sharing among siblings reduced individual practice time by an estimated 28 minutes per day
- Only 61% of participating families completed the onboarding tutorial within 48 hours

## Implementation Challenges
The partnership with Horizon Digital Learning faced unexpected delays after a server migration on February 9 disrupted progress tracking for 11 school days. Meanwhile, staff turnover at the Detroit hub meant that two planned family workshops were canceled, lowering caregiver participation to 19% versus a 40% target.

## Next Steps
Moving into Q3 2024, we will prioritize offline-capable modules and expand the mentor network to include 15 new university tutors by August 1. Dr. Okonkwo will present the full dataset at the National Learning Sciences Conference on June 22. We also aim to secure supplemental funding to distribute 500 dedicated tablets before the fall semester begins.
