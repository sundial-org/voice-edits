# Product Requirements Document: TerraSync Grid Intelligence Platform v2.4

## Context and Objectives
TerraSync is building the operating layer for distributed clean energy. The v2.4 release targets independent power producers managing portfolios over 50 MW. By integrating real-time weather telemetry with battery dispatch algorithms, we aim to reduce curtailment losses by 12% before Q3 2025. Lead product manager Dr. Elena Vasquez will oversee rollout across our pilot regions in the North Sea and Ontario.

## Functional Requirements
The platform must ingest satellite irradiance feeds every 15 minutes and trigger automated charge cycles when spot prices drop below €32/MWh. Core modules include:
- Predictive degradation tracking for lithium-iron-phosphate cells rated at 4,000 cycles
- API endpoints compliant with IEC 61850 for substation integration
- A dashboard displaying carbon intensity per MWh against the 2024 baseline of 147 gCO2/kWh
- Automated alerting to grid operators within 90 seconds of frequency deviation

## Non-Functional Requirements
Latency for dispatch commands must stay under 250 ms during peak load events exceeding 1.2 GW. The architecture will run on Kubernetes clusters in Frankfurt and Toronto with 99.99% uptime SLAs. Security audits by CyberGrid Labs are scheduled for March 15, 2025, before commercial launch.

## Milestones and Metrics
Beta deployment begins January 20, 2025, with full production release targeted for June 30, 2025. Success criteria include processing 18 terabytes of meteorological data monthly and achieving customer onboarding within 14 days. Revenue impact is projected at $4.2M ARR by end of fiscal 2025.

## Risks and Dependencies
Regulatory approval in Germany depends on updated BNetzA guidelines expected by February 2025. Supply chain delays for edge-computing gateways remain a risk; we have secured backup vendors in Denmark. If weather model accuracy falls below 94%, dispatch optimization will require manual override protocols.
