# Weekly Operations Status Update — Meridian Freight Logistics

## Executive Summary
This week, Meridian Freight Logistics maintained strong operational momentum across our North American corridor. With the holiday surge approaching, we focused on capacity scaling, driver retention, and real-time route optimization. Overall network on-time performance held at 94.2%, while fuel expenditure came in 3.8% under forecast thanks to strategic rerouting through the I-75 corridor.

## Operational Metrics
Fleet utilization averaged 87% Monday through Friday, with peak volume hitting Wednesday, October 16. Our Atlanta hub processed 14,320 pallets—up 11% from the prior week—without a single safety incident. Cross-dock efficiency improved after we implemented the new WMS module on October 14.

- On-time delivery rate: 94.2%
- Average dwell time: 4.6 hours
- Fuel cost variance: -3.8%
- Pallets processed (Atlanta): 14,320
- Safety incidents: 0
- WMS rollout date: October 14

## Route and Capacity Adjustments
We added two dedicated lanes between Chicago and Dallas to absorb demand from the manufacturing uptick in Cook County. Driver Marcus Chen completed 1,042 miles with zero detention time, setting a new benchmark for the Midwest division. Meanwhile, the Denver yard expanded cold-storage capacity by 220 slots, operational as of Friday, October 18.

## Customer and Partner Updates
Client feedback scores improved to 4.8 out of 5, driven largely by the transparent tracking updates pushed through the Meridian app. Contract renewals with Apex Retail Group and Horizon Distribution are on track for November 1 execution. We also finalized insurance terms with Liberty Logistics Coverage for Q4.

## Issues and Mitigation
Heavy rainfall in the Gulf Coast region delayed three inbound containers destined for Houston on Tuesday, October 15. Contingency routing through Memphis recovered 92% of the affected cargo by Thursday morning. We are reviewing contingency protocols with Regional Manager Elena Vasquez to close the remaining gap.

## Next Week Priorities
We will onboard 18 new owner-operators, deploy updated ELD firmware fleet-wide by October 22, and run stress tests at the Phoenix cross-dock. Our goal is to maintain sub-5-hour dwell times through the end of the month while scaling capacity for the pre-holiday peak.
