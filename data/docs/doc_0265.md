# Grant Proposal: Resonance Collective — Community Sound Labs

## Project Overview
Resonance Collective seeks $42,000 to launch Community Sound Labs, a free music-production initiative serving the Eastside Arts Corridor. Founded in 2019 by producers Mara Voss and Darnell Reed, our collective operates out of a converted warehouse at 884 Cedar Street. Over the past four years we have released 73 tracks by 28 local artists, yet we lack dedicated educational hardware. This grant will bridge that gap.

## Program Design
Community Sound Labs will run three 10-week cohorts in 2025, serving 36 emerging producers aged 16–25. Each participant receives 20 hours of guided studio time, mentorship from industry engineers, and a completed EP released on our label. The curriculum emphasizes analog synthesis, field recording, and collaborative mixing.

Key deliverables include:
- Installation of 6 workstation pods with MIDI controllers and reference monitors
- A mobile recording kit for neighborhood outreach
- A final showcase concert at the Cedar Street Warehouse on October 18, 2025
- Digital archives of every session, licensed under Creative Commons

## Budget and Timeline
Funding will be distributed across equipment (55%), personnel (30%), and outreach (15%). We request an initial disbursement of $18,000 by March 1, 2025, with the balance released by June 15 upon milestone review. All hardware will be purchased through local vendors, including Soundline Electronics and Reed Audio Services.

## Impact and Sustainability
By 2026 we project 120 graduates, 90% of whom will continue producing independently. Partnerships with the Eastside Public Library and Metro Youth Arts ensure long-term venue access and promotional support. Resonance Collective will match 20% of the grant through in-kind studio hours and volunteer engineering.

We ask the Arts Futures Foundation to invest in equitable sound education. Together we can turn idle creativity into published art.
