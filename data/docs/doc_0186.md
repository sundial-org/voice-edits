# Modular Synthesis & Collective Workflow: Lecture Notes

## Session Overview
On 14 March 2024, the Resonance Collective gathered in Studio B to discuss how modular patching can reshape collaborative production. Our goal was not to replace DAW-based workflows but to introduce voltage-controlled thinking into group composition. Guests included synth builder Elena Marquez, mix engineer Tomás Ruiz, and resident producer Juno Park.

## Signal Path Fundamentals
Before touching a cable, we mapped the signal chain. Marquez reminded us that a single Eurorack case running at 12V can behave differently than a 5U system. We traced audio-rate modulation from an oscillator through a low-pass gate, then into a stereo mixer with -6 dB headroom reserved for live overdubs.

## Collaborative Patch Protocol
To prevent chaos with six hands in one rack, we adopted a color-coding system:
- Red cables for clock and gate signals
- Yellow for audio path
- Blue for control voltage only
- White for send/return loops with the collective mixer

This protocol reduced crosstalk errors by 40 percent during our first trial session on 22 April.

## Recording & Integration
We captured takes at 48 kHz / 24-bit using a direct-to-disk setup. Ruiz insisted on leaving 3 dB of analog headroom before the A/D stage, which preserved transient detail when we layered modular drones under acoustic recordings from Park’s field mic session in Kyoto, recorded 9 November 2023.
