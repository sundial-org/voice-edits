# Robotics Team Weekly Status — Week of October 14

## Mechanical Milestones
The chassis assembly is now 90% complete. Marcus Chen finalized the aluminum frame welding on Tuesday, and the drive-train module was mounted by Thursday evening. We are still waiting on the custom 12-tooth sprocket from our supplier, expected no later than October 18.

## Software & Controls
Autonomous navigation saw major progress this week. The LiDAR integration pipeline, led by Dr. Elena Vasquez, passed its first closed-loop test on Wednesday. Key updates include:
- Recalibrated PID constants for the 2.4 m/s sprint mode
- Fixed coordinate-frame drift in the SLAM node
- Added emergency stop handshake for the ROS 2 middleware layer
- Validated obstacle avoidance within a 1.5-meter buffer zone

## Electrical & Power Systems
Battery management remains our critical path. We recorded a 14.2 V drop under 35 A load during stress testing—well within tolerance, but the thermal sensors on cell bank B triggered a false positive twice. The wiring harness for the manipulator arm is complete, with all 18 connections verified.

## Testing Schedule & Next Steps
Our field trials at the Northfield Arena are locked in for October 22. Before then, the team must complete endurance runs, validate the 45-second cycle-time target, and resolve the sprocket delay. Daily standups will shift to 08:00 starting Monday to align with the integration sprint.

## Budget & Logistics
