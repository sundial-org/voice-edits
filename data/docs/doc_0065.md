# Lecture Notes: Scaling Direct Air Capture — Q3 Internal Seminar

## Context and Market Signal
On 14 August 2024, the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change released its supplementary brief stating that anthropogenic carbon removal must reach 5 Gt CO₂ annually by 2040 to stay within the 1.5 °C pathway. For Meridian Climate Technologies, this is not abstract policy; it is a procurement pipeline. Our Series C milestones were explicitly tied to demonstrating 50,000 tonnes of durable sequestration capacity by Q2 2025.

## Core Technology Stack
Dr. Elena Vasquez presented updates on the sorbent regeneration loop developed at our Rotterdam pilot site. Key takeaways:
- Thermal swing regeneration now operates at 92 °C, down from 118 °C in the March build.
- Energy intensity per tonne captured dropped to 1,850 kWh, within 8 % of our 2026 target.
- The modular container design allows parallel scaling without custom civil works.

## Operational Risks and Mitigations
We are not ignoring the headwinds. Three immediate concerns:
- Grid interconnection delays in the Netherlands have pushed the Rotterdam Phase II energization from October 2024 to January 2025.
- Supply chain volatility for specialty zeolites requires dual-sourcing from both Clariant and BASF.
- Regulatory credits under the EU Carbon Removal Certification Framework remain undefined for direct air capture paired with geological storage.

## Financial Roadmap
CFO Marcus Li confirmed that the $42 million round led by NorthBridge Ventures closes on 1 October 2024. Allocation breaks down as 60 % capital expenditure for Rotterdam, 25 % R&D for next-generation sorbents, and 15 % working capital. We maintain a 24-month runway assuming no revenue until offtake agreements are signed in H1 2025.

## Next Steps
All engineering leads must submit updated Gantt charts by 22 August. The board review is scheduled for 5 September 2024, where we will present validated capture-rate data from the 2,000-hour continuous run. If the metrics hold, we greenlight procurement for Module 4.
