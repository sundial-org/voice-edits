# Orion's Belt Stargazing Night: Event Plan

## Overview and Objectives
The Riverside Astronomy Club will host its annual deep-sky observation on Saturday, November 14, 2025, at the Oak Hill Observatory grounds. Dr. Elena Vasquez will lead the session, focusing on the Orion Nebula and the Pleiades cluster. Our goal is to enroll 40 members and guests, provide guided telescope viewing, and distribute educational materials about winter constellations. We will begin at 6:30 PM and conclude by 10:00 PM, weather permitting.

## Venue and Logistics
The Oak Hill Observatory offers dark skies with a Bortle scale of 3, ideal for viewing faint messier objects. We will set up in the eastern meadow to avoid light pollution from the parking area. The club will provide three 8-inch Dobsonian telescopes, one 4-inch refractor, and two pairs of 10x50 binoculars. Members should bring red flashlights and warm blankets, as temperatures are expected to drop to 42°F after sunset. Parking is available for 25 vehicles near the visitor center.

## Program Schedule
- 6:15 PM — Check-in and equipment calibration
- 6:45 PM — Welcome remarks by Dr. Vasquez
- 7:15 PM — Guided tour of Orion's Belt and the Trapezium
- 8:30 PM — Hot cider break and Q&A session
- 9:15 PM — Astrophotography workshop using the club's Canon EOS R tracking mount
- 9:45 PM — Closing remarks and next event preview

## Safety and Accessibility
We will maintain a first-aid station near the registration table and require signed waivers for anyone under 18. Pathways to the telescopes will be marked with reflective tape, and a volunteer will assist guests with mobility needs. In case of cloud cover exceeding 60 percent, we will shift to an indoor presentation in the visitor center auditorium.

## Promotion and Follow-Up
Flyers will be distributed at Riverside Community College by October 30. Social media posts will highlight Dr. Vasquez's research on stellar nurseries. After the event, attendees will receive a survey link and an invitation to the December 5 workshop on planetary imaging. We aim to grow our mailing list by 15 subscribers and secure funding for a new solar telescope by January 2026.

## Conclusion
This stargazing night represents our commitment to accessible astronomy education. With careful planning, quality equipment, and expert leadership, the Riverside Astronomy Club will deliver a memorable experience that deepens our community's connection to the night sky.
