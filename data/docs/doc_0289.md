# Incident Postmortem: Apex Logistics Routing Platform Outage — March 14, 2024

## Executive Summary
On March 14, 2024, Apex Logistics experienced a 4-hour degradation of its central routing and dispatch platform, Meridian Route. The incident began at 06:17 EST and was fully resolved by 10:42 EST. During the window, automated load balancing failed for 47 active delivery vehicles in the Northeast corridor. Dispatchers reverted to manual paper manifests, delaying an estimated 12,000 packages. No cargo was lost, but service-level agreements for 312 business customers were breached. This report details the timeline, root cause, and corrective actions.

## Timeline of Events
- **06:17 EST** — Monitoring alerts fired for elevated latency on the Meridian Route API gateway.
- **06:31 EST** — On-call engineer Priya Malhotra acknowledged the page and observed 502 errors spiking to 34% of requests.
- **07:05 EST** — Incident commander declared a Severity-1 event and activated the logistics emergency bridge.
- **08:12 EST** — Team identified a corrupted Redis cache cluster following an overnight patch deployment (v2.4.1).
- **09:30 EST** — Rollback to v2.4.0 completed; routing engine began recovering.
- **10:42 EST** — All 47 vehicles resumed digital manifest synchronization and metrics returned to baseline.

## Impact Assessment
The outage primarily affected the Philadelphia, Baltimore, and Newark distribution hubs. Key metrics include:
- **47 vehicles** diverted to manual dispatch protocols
- **12,000 packages** delayed by an average of 3.2 hours
- **312 customers** impacted, with 18 requesting service credits totaling $41,500
- Zero safety incidents or temperature-controlled cargo spoilage reported

Customer communications were sent by 11:00 EST via the automated notification pipeline.

## Root Cause Analysis
The trigger was a configuration drift introduced during the v2.4.1 deployment at 03:00 EST. A missing environment variable caused the caching layer to write invalid geospatial indexes into the primary Redis cluster (node `redis-rout-04`). Once cache eviction cycles began at 06:00 EST, corrupted route polygons propagated to the load-balancer. The system did not fail closed—rather, it returned partial routing tables—making detection slower. The deployment gate lacked a canary stage for the geospatial microservice.

## Remediation and Action Items
To prevent recurrence, the engineering and operations teams have committed to the following:
- Implement a 30-minute canary rollout for all Meridian Route releases, monitored by automated rollback triggers.
- Add redundant read-replicas for geospatial caches with automatic failover, reducing single-node dependency.
- Revise the incident response playbook to include manual dispatch activation checklists for all 23 regional hubs.
- Conduct a quarterly disaster-recovery drill; the next is scheduled for June 10, 2024.

All action items are tracked in Jira under project LOG-4492 with assigned owners.

## Conclusion
The March 14 incident revealed a critical gap in deployment validation and cache resilience at Apex Logistics. While manual protocols contained the operational damage, customer trust depends on faster detection and graceful degradation. The implemented fixes—canary releases, redundant replicas, and updated drills—address both technical and procedural weaknesses. The operations leadership will review progress in 30 days and publish a follow-up memo by April 15, 2024.
