# Changelog — Open Horizon Learning Foundation

## Summer 2024 Platform Release
On July 14, 2024, Open Horizon rolled out version 3.2 of the Learning Gateway to all partner districts. The update introduces adaptive lesson pathways for middle-school mathematics, developed in partnership with Dr. Elena Vasquez at the Regional Pedagogy Center. Districts in the pilot—Denver Public Schools, Riverside County Office of Education, and the Austin Independent School District—reported a 14% reduction in time-to-mastery across 1,200 students during the six-week trial.

## Accessibility and Localization
The Foundation completed WCAG 2.1 AA remediation across 38 core modules. Key improvements include:
- Full keyboard navigation for the interactive simulation suite
- Screen-reader optimized alt-text for all 240 data-visualization assets
- Spanish-language narration for grades 6–8 science units, recorded by the Mexico City studio of María Santos
- High-contrast mode and reduced-motion preferences now saved at the account level

These changes were funded by the 2023–2025 Gates Foundation Accessibility Grant and are live for all users as of August 2, 2024.

## Infrastructure and Security
To support growing enrollment—now exceeding 47,000 active learners—the engineering team migrated student data to an encrypted, regionally distributed cluster hosted in partnership with the University of Michigan’s Civic Tech Lab. The migration, finalized on June 30, 2024, reduces average page-load latency to under 400 milliseconds and achieves SOC 2 Type II compliance.

## Upcoming Initiatives
Looking ahead to the fall semester, Open Horizon will launch the MentorMatch pilot, pairing 500 high school students with volunteer tutors from the National Education Association. Training begins September 9, 2024, with full rollout targeted for October 15. The Foundation also invites feedback on the proposed open-data standard for learning analytics, with public comment open through November 1, 2024.
