# Product Requirements Document: TerraSync Carbon Ledger v2.0

## Overview
TerraSync is building the operational backbone for industrial decarbonization. This PRD defines the v2.0 release of our Carbon Ledger platform, a real-time emissions accounting engine designed for supply-chain operators across North America and the EU. The goal is to replace quarterly spreadsheet audits with continuous, audit-grade carbon telemetry.

## Objectives & Success Metrics
We will ship by March 14, 2025, with a core objective of reducing customer reporting latency from 45 days to under 6 hours. Success will be measured by adoption within the first 90 days: we need 12 enterprise clients processing at least 10,000 metric tons of CO2e per month through the ledger.

Key targets:
- Achieve 99.97% data pipeline uptime for Q1 2025
- Onboard Meridian Logistics and two other pilot partners by February 28
- Reduce manual reconciliation errors by 80% compared to v1.4

## Core Requirements
The v2.0 platform must integrate with existing ERP and IoT sensor networks without requiring on-site hardware replacements.

Functional needs:
- Real-time ingestion from satellite imagery APIs and ground-level methane sensors
- Automated Scope 1, 2, and 3 categorization using the GHG Protocol 2024 taxonomy
- Immutable audit trail with cryptographic hashing for every entry
- Role-based access control for auditors, operators, and regulators
- Export modules for EU CSRD and SEC climate disclosure formats

Non-functional requirements include sub-500ms query latency for dashboards rendering up to 5 years of monthly data.

## Data & Integration Specifications
The ingestion layer will use an event-driven architecture running on our existing Kubernetes cluster in us-east-1. We will support OAuth 2.0 and SAML 2.0 for identity, with API rate limits set to 2,000 requests per minute for standard tiers and 10,000 for enterprise contracts signed after January 1, 2025.

Data retention policies require raw telemetry storage for 7 years, with aggregated summaries kept indefinitely in cold storage. All personal data must be anonymized within 30 days of collection under our Privacy-by-Design framework.

## Timeline & Milestones
Development begins January 6, 2025. Internal alpha testing runs through February 10, followed by a closed beta with Meridian Logistics and the Port of Rotterdam consortium. General availability is targeted for March 14, 2025, with a public announcement at the Climate Tech Summit in Berlin on March 20.

Engineering sprints are organized in two-week cycles, with demo reviews every other Friday. Risk mitigation includes a fallback manual ingestion portal if automated sensor integrations fail certification by February 20.

## Risks & Open Questions
The primary risk is regulatory divergence: the EU CSRD mandates granular supplier-level disclosure by mid-2026, while the proposed SEC rules remain in litigation. We need legal confirmation by February 1 on whether to build dual-format reporting now or defer to v2.1.

Another concern is sensor calibration drift in cold climates. We should confirm with the hardware team whether our -20°C operational guarantee applies to methane detectors alone or to the full telemetry suite.

Next steps: finalize integration contracts, lock the taxonomy schema, and schedule the security audit with Dr. Elena Vasquez by January 20.
