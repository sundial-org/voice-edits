# DevSpine Seed Grant Proposal

## Executive Summary
DevSpine, founded in March 2023 by Dr. Elena Vasquez and Marcus Chen, requests $450,000 to scale our open-source dependency-health scanner. Our CLI tool, Spindle v2.1, already protects 14,000 repositories across three continents by surfacing supply-chain risks in under 400 milliseconds.

## Problem Statement
Modern CI/CD pipelines move faster than security audits. In 2024 alone, the National Vulnerability Database recorded 28,069 new CVEs affecting developer libraries. Teams at Fintech Partners and Meridian Labs reported that manual dependency reviews added an average of six engineer-hours per sprint, delaying releases without reducing risk.

## Proposed Solution
Spindle integrates directly into GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, and Bitbucket Pipelines. Rather than blocking builds with noisy alerts, it ranks vulnerabilities by exploitability within your specific call graph and suggests verified patches.

Core capabilities include:
- Sub-second scanning of monorepos exceeding 500,000 lines of code
- Automated PR comments with one-click fix commands for 92% of flagged issues
- A compliance dashboard exporting SBOMs aligned with Executive Order 14028
- Continuous monitoring via webhook streams with 99.97% uptime since January 2024

## Milestones and Budget
We will deploy production clusters in Frankfurt and Singapore by Q3 2025, hire two senior security engineers, and publish a peer-reviewed whitepaper with the University of Toronto. The $450,000 allocation splits evenly between infrastructure ($150,000), personnel ($225,000), and open-source community grants ($75,000).

## Team and Track Record
Dr. Vasquez previously led supply-chain research at the Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency. Chen built the dependency resolver adopted by Apex Financial. Together they launched Spindle in March 2023; within eighteen months the project earned 8,200 GitHub stars and sponsorship from the Linux Foundation.

## Conclusion
With this seed grant, DevSpine will transition Spindle from an open-source utility into the industry-standard shield for developer supply chains. We invite the committee to support a future where security moves at the speed of code.
