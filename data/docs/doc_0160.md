# Grant Proposal: Zero-Trust Endpoint Resilience Initiative — SentinelGrid Labs

## Executive Summary
SentinelGrid Labs requests $1.2 million over 18 months to deploy the Cortex Shield endpoint protection framework across critical infrastructure partners. Founded in 2019, our team combines kernel-level telemetry with behavioral analytics to reduce mean-time-to-detect (MTTD) by 64% in pilot environments. This proposal outlines a phased rollout beginning March 2025, targeting energy and healthcare sectors with measurable resilience outcomes.

## Technical Approach
The Cortex Shield platform operates on a zero-trust architecture that assumes breach. Rather than relying solely on signature-based detection, our engine correlates process ancestry, memory anomalies, and network egress patterns in real time.

Key capabilities include:
- Kernel driver telemetry collecting 400+ events per second with sub-10 millisecond latency
- On-device machine learning models updated via encrypted delta packages every 6 hours
- Automated containment workflows that isolate compromised endpoints within 90 seconds
- Integration APIs for Splunk, Sentinel, and Palo Alto XSIAM ecosystems

## Implementation Timeline
Phase 1 (March–June 2025) focuses on infrastructure hardening and partner onboarding at Meridian Health Systems and the Pacific Northwest Energy Cooperative. Phase 2 (July–December 2025) expands coverage to 12,000 endpoints and introduces red-team validation exercises conducted by the Cyber Defense Institute.

Milestones are tied to verifiable metrics: 99.99% telemetry uptime, 40% reduction in false positives, and full compliance with NIST 800-53 Rev. 5 controls by November 2025.

## Budget and Impact
The $1.2 million allocation divides into engineering ($640,000), partner deployment ($310,000), and independent audit ($250,000). We project a direct cost avoidance of $8.7 million for partner organizations over three years by preventing ransomware incidents and reducing incident response labor.

Dr. Elena Vasquez, Chief Scientist at SentinelGrid Labs, will serve as principal investigator, supported by a team of seven security engineers. All deliverables will be documented in open-access technical reports to advance collective defense.

## Conclusion
This grant accelerates the transition from reactive cybersecurity to predictive resilience. By investing in Cortex Shield, funders enable immediate protection for 12,000 critical endpoints while generating replicable architectures for national infrastructure security. We welcome review and discussion by January 15, 2025.
