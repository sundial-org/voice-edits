# Summit for Equitable Access: Q3 Strategic Event Plan

## Overview and Objectives
The Learning Bridge Alliance will host its annual "Summit for Equitable Access" on October 14, 2024, at the Denver Convention Center. The event targets 350 educators, district administrators, and technology partners. Our primary objective is to secure $120,000 in unrestricted funding to expand the Open Pathways digital literacy program into rural Colorado districts by January 2025.

## Venue and Logistics
Registration opens at 8:00 a.m., with the keynote beginning promptly at 9:30 a.m. in Hall B. We have contracted the Denver Convention Center for a full-day rental including three breakout rooms and a 400-seat auditorium. Catering will be provided by Mile High Hospitality, accommodating vegan, gluten-free, and standard dietary needs.

## Program Highlights
The agenda balances policy discussion with hands-on workshops:

- Dr. Elena Vasquez presenting the 2024 District Connectivity Report
- A live demonstration of the BridgeKit tablet bundle priced at $89 per unit
- Panel on federal E-Rate modernization featuring Marcus Chen from the FCC
- Afternoon design sprint for nonprofit partners

Each session runs 75 minutes with 15-minute transitions built into the schedule.

## Partnerships and Sponsorship
We have confirmed six title sponsors, including TechForward and Colorado State Library. In-kind donations cover audiovisual production and printing for 500 program guides. Sponsorship tiers range from $5,000 Community Partners to $25,000 Visionary Circle members.

## Marketing and Outreach
Promotion begins August 12, 2024, through direct email, regional education newsletters, and LinkedIn campaigns targeting K-12 technology directors. We anticipate a 22% conversion rate from our invitation list of 1,600 contacts. All marketing materials will highlight our nonprofit status and the tax-deductible nature of summit registrations.

## Budget and Success Metrics
The total event budget is $42,500, with projected revenue of $165,000 through registration and sponsorship. Success will be measured by attendee satisfaction scores above 4.5 out of 5, 50 new district partnerships signed by November 30, and full funding of the Open Pathways expansion. Post-event, the team will deliver a report to the board by October 28, 2024.
