# Robotics Integration Notes — Project AETHER

## Project Overview
The AETHER quadruped platform reached Milestone 3 on October 14. Dr. Elena Vasquez confirmed that the new hip actuator design reduces backlash by 18 percent compared to the August prototype. Our objective for Q4 is stable trotting over uneven terrain at 1.2 m/s without visual odometry failure.

## Hardware Modifications
We replaced the original 12V brushed motors with 24V BLDC units rated at 45 N·m peak torque. The carbon fiber shank extensions added 340 grams per leg, pushing total mass to 18.4 kg. To compensate, we upgraded the battery pack from 6S to 8S LiPo (22.2V nominal, 10 Ah).

- Reinforced ankle joints with titanium inserts
- Integrated IMU module (MPU-9250) at the pelvis
- Redrouted CAN bus lines away from motor heat sinks
- Added rubberized foot pads with 3 mm durometer rating
- Recalibrated joint encoders using the 4096-count protocol

## Software and Control Updates
Marcus Chen rewrote the impedance controller in C++ and deployed version 2.4.1 on November 2. The state estimator now fuses leg odometry with LIDAR point clouds at 100 Hz. We observed a 22 percent reduction in root-mean-square tracking error during the 45-minute endurance trial.

## Testing Log — November 8
The team ran ten consecutive trials on the gravel course behind Building C. Trial 7 failed due to a loose connector on the left hind encoder. After repair, Trials 8 through 10 completed successfully with average traversal speeds of 1.15 m/s, 1.21 m/s, and 1.18 m/s respectively. Temperature readings stayed below 62 °C across all actuators.

## Next Steps
We will freeze the mechanical design by November 20 and begin the long-duration stress test scheduled for December 3. Dr. Vasquez requested a full torque audit before the winter conference submission on January 10. Marcus Chen will integrate the new foot-contact sensor array by then.
