# Aurora Robotics — Sprint Changelog: March 3 – March 28

## Milestone Summary
Over the past four weeks, Team Aurora completed the integration of the Mark IV manipulator and validated the autonomous navigation stack on the simulated lunar terrain course. Lead engineer Dr. Elena Vasquez approved the final CAD release on March 14, allowing fabrication to begin at the university machine shop. Overall system readiness moved from 62% to 87%, with only the power distribution board remaining in procurement.

## Hardware Revisions
The chassis underwent a significant weight reduction after stress testing revealed fatigue cracks near the rear drive mount. We replaced the aluminum cross-brace with a carbon-fiber composite, dropping the frame mass by 1.8 kg. The new manipulator wrist assembly now supports a 14.2 kg payload without exceeding the 0.4° deflection tolerance specified in the design brief.

- Replaced aluminum cross-brace with carbon-fiber composite (–1.8 kg)
- Installed Mark IV wrist with 14.2 kg rated payload
- Added redundant IMU mount near the center of gravity
- Updated cable routing harness to prevent pinch points during 270° rotation

## Software & Autonomy
Navigation lead Marcus Chen merged the visual-SLAM branch into main on March 22. The updated pipeline processes stereo frames at 30 Hz and reduces loop-closure drift to under 0.15 m across the 400-meter test arena. The obstacle-avoidance layer now handles dynamic obstacles up to 1.2 m/s, which covers the expected rover traffic in the upcoming field trial.

## Testing & Validation
We ran twelve full-duration missions in the outdoor sand pit, achieving successful autonomous docking in ten attempts. Battery thermal performance remained within safe margins during the 48-minute endurance run on March 19, though we observed a 6% voltage sag during the final climb. The team will address this with a revised discharge profile before the competition.

## Upcoming Tasks
Fabrication of the updated drive modules should finish by April 4. Once assembled, we will begin integrated system tests with the complete sensor suite and perform final calibration of the LiDAR-to-camera extrinsics. Dr. Vasquez requested a full safety audit prior to the April 12 demonstration for the advisory board.
