# Incident Postmortem: HeliosGrid Aurora Forecast Pipeline Outage

## Summary
On 14 October 2024 at 03:42 UTC, HeliosGrid's Aurora forecasting pipeline for the Nordic solar array network failed silently after a malformed satellite ingestion job corrupted the feature store. The outage lasted 6 hours and 18 minutes, affecting 340 MW of contracted generation capacity across the Oslo and Trondheim nodes until full restoration at 10:00 UTC.

## Impact
- Forecast accuracy for the 120 MW Kongsberg solar farm dropped to zero, triggering manual curtailment protocols.
- Trading desk at HeliosGrid headquarters in Rotterdam lost real-time irradiance feeds for 14 utility partners.
- Estimated revenue exposure: €47,000 in imbalance penalties and €12,000 in emergency labor costs.

## Timeline
- 02:15 UTC: Nightly ingestion of Sentinel-3 SLSTR data completed with unvalidated null coordinates.
- 03:42 UTC: Automated ETL job wrote 8,400 corrupt records into the PostgreSQL feature store.
- 04:10 UTC: Alerting threshold breached; on-call engineer Priya Malhotra paged.
- 06:55 UTC: Rollback initiated to snapshot taken on 13 October 2024 at 22:00 UTC.
- 09:30 UTC: Validation suite confirmed restored pipeline integrity.
- 10:00 UTC: All 340 MW of affected capacity returned to automated dispatch.

## Root Cause
A missing schema guard in the v2.4.1 ingestion module allowed latitude values of -999.0 to propagate. The downstream gradient-boosting model, trained on normalized Nordic grids, interpreted these as polar anomalies and collapsed prediction variance.

## Remediation
- Patch ingestion validator and deploy v2.4.2 by 18 October 2024.
- Add automated snapshot verification every six hours.
- Introduce canary deployment for satellite data feeds.
- Conduct runbook drill with Malhotra's team before 01 November 2024.

## Lessons Learned
The silent failure mode revealed a gap between data-quality monitoring and model-output alerting. We will integrate statistical drift detectors directly into the Aurora pipeline and require dual-engineer sign-off for any feature-store schema changes.
