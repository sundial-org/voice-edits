# Internal FAQ — DevTooling Inc.

## Onboarding & Access
Every engineer gets a laptop configured by IT within 48 hours of signing. Ask Marcus Chen in Ops for the YubiKey and 1Password vault invite. If your GitHub handle is missing from the `devtooling/core` org by Wednesday, ping `#it-urgent` with your employee ID.

## Our Stack in 2024
We ship on Kubernetes 1.29, PostgreSQL 16, and Go 1.22. The CLI is written in Rust; the dashboard is Next.js 14 with TypeScript strict mode. Do not upgrade dependencies on Fridays. The last time we ignored this, the deploy pipeline broke on March 14.

## Release Cadence
We cut releases every Tuesday and Thursday at 10:00 UTC. Hotfixes need approval from either Sarah Lin or David Park. Feature flags live in Unleash; toggle names must follow the `feat_region_description` pattern.

- Production deploys require two reviewers
- Canary runs for 30 minutes before full rollout
- Rollback target is under 90 seconds
- Incident commander rotates weekly
- Post-mortems are due within 5 business days
- All logs feed into Loki with 30-day retention

## Support & Escalation
Customers file tickets through Zendesk. Severity 1 pages the on-call engineer via PagerDuty within 2 minutes. For vendor issues—AWS, Cloudflare, or Datadog—check the status page before filing an internal ticket. The 2023 outage taught us to always verify upstream first.

## Culture & Communication
We work async-first, with core hours 14:00–18:00 UTC. Standup is a Loom video, not a meeting. If you need a decision, write an RFC in Notion and tag the relevant team. No slide decks.
