# Grant Proposal: Riverside Transit Authority Zero-Emission Bus Corridor Pilot

## Executive Summary
The Riverside Transit Authority (RTA) requests $4.2 million from the Federal Transit Administration’s Low-No Emission Grant Program to deploy 12 battery-electric buses along the Central Avenue corridor by Q3 2026. This initiative will replace aging diesel units, cut annual emissions by 1,850 metric tons of CO2, and establish the region’s first fully electric high-frequency route serving 18,000 daily riders.

## Project Background
Since Mayor Elena Vasquez announced the Climate Mobility Plan in March 2024, RTA has prioritized decarbonization without reducing service. The Central Avenue corridor, spanning 8.4 miles from Union Station to Oak Hill Medical Center, currently operates with 2014-model diesel buses that exceed federal NOx thresholds. Traffic studies conducted in January 2025 show peak delays of 14 minutes per trip during morning rush, making zero-emission rapid transit both an environmental and reliability imperative.

## Proposed Implementation
RTA will execute the pilot through three integrated phases over 18 months:

- Procurement of 12 40-foot battery-electric buses from Proterra, with delivery scheduled for June 2026
- Installation of 6 overhead depot chargers and 2 en-route pantograph stations along Central Avenue
- Retraining of 34 existing operators and maintenance staff through a 240-hour certification program developed with Riverside Community College

## Budget and Timeline
The total project cost is $7.8 million. The requested federal share of $4.2 million will cover vehicle acquisition ($3.1 million) and charging infrastructure ($1.1 million). RTA will contribute $2.4 million in local match funds from the 2025 Transit Modernization Bond, while the Sacramento Air Quality Management District has committed $1.2 million for grid-upgrade support. Construction begins October 2025, with revenue service targeted for September 2026.

## Expected Outcomes
Upon completion, the corridor will eliminate 340,000 gallons of diesel consumption annually and improve on-time performance to 92 percent. The pilot will also generate open data on energy consumption and ridership patterns to inform RTA’s system-wide electrification strategy through 2030, positioning Riverside as a model for mid-sized agencies pursuing equitable clean mobility.
