# Metro Transit Weekly Status Update — October 14, 2024

## Service Adjustments
The Red Line is running on a modified weekend schedule through November 3 while crews replace aging track switches near Central Station. Peak-hour headways remain at 8 minutes, but midday service shifts to 14 minutes. Bus Route 42 resumes full service along Harbor Boulevard beginning Monday, October 21, following the completion of road resurfacing by the Department of Public Works.

## Ridership & Performance
Average weekday boardings reached 312,400 last week, up 4.2% from the same period in September. On-time performance held steady at 91.3%, with the Blue Line outperforming other corridors at 94.7%. Late-evening delays on the Green Line were primarily caused by signal maintenance near Westbrook Yard.

## Capital Projects
The Central Station accessibility renovation remains on track for its December 15 milestone. Elevators 3 and 4 are now operational, and tactile platform edging installation is 78% complete. The electric bus procurement contract with NovaBus will deliver 24 vehicles by March 2025, with pilot charging infrastructure breaking ground at the Eastside Depot on October 28.

## Safety & Operations
- All operators completed refresher training on winter weather protocols by October 11
- Station cleaning frequency increased to twice daily at the 12 highest-traffic stops
- Maintenance teams replaced 1,840 feet of third-rail cabling along the North Corridor
- Emergency response drills were conducted at Union Square and Riverside Park without disruption to service

## Community Engagement
General Manager Elena Vasquez will host a public forum at the downtown library on Wednesday, October 23, at 6:00 p.m. to discuss the 2025 fare proposal. Feedback forms are available online through October 31. The agency also partnered with Lincoln High School to launch a student ambassador program, placing 18 volunteers across major transfer hubs through the end of the semester.
