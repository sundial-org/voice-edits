# Product Requirements Document: OpenPath Literacy Platform v2.0

## Overview
The OpenPath Foundation is a nonprofit organization dedicated to closing the literacy gap for middle-school students in under-resourced districts. This document defines the requirements for version 2.0 of the OpenPath Literacy Platform, a web-based adaptive reading environment scheduled for pilot deployment on September 15, 2025.

## Goals and Success Metrics
Our primary objective is to increase student reading fluency by 25 percent within a single 12-week semester. We will measure success using the standardized NWEA MAP Reading assessment, administered at baseline, mid-point, and final evaluation. By June 2026, the platform must serve at least 4,000 active learners across partner districts in Ohio and Michigan without requiring proprietary hardware.

## Target Users and Personas
The core users are students in grades 6 through 8, teachers with limited technology support, and district literacy coordinators. Dr. Elena Vasquez, our Director of Curriculum, has emphasized that any feature requiring more than five minutes of setup will see near-zero adoption in rural classrooms.

## Core Features
The platform must include the following capabilities:
- Adaptive passage selection calibrated to each learner's Lexile measure
- Real-time teacher dashboard showing engagement minutes and mastery indicators
- Offline reading mode that synchronizes when connectivity returns
- Text-to-speech support in English and Spanish with adjustable playback speed
- Accessibility compliance meeting WCAG 2.1 AA standards

## Technical Requirements
OpenPath Literacy Platform v2.0 will be built on an open-source stack hosted on AWS Educate credits. All student data must be encrypted at rest using AES-256 and in transit via TLS 1.3. The engineering team, led by Marcus Chen, must deliver a stable beta by August 1, 2025, allowing six weeks for classroom testing before the official launch.

## Timeline and Milestones
Development begins March 3, 2025. User acceptance testing runs July 7 through July 25, 2025. The pilot launches in the Cleveland Metropolitan School District and Grand Rapids Public Schools on September 15, 2025. A full evaluation report will be published by January 30, 2026.

## Risks and Mitigations
The largest risk is inconsistent broadband access in partner schools. We will mitigate this with the offline synchronization feature and by distributing pre-loaded content on low-cost tablets. Another concern is teacher training bandwidth; we will provide asynchronous video modules capped at 15 minutes each.
