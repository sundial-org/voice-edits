# Solar Installer Lecture Notes — Residential Array Commissioning

## Safety and Lockout Protocol
Before touching any DC conductors, verify that the AC disconnect is open and the rapid shutdown initiator is engaged per NEC 690.12. Marcus Chen, our lead electrician, requires a tagged lockout on every inverter enclosure until continuity checks are complete. Ground fault protection must trip within 30 seconds of a simulated fault; if it does not, replace the GFP sensor before proceeding.

## Module Layout and Racking
We are using the IronRidge XR10 rail system across 24 panels rated at 410 W each. Maintain a 3-inch clearance from the roof surface to allow airflow and reduce cell temperature derating. On the Henderson residence (installed 14 May 2024), we lost 8% yield because the south-facing array was spaced only 1.5 inches from asphalt shingles. Always torque rail splices to 18 ft-lbs.

## Wiring and Conduit Runs
Use 10 AWG THWN-2 in 3/4-inch EMT for home-run circuits up to 80 feet. Label every junction box with the circuit number and polarity. The inverter is a SolarEdge SE7600H-US installed in the garage. Key checks:
- Verify open-circuit voltage does not exceed 480 VDC at 14 °F
- Confirm MPPT strings match the design: 11 modules per string, two strings
- Tighten MC4 connectors until the audible click; never force them in direct sunlight

## Monitoring and Handoff
Register the system in the SolarEdge monitoring portal before leaving the site. The homeowner should expect generation reports by 10:00 AM daily. Provide printed copies of the 25-year panel warranty and the 12-year inverter warranty. Schedule the utility interconnection inspection for 22 June 2024, and remind the client that net metering activation takes 7 to 10 business days after PASA approval.
