# Postmortem: MetroLine Signal Failure — March 14, 2024

## Executive Summary
On March 14, 2024, at approximately 07:42 EDT, the MetroLine Central Corridor experienced a cascading signal failure that halted northbound service between Union Station and Riverside Terminal for 94 minutes. The incident affected 12,400 morning commuters and required manual switch overrides by field technicians. Root cause was traced to a corrupted firmware update deployed to relay cabinet RC-7 during overnight maintenance.

## Timeline of Events
- 03:15 — Maintenance team applies firmware patch v2.4.1 to RC-7.
- 07:42 — First track-circuit fault reported by dispatch.
- 07:58 — Full northbound halt ordered; bus bridges activated.
- 09:16 — Service restored after rollback to v2.4.0.

## Impact Assessment
- Passengers delayed: ~12,400
- Trains affected: 18
- Revenue impact: estimated $47,000 in waived fares and overtime.
- No injuries or equipment damage were reported.

## Root Cause Analysis
The update introduced an unhandled exception in the relay logic that caused false occupancy signals. Because RC-7 controls four adjacent blocks, the error propagated within seconds. The rollback script failed silently, leaving technicians to perform manual restoration.

## Corrective Actions
- Revert all relay cabinets to firmware v2.4.0 by March 16.
- Add automated rollback verification checks before 06:00 deployments.
- Require dual-authorization for overnight patches on active corridors.
- Conduct quarterly disaster-recovery drills with Operations Center staff.

## Lessons Learned
The incident revealed gaps in our change-management pipeline. Moving forward, all firmware updates for critical signaling infrastructure must pass through the staging environment at Riverside Depot for 48 hours before production deployment. We will also publish internal runbooks by April 5, 2024.
