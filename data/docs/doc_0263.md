# Project Aether Weekly Status — 14 Oct 2024

## Hardware Integration
The chassis assembly for Aether-7 is now 94% complete. Marcus Chen finished welding the rear actuator mounts on Tuesday, and the carbon-fiber side panels arrived from AeroForm on 9 October. We still need to torque the shoulder pivot bolts to 45 N·m before running the load tests scheduled for Friday.

## Software & Controls
Elena Voss pushed the v2.4.1 firmware build to the test bed. Key updates include:
- Re-tuned PID loops for the left hip joint
- Added safety interlock for the 12 V battery rail
- Fixed the latency spike in the ROS 2 topic mapping
- Integrated the new LIDAR filter stack from Dr. Patel’s group

The navigation stack now maintains sub-5 cm drift over 100 m in the warehouse map.

## Testing & Validation
We ran three endurance cycles on Wednesday. The robot operated continuously for 2 hours and 17 minutes before thermal throttling kicked in at joint 4. Cooling ducts have been redesigned; parts go to print Thursday morning.

## Upcoming Milestones
Competition registration closes on 28 October. Before then we must:
- Complete full-system stress testing
- Certify the emergency-stop wiring with safety officer Rina Okonkwo
- Deliver the finalized bill of materials to procurement

## Issues & Blockers
The custom harmonic drive for joint 7 is delayed. The vendor, Harmonic Dynamics, pushed delivery to 21 October. We are evaluating a temporary steel-gear substitute, but that may reduce peak torque by 18%. A decision is needed by Monday’s stand-up.
