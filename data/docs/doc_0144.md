# LogiCore Q3 Operational Briefing: Lecture Notes

## Supply Chain Overview and Q3 Metrics
On August 14, 2024, LogiCore processed 42,000 TEU across the Rotterdam and Singapore hubs. Director Elena Vasquez emphasized that dwell time dropped to 2.1 days, a 14% improvement over June. The lecture highlighted how predictive routing algorithms reduced empty-mile runs by 8,300 kilometers during the quarter.

## Fleet Modernization Strategy
We are integrating 18 new electric refrigerated units into the European corridor by March 2025. Maintenance lead Marcus Chen presented lifecycle data showing a 22% reduction in fuel expenditure per route. Key priorities include:
- Retrofitting 120 existing trailers with IoT temperature sensors
- Training 45 drivers on cold-chain compliance protocols
- Deploying hydrogen pilot trucks on the Berlin–Munich lane by Q1 2025
- Updating warehouse management software to version 4.7 by October 30

## Risk Management and Regulatory Updates
The IMO 2024 sulfur cap requires adjustments to bunker scheduling. Vasquez noted that non-compliance fines reached €180,000 in July. Mitigation steps involve dual-fuel conversion and real-time emissions tracking across all 340 active vessels. Insurance partners Lloyd Maritime revised coverage terms effective September 1.

## Action Items and Next Steps
Every regional manager must submit route-optimization audits by September 15. The next lecture, scheduled for October 22, will review Q4 forecasts and pilot results from the hydrogen fleet. Staff should review the updated safety manual (revision 9.2) before attending.
