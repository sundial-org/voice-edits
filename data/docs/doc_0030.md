# Project Helios: CRISPR-Mediated Knock-in of Reporter Cassette at Locus 19q13.41

## Overview
Initiated in March 2023 by Dr. Elena Vasquez at the Meridian Biosciences Institute, Project Helios aims to generate a stable HEK293 reporter line expressing tdTomato upon activation of the endogenous p53 pathway. The target insertion site at chromosome 19q13.41 was selected after whole-genome sequencing confirmed minimal off-target homology across 14 candidate loci. All wet-lab work is conducted in BSL-2 facilities under protocol MBI-2024-117.

## Materials and Methods
- SpCas9-HF1 ribonucleoprotein complexes delivered via electroporation at 1,200 V for 20 ms
- Homology-directed repair template synthesized by GenScript with 800 bp left and right arms
- Single-cell sorting performed on the BD FACSAria III at 48 hours post-transfection
- Clone expansion in DMEM supplemented with 10% FBS and 1 µg/mL puromycin selection

## Results
Out of 312 single-cell clones screened, 23 exhibited correct integration confirmed by junction PCR and Sanger sequencing. Clone 7F showed the brightest and most uniform tdTomato fluorescence with a Z-score of 4.8 relative to wild-type controls. Quantitative flow cytometry on 14 September 2024 revealed 94.2% reporter-positive cells after 5 µM doxorubicin induction for 24 hours.

## Timeline and Milestones
The first successful knock-in was verified on 22 June 2023. Scale-up to 10-liter bioreactor runs is scheduled for Q1 2025, with a target yield of 2.4 × 10^9 viable cells per batch. Data packages for regulatory pre-submission will be finalized by Dr. Vasquez and the quality team before 30 November 2025.
