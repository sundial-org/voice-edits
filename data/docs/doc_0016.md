# Transit Authority Research Notes — Q3 Service Optimization Review

## Executive Summary
Prepared for the MetroLink Planning Division on October 14, 2024. This document consolidates preliminary findings from the summer pilot conducted across the Riverdale and Easton corridors. Lead analyst Dr. Elena Vasquez coordinated data collection with field supervisors Marcus Chen and Aisha Okonkwo.

## Route Performance Metrics
The pilot monitored three adjusted lines over a 90-day window from June 1 through August 30, 2024.

- Route 42B (Riverdale Express): average headway improved to 8.4 minutes, down from 12 minutes
- Route 17 (Easton Connector): ridership grew 14 percent, reaching 9,200 weekday boardings
- Route 88 (Northgate Local): on-time performance stalled at 71 percent despite schedule padding

## Operational Constraints
Driver availability remains the primary bottleneck. The agency currently operates with 312 certified operators against a budgeted complement of 345. Overtime expenditure for August exceeded $480,000, a 22 percent increase over July figures.

Maintenance backlogs at the Central Depot have delayed the rollout of 18 low-floor vehicles originally slated for September deployment.

## Rider Feedback Integration
Survey responses from 2,104 passengers highlighted recurring concerns:

- Insufficient shelter lighting at the Meridian and 4th Street transfer points
- Inconsistent real-time arrival displays on the mobile application
- Overcrowding on Route 42B between 7:15 a.m. and 8:30 a.m.

Focus groups conducted on September 3 and September 10 emphasized accessibility needs, particularly for passengers using mobility devices on the Easton Connector.

## Next Steps and Recommendations
Staff recommend extending the pilot framework through November 30 to capture fall semester patterns. Dr. Vasquez will present cost projections to the Board of Directors on October 28. Interim adjustments should prioritize operator recruitment and the accelerated delivery of the 18 delayed vehicles before winter weather impacts reliability.
