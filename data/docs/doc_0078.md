# Grant Proposal: Scaling Direct-Air Capture at Helios Carbon Labs

## Executive Summary
Helios Carbon Labs seeks $4.2 million in Phase-II funding to deploy our modular direct-air capture (DAC) system, codenamed **Stratos-9**, across three pilot sites in Nevada by Q3 2026. Founded in 2019 by Dr. Elena Vasquez and James Park, our team has demonstrated 340 metric tons of CO₂ removal in laboratory conditions. This proposal outlines how we will transition from prototype to commercial validation.

## Technology & Innovation
The Stratos-9 unit uses a proprietary solid-sorbent matrix that reduces regeneration energy by 42 percent compared to liquid-amine systems. Each module occupies 12 square meters and captures 2.1 tons of CO₂ per day at 85 percent capacity factor. Our innovation lies in integrating waste heat from adjacent geothermal plants, lowering operational costs to $94 per ton—well below the $180 industry benchmark.

Key technical milestones include:
- Completion of third-party verification by DNV in March 2025
- Pilot installation at the Reno Geothermal Facility by August 2025
- Real-time telemetry dashboard launched in October 2024
- Scale-up partnership with Nevada Clean Energy Consortium signed January 2025

## Financial Plan & Impact
We request $4,200,000 over 24 months. Funds will cover manufacturing of 14 Stratos-9 modules, site engineering, and 18 months of monitoring. Projected outcomes include 10,500 tons of verified carbon removal by December 2026 and creation of 22 specialized jobs in Washoe County.

## Team & Partnerships
Dr. Vasquez leads sorbent chemistry with 17 patents. James Park, former engineer at Tesla Energy, directs systems integration. Our advisory board includes Professor Li Wei from Stanford University and regulatory expert Maria Gonzalez, who previously directed EPA Region IX carbon programs.

## Conclusion
With demonstrated efficiency, strategic partnerships, and a clear path to $94-per-ton economics, Helios Carbon Labs is positioned to deliver measurable climate impact by 2026. We invite the Climate Innovation Fund to partner in scaling this critical technology.
