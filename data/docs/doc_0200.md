# EdTech Forward: 2025 Impact Summit & Launch Event Plan

## Event Overview and Mission
The Open Horizon Foundation, an edtech nonprofit serving under-resourced districts, will host its annual Impact Summit on March 14, 2025, at the Civic Arts Center in Denver. The gathering convenes educators, technologists, and funders to unveil our new open-source literacy platform, ReadBridge 2.0. With a projected attendance of 340 guests, the event blends keynote programming, live classroom demonstrations, and structured networking to accelerate equitable access to adaptive learning tools.

## Venue, Date, and Logistics
The one-day summit runs from 8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. on Friday, March 14, 2025. The Civic Arts Center offers 12 breakout rooms, an auditorium seating 500, and complimentary parking for 180 vehicles. Registration opens January 20, 2025, through the Foundation portal, with early-bird pricing available until February 10. Catering will accommodate 340 plated lunches prepared by local vendor Green Plate Co-op, and all sessions will be live-captioned and archived for remote access.

## Program Highlights
Dr. Amara Okonkwo, our Chief Learning Officer, will deliver the morning keynote on closing the digital divide. The afternoon features hands-on labs where teachers pilot ReadBridge modules in simulated classrooms. A panel moderated by journalist Leo Park will address funding sustainability with representatives from the Gates Learning Fund and the Colorado Department of Education.

- ReadBridge 2.0 product demo at 11:15 a.m. in Hall B
- Youth educator panel at 1:30 p.m. featuring three district principals
- Networking reception with 25 partner organizations from 3:45 p.m. to 4:00 p.m.
- Accessible shuttle service running every 20 minutes from Union Station

## Partnerships and Sponsorship
This year’s presenting sponsor is the Meridian Technology Foundation, contributing $75,000 toward venue costs and scholarship travel for 40 rural teachers. Secondary support comes from BrightPath Devices, which will donate 120 refurbished tablets for our pilot classrooms. We are also partnering with Denver Public Media to broadcast the opening address to an estimated audience of 8,000 remote viewers.

## Marketing, Outreach, and Metrics
Our communications team will execute a six-week campaign beginning February 1, 2025. The strategy emphasizes direct outreach to 1,200 district technology coordinators, social promotion through educator networks, and press releases to regional education desks. We aim for 280 in-person registrations by March 1, with 85 percent of attendees representing Title I schools. Post-event surveys will measure partner engagement and platform adoption rates within 90 days.

## Budget and Risk Management
The total event budget is $142,000, with 60 percent covered by sponsorship and 40 percent by Foundation reserves. Contingency allocations include $8,000 for weather-related travel disruptions and $3,500 for last-minute AV upgrades. Security and health protocols will align with Civic Arts Center guidelines, including on-site first aid staff and clear accessibility signage throughout the venue.
