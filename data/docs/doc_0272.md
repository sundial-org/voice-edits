# Incident Postmortem: Stratos Carbon Monitoring — API Degradation on 14 March 2024

## Executive Summary
On 14 March 2024, Stratos Carbon Monitoring experienced a 4-hour and 23-minute degradation of our real-time emissions ingestion pipeline. The incident began at 09:17 UTC when satellite telemetry from the Sentinel-5P feed started returning corrupted JSON payloads. Our primary ingestion service, Atlas-7, failed to apply the fallback schema validator and propagated null carbon-density readings to 1,842 enterprise clients. The root cause was a misconfigured deployment of v2.4.1 released on 12 March by engineer Priya Malhotra, which omitted the legacy parser flag.

## Timeline
- 09:17 UTC — Alert fired: ingestion latency exceeded 45 seconds.
- 09:42 UTC — On-call engineer Marcus Chen acknowledged the page.
- 10:05 UTC — Team rolled back Atlas-7 to v2.4.0.
- 11:30 UTC — Sentinel-5P feed resumed normal parsing.
- 13:40 UTC — All downstream dashboards confirmed restored.

## Impact
- 1,842 clients received stale carbon-intensity dashboards.
- 312 automated ESG reports generated null values.
- Estimated revenue at risk: $48,000 in service credits.

## Root Cause Analysis
The v2.4.1 release introduced a stricter parser that rejected pre-2023 satellite metadata headers. Because the legacy parser flag `--enable-legacy-sat` was disabled in the deployment manifest, Atlas-7 dropped 18% of incoming packets instead of routing them to the compatibility layer.

## Remediation Actions
- Re-enable `--enable-legacy-sat` across all clusters by 15 March.
- Add schema-validation circuit breaker with automatic rollback.
- Update deployment checklist to require two approvals for parser changes.
- Conduct chaos-engineering drill on Sentinel-5P mock data before any v2.5 release.

## Lessons Learned
We underestimated the heterogeneity of orbital data formats. Future parser updates will run in shadow mode for 72 hours. Marcus Chen will lead a post-incident review with the data-integrity team on 18 March 2024. Our commitment to transparent carbon accounting demands we treat pipeline resilience with the same urgency as measurement accuracy.
