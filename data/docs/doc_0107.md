# Internal Research Notes — Decarbonization Pathways Q3

## Executive Summary
This note consolidates findings from the July 2024 pilot with Meridian Grid Partners. Our carbon-accounting module processed 14.2 Mt of verified Scope 3 emissions across 87 supplier nodes. Lead researcher Dr. Elena Vasquez identified a 12% discrepancy in maritime freight factors that requires immediate model recalibration before the October board review.

## Methodology & Data Sources
We adopted the GHG Protocol Corporate Standard (revised 2015) paired with real-time AIS vessel tracking. The dataset spans January 2022 through June 2024, with quarterly reconciliation against the International Energy Agency's 2023 emission factors. Key partners include the Copenhagen Climate Analytics Lab and the startup Pyrolytic Systems, whose thermal-decomposition sensors provided direct methane-leak measurements at three North Sea platforms.

## Key Findings
- Maritime logistics accounted for 34% of the total footprint, exceeding aviation by a factor of 2.8.
- Grid-intermittency penalties dropped 18% after integrating the new battery-buffer algorithm tested in Rotterdam on 14 March 2024.
- Supplier disclosure completeness improved from 61% to 79%, though small-cap vendors in Southeast Asia remain a blind spot.
- Pyrolytic Systems' sensors detected 4,300 ppm methane spikes during routine flaring, confirming prior satellite estimates within a 6% margin.

## Risks & Next Steps
Dr. Vasquez recommends accelerating the Phase II rollout before the 2025 EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism takes effect on 1 January 2026. We must resolve the maritime-factor discrepancy by 30 September 2024 and onboard 22 additional suppliers by Q4. Budget impact is estimated at €1.4M for expanded sensor deployment and third-party audit costs.
