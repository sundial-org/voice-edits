# Internal Logistics Research Notes — Q3 Routing & Capacity Review

## Executive Summary
This document summarizes findings from the operational audit conducted at our Midwest Distribution Hub in Columbus, Ohio. Lead researcher Marcus Delgado compiled data between August 12 and September 3, 2024, focusing on cross-dock efficiency, fuel expenditure patterns, and carrier contract renegotiations with Apex Freight Partners.

## Route Optimization Findings
Over the past quarter, average dwell time at the Columbus facility increased to 14.6 hours, up from 9.2 hours in Q2. The primary bottleneck remains the inbound receiving bay, which processes approximately 380 pallets per shift with only three active forklifts.

Key adjustments under evaluation:
- Reallocate two forklifts from outbound staging to receiving during the 06:00–14:00 window
- Implement dynamic slotting for high-velocity SKUs handled by Apex Freight Partners
- Pilot a pre-sorted trailer program with carriers arriving before 10:00 AM

## Carrier Performance Metrics
Apex Freight Partners delivered 97.4% on-time performance in August, exceeding the contractual threshold of 95%. However, their per-mile rate rose by $0.18 following the September 1 fuel surcharge amendment. Secondary carrier Meridian Logistics maintained 91% reliability but offers a 12% discount on backhaul lanes between Indianapolis and Detroit.

## Cost Impact Projections
If current trends hold, the Columbus hub will exceed its Q3 fuel budget of $428,000 by an estimated $63,500. Shifting 30% of regional volume to Meridian Logistics on qualified backhaul routes could recover roughly $18,000 monthly, though this requires renegotiating insurance liability clauses before October 15.

## Next Steps
Marcus Delgado will present the revised slotting map and carrier split proposal to the Operations Committee on September 22. A decision on the Meridian Logistics pilot must be finalized by September 30 to apply Q4 pricing tiers.
