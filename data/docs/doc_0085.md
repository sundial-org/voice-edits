# Transit Agency Internal Research Notes: Q2 Service Optimization & BRT Pilot Evaluation

## Executive Summary
Prepared by Dr. Elena Vasquez for the Metro Transit Authority on June 14, 2024. This memo synthesizes ridership trends, operational adjustments implemented in March 2024, and preliminary findings from the Rivertown Bus Rapid Transit (BRT) pilot launched in January.

## Ridership Recovery Metrics
Since the baseline established in November 2023, system-wide weekday boardings have climbed from 412,000 to approximately 489,000—a 18.7 percent increase. Weekend usage remains softer, averaging 198,000 trips, but represents a notable rebound from the 164,000 recorded during the same period last year. Notably, the Blue Line corridor accounts for nearly 31 percent of total revenue hours despite serving only 22 percent of routes.

- Blue Line: +24% ridership, peak headways reduced to 6 minutes
- Green Line: +12% ridership, signal priority activated at 14 intersections
- Orange Line: -3% ridership, under review for rerouting near Central Station

## Rivertown BRT Pilot Findings
The 4.2-mile Rivertown corridor pilot, operating since January 8, 2024, utilizes dedicated lanes between Harbor Street and Westfield Mall. Average travel time along the corridor dropped from 34 minutes to 19 minutes during peak periods. Farebox recovery on the pilot route currently stands at 42 percent, compared to 28 percent for comparable local bus service.

Driver feedback from the 22 operators assigned to the pilot highlights improved schedule reliability but also increased stress near the unprotected left-turn at 5th Avenue and Market Street.

## Operational Constraints & Budget Outlook
The Board approved $14.3 million in supplemental funding on May 22, 2024, allowing the agency to extend pilot service through December. However, procurement delays for the 18 articulated buses ordered from NovaTransit Manufacturing may push full fleet deployment into Q1 2025. Maintenance staff report that brake wear on pilot vehicles exceeds projections by 11 percent, likely due to frequent stop-and-go conditions in mixed-traffic segments.

## Recommendations
Extend the Rivertown pilot through fiscal year 2025, contingent on addressing the 5th Avenue intersection conflict. Prioritize signal synchronization upgrades along the Blue Line before expanding BRT branding. Dr. Vasquez and the Planning Division will present detailed cost-benefit modeling to the Board at the July 9 hearing.
