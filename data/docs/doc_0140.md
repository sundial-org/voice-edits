# StackWeave SBIR Phase I Proposal: Deterministic Dependency Resolution for Polyglot Build Systems

## Executive Summary

StackWeave, founded in 2023 by Dr. Elena Vasquez, requests $256,000 in NSF SBIR Phase I funding to commercialize a deterministic dependency-resolution engine for polyglot build systems. Modern repositories containing Rust, Python, and Go modules suffer from non-reproducible builds caused by floating lockfiles and transitive version drift. Our prototype resolves full dependency graphs in under 120 milliseconds with 100% reproducibility across 1,200 tested repositories. This grant will validate our algorithm against enterprise-scale monorepos and prepare a Phase II commercialization path by Q3 2025.

## Technical Approach and Innovation

Current package managers—Cargo, Pip, and Go Modules—treat each language ecosystem as an isolated graph. StackWeave introduces a unified constraint-solver that models cross-language dependencies as a single weighted hypergraph. Key innovations include:

- A deterministic SAT solver that eliminates floating lockfile conflicts without manual intervention
- A cross-ecosystem vulnerability scanner integrated at resolution time, reducing mean-time-to-patch by 40%
- A lightweight proxy cache that reduces redundant registry fetches by 65% in CI pipelines

We will benchmark the engine against repositories from GitHub’s top 500 open-source projects, measuring build-time variance and binary reproducibility.

## Market Opportunity and Impact

The developer-tools market exceeded $8.2 billion in 2024, yet build-system reliability remains a top-three productivity blocker in Stack Overflow’s annual survey. Enterprise customers at two pilot partners—Meridian Labs and Orbit Commerce—have committed to Phase II evaluation letters if Phase I demonstrates sub-second resolution on codebases exceeding 50,000 modules.

By March 2025, we will release an open-source CLI and a hosted enterprise tier priced per seat. Revenue projections indicate $1.4M ARR by Q4 2026, with 70% gross margins.

## Milestones and Budget

Funding will be allocated over 12 months beginning January 2025. Milestones include algorithm validation (Months 1–4), pilot integration (Months 5–8), and open-source release preparation (Months 9–12). The $256,000 budget covers two senior engineers, cloud benchmarking infrastructure, and legal costs for enterprise licensing frameworks.

We seek NSF partnership not only for capital but for credibility within the federal procurement pipeline. StackWeave’s technology aligns directly with NIST secure-software supply-chain directives issued in 2024. With this support, we will replace fragile build scripts with deterministic, auditable infrastructure.
