# Welcome to Ember & Oak Roastery

Welcome to the team. Ember & Oak was founded in 2014 by head roaster Marisol Vega, and since then we have focused on traceable, small-batch coffees from the Pacific Northwest and Central America. This guide covers the essentials you need for your first thirty days, from warehouse safety to cup quality.

## First Day Logistics

On your first morning, arrive at the loading dock on 7th and Alder by 6:45 a.m. Meet warehouse lead Derek Hahn for your badge and steel-toe boot fitting. You will receive two sets of heat-resistant gloves, a copy of our 2023 safety manual, and a locker assignment in the north corridor. Do not enter the roasting floor without signing the daily temperature log.

## Safety and Roasting Protocols

The Probat P25 roasters run at internal temperatures exceeding 425°F. Always wear your long-sleeve apron and eye protection when dropping batches. Before each roast, check that the afterburner is clear and the chaff collector is locked. If you smell gas or hear an irregular drum rattle, hit the red emergency stop and alert Marisol immediately.

## Quality Control Standards

Every batch is cupped within 24 hours of cooling. We use a standard 84-point SCA scale, and any lot scoring below 82 requires a second evaluation by Derek. Your daily tasks include:

- Weighing green coffee to the nearest gram on the Acaia Lunar
- Logging moisture readings with the Wile 55 meter before roasting
- Recording first crack times to within 5 seconds
- Labeling burlap storage bins with the origin, date, and roast profile ID

## Team Culture and Growth

We hold a staff cupping every Thursday at 9:00 a.m. sharp. Bring your own spoon, but leave opinions at the door—this is about calibration, not competition. After 90 days, you will be eligible for the Q Grader prep course, which Marisol teaches in-house each March and September. We believe precision comes from repetition, but excellence comes from curiosity.

Thank you for joining Ember & Oak. Respect the bean, watch the flame, and ask questions before the drum starts.
