# Grant Proposal: Green Last-Mile Optimization Initiative

## Executive Summary
Meridian Freight Solutions seeks $1.2 million from the Department of Transportation Sustainable Logistics Program to modernize our Indianapolis distribution hub. Founded in 1998, Meridian operates a 340-vehicle fleet across the Midwest, handling approximately 18,000 deliveries weekly. This proposal outlines a 24-month pilot to reduce carbon emissions by 30 percent through electric cargo bikes, AI-driven route consolidation, and solar-powered micro-fulfillment centers.

## Project Background
Urban congestion and rising fuel costs have eroded our last-mile margins by 14 percent since 2021. Customers such as Brightline Retail and Carter Health Systems now demand carbon-neutral delivery options by Q3 2025. Our current diesel van fleet averages 9.4 miles per gallon in downtown Indianapolis corridors, generating 420 metric tons of CO2 annually within the 46204 ZIP code alone.

## Implementation Strategy
We will deploy resources in three coordinated phases:

- Phase 1 (Months 1-8): Install 120 kW solar arrays and charging infrastructure at the Meridian Indianapolis Hub on Kentucky Avenue
- Phase 2 (Months 6-14): Integrate 45 electric cargo bikes and deploy the Meridian SmartRoute algorithm developed with Purdue University
- Phase 3 (Months 15-24): Launch two solar micro-fulfillment centers in Broad Ripple and Fountain Square, reducing average delivery distance from 6.2 miles to 2.1 miles

## Expected Outcomes
By December 2026, we project a 30 percent reduction in last-mile emissions, saving 126 metric tons of CO2 per year. The initiative will create 22 technical and logistics positions, with 60 percent of hires drawn from the Near Eastside workforce development pipeline. Operational cost savings are estimated at $340,000 annually through reduced fuel and maintenance expenditures.

## Budget and Timeline
The total project cost is $1.85 million, with $1.2 million requested in federal grant support and $650,000 in matching funds from Meridian Freight Solutions and the Indianapolis Chamber of Commerce. Key milestones include solar installation completion by August 2025, full e-cargo bike deployment by February 2026, and pilot evaluation reporting submitted by January 2027.

## Conclusion
This grant represents a strategic investment in resilient, low-carbon urban logistics. We respectfully request the committee's approval to begin Phase 1 on January 15, 2025.
