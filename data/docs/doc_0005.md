# Welcome to the Orion Observatory Club

Welcome to the Orion Observatory Club, founded in 1987 and now the region's largest amateur astronomy network. Whether you are borrowing our 11-inch Celestron Schmidt-Cassegrain or joining a dark-sky expedition to Death Valley, this guide will help you navigate your first semester safely and productively.

## Membership and Dues

Annual dues are $45 for students and $75 for general members. Your card unlocks the dome every Tuesday and Thursday after 7:00 PM. Please register your vehicle with Dr. Elena Vasquez, our facilities coordinator, so we can keep the parking lot clear on observation nights.

## Equipment Protocol

We maintain shared instruments in Bay Three. Before using any telescope, complete the 30-minute checkout with a certified keyholder. Key rules include:

- Always cover optics when not in use to prevent dew accumulation
- Log every session in the red binder near the control panel
- Never force the mount; if tracking stalls, call Marcus Chen on the duty radio
- Return eyepieces to the foam-lined case in ascending focal-length order

## Observation Schedule

Our public star parties occur on the first and third Saturdays of each month, regardless of weather. The autumn 2024 calendar highlights a Perseid meteor watch on August 12 and a lunar eclipse viewing on September 18. Private group bookings are available on Wednesdays by emailing orionclub@astro.edu with a 48-hour notice.

## Safety and Etiquette

Red light only after 9:00 PM. No white flashlights, phone screens, or laser pointers aimed near the dome. If you bring guests, limit the group to four people and sign the liability waiver at the gate. Respect the quiet zone within 20 feet of active scopes.

## Getting Involved

New members are encouraged to join one of three working groups: astrophotography, public outreach, or telescope maintenance. Attend the orientation on October 5 to meet committee leads and claim your first volunteer shift. We look forward to exploring the sky together.
