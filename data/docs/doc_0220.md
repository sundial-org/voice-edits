# Internal Solar Installer FAQ — Field & Office Reference

## Safety & Site Access Protocols
All crews must complete the updated fall-protection certification before stepping onto any residential roof. As of March 14, 2025, harness inspection logs are required at every job site, with copies uploaded to the Helios Portal within 24 hours. If a homeowner denies ladder access after 9:00 a.m., reschedule through dispatch rather than attempting alternate entry points. Remember that the 2023 incident on Linden Street remains the benchmark for why we never bypass lockout procedures on live panels.

## System Sizing & Component Rules
Installers should reference the 2024 equipment matrix when pairing inverters with battery banks. For arrays exceeding 12 kW, use the Enphase IQ8HC units exclusively—no substitutions without approval from Marcus Chen, Lead Engineer. Typical residential timelines run 6 to 8 weeks from permit approval to final inspection, though jurisdictions like Riverside County currently average 11 weeks due to backlog. Always verify racking compatibility against the updated aluminum rail spec released in January.

## Warranty, Service, and Customer Handoff
- Confirm the 25-year performance warranty is activated in the manufacturer portal before leaving site
- Provide the homeowner with the emergency disconnect card labeled with the installation date and crew lead name
- Photograph the final meter configuration and upload it to folder 442-B within one business day
- Note any shade analysis discrepancies in the post-install survey; these affect the 10-year workmanship guarantee

## Common Field Issues
If voltage drop exceeds 2 percent on a run longer than 150 feet, upsize the conductor per NEC table 310.16 rather than accepting the loss. Microinverter failures should be swapped on-site only when spare inventory exceeds three units; otherwise, schedule a return visit with the parts coordinator, Dana Reeves. Never leave conduit exposed overnight—weather sealing is mandatory regardless of forecast conditions.
