# Lecture Notes: Scaling Access Through Open Curriculum — Fall Seminar

## Context and Mission

Founded in 2014, the OpenPath Initiative operates as a nonprofit edtech organization dedicated to closing the digital divide in secondary education. Our 2023 Impact Report documented partnerships with 142 schools across 18 countries, reaching approximately 34,000 students who previously lacked structured computer science pathways. These notes summarize the October 14, 2023 seminar led by Dr. Amara Okonkwo, Executive Director, on sustainable curriculum design.

## Core Design Principles

Dr. Okonkwo emphasized that technology alone does not produce equity. The initiative follows three guiding rules for every module released under the Creative Commons license:

- Prioritize low-bandwidth compatibility for regions with intermittent connectivity
- Build assessments in local languages alongside English master copies
- Require open peer review from at least two educators in different hemispheres before publication

These safeguards have reduced content revision cycles by 40 percent since their adoption in early 2022.

## Funding and Sustainability Metrics

Nonprofit edtech must balance mission fidelity with operational reality. The initiative's 2024 operating budget of $2.8 million derives from a blended model: 55 percent individual donors, 30 percent institutional grants, and 15 percent corporate partnerships. Notably, the March 2024 agreement with the Meridian Foundation secured five years of unrestricted funding, allowing the team to hire 12 regional curriculum coordinators rather than relying on volunteer translations alone.

## Implementation Case Study

In rural Kenya, Pilot Program 7 launched in January 2024 across 9 secondary schools. Teachers received 40 hours of synchronous training followed by asynchronous mentoring through a moderated forum. By June 2024, student completion rates for the introductory module reached 78 percent, compared to 41 percent in control districts using proprietary textbooks. The data suggest that open licensing combined with localized support outperforms one-size-fits-all software subscriptions.

## Next Steps for Educators

Participants are encouraged to adapt the OpenPath framework within their own institutions by January 2025. Key actions include auditing existing digital resources for accessibility barriers, joining the global review network, and submitting pilot data to the annual transparency repository maintained by the nonprofit's research partner at the University of Edinburgh. Sustainable impact depends not on scaling quickly, but on scaling with care.
