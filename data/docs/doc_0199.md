# Solar Installer Onboarding Guide

Welcome to Helios Field Services. This guide outlines the safety protocols, installation workflows, and reporting standards you must follow from your first day on the job site. New technicians complete a 40-hour orientation before working unsupervised.

## Safety and PPE Requirements

Every crew member reports to Site Supervisor Marcus Chen by 6:30 AM for the daily JSA briefing. You must wear a Class E hard hat, arc-rated gloves, and steel-toe boots rated for 75 lbs of impact resistance. Never approach an energized inverter until lockout-tagout is verified by two qualified persons.

- Inspect fall-protection harnesses for fraying before ascending
- Test DC disconnect switches with a CAT III multimeter
- Maintain a 10-foot clearance from live busbars at all times
- Confirm weather conditions; work stops if sustained winds exceed 25 mph

## Installation Workflow

Standard residential arrays follow the March 15, 2024 revised layout spec. Begin with racking alignment on the south-facing plane, then secure modules using the 4-bolt clamping pattern specified by SunRail. All conduit runs must stay within 30 feet of the inverter location to limit voltage drop.

Grounding conductors require continuous green insulation and terminate at the UL-listed ground bus. After module placement, torque every bolt to 180 in-lbs using a calibrated driver. Photograph each completed row for the digital quality log.

## Documentation and Closeout

At the end of shift, upload inspection photos to the Helios portal and complete Form SF-12. Report any damaged panels to Warehouse Lead Dana Reeves within 4 hours so replacement stock can be reserved. Your probationary review occurs after 90 days, with full certification awarded upon passing the NABCEP practical exam.
