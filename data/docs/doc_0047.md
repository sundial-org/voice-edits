# Biotech Lab Lecture Notes: CRISPR-Cas9 Knockout Validation

## Overview and Protocol Background
On March 14, 2024, Dr. Elena Vasquez introduced the revised validation pipeline for our HEK293T knockout lines. The objective of this module is to confirm biallelic frameshifts in the TRAC locus using a combination of T7 endonuclease I digestion and targeted Sanger sequencing. All work must be performed in BSL-2 hoods with approved PPE, and cell lines should be maintained below passage 25 to preserve karyotypic stability.

## Materials and Reagent Preparation
Prepare master mixes on ice using nuclease-free water. The T7E1 reaction requires 200 ng of purified PCR amplicon, 1 µL of NEBuffer 2, and 0.5 µL of T7 Endonuclease I (NEB #M0302). For sequencing submissions, dilute purified products to 10 ng/µL in 10 µL volumes. Remember that the guide RNA sequence targeting exon 2 is 5'-GAGUUCAGUCUGCUGAGCAG-3', and the positive control plasmid is pX330-v2.

## Experimental Steps
- Harvest 1.5 × 10^6 cells per replicate 72 hours post-transfection using 0.25% trypsin-EDTA.
- Extract genomic DNA with the DNeasy Blood & Tissue Kit (Qiagen #69504), eluting in 50 µL AE buffer.
- Amplify the 742 bp target region using Phusion Hot Start Flex DNA Polymerase with an annealing temperature of 64 °C.
- Perform heteroduplex formation: heat to 95 °C for 5 minutes, then cool to 85 °C at -2 °C/sec, followed by 25 °C at -0.1 °C/sec.
- Digest with T7E1 at 37 °C for exactly 45 minutes, then quench with 1.5 µL of 0.5 M EDTA.

## Data Interpretation and Next Steps
A successful knockout should yield cleavage products at approximately 412 bp and 330 bp, indicating a heteroduplex formed by insertion or deletion mutations. If the digestion pattern remains wild-type, repeat the transfection with a 1:3 Cas9:gRNA ratio and increase the puromycin selection window to 96 hours. Submit all ambiguous clones for deep sequencing through the Genomics Core by April 3, 2024. Document all observations in the shared electronic notebook and flag any mycoplasma-positive cultures to the biosafety officer immediately.
