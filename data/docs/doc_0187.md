# Helios Ridge Solar — Commercial Installation Playbook

## Project Overview
This repository documents the 2024 deployment of a 2.4 MW rooftop photovoltaic array at the Meridian Logistics Hub in Denver, Colorado. Lead installer Aurora Volt Systems contracted the build in March 2024, with full interconnection achieved by October 14, 2024. The system comprises 6,840 monocrystalline panels across three warehouse roofs, feeding two Solis 1.2 MW inverters tied to a 34.5 kV utility transformer.

## Technical Scope
- Site survey and structural load verification completed by Kessler Engineering
- 480 V AC three-phase distribution with 800 A busway
- Real-time monitoring via Aurora Power-One gateway and Modbus TCP integration
- 25-year production warranty and 10-year workmanship guarantee

## Installation Timeline
Phase 1 ran from April 2 through June 18, covering racking and DC cabling. Phase 2, from July 9 to September 22, handled inverter installation, commissioning, and utility witness testing. Final PTO was granted on October 14, 2024, after Xcel Energy completed the interconnection study.

## Safety and Compliance
All crews maintained OSHA 30 certification. Daily JHA briefings were required, and fall protection was enforced at heights above 6 feet. Lockout/tagout protocols followed NFPA 70E, and arc-flash labeling was applied to every 480 V enclosure.

## Maintenance Notes
Quarterly thermal imaging is scheduled for March, June, September, and December. Inverter firmware updates are pushed remotely through the Aurora portal. Panel cleaning is recommended twice yearly, ideally after pollen season in late May and before winter freeze in early November.
