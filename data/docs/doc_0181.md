# Collective Frequency: Lecture Notes — Session 04

## Signal Flow in the Hybrid Room

On March 14, 2024, the collective reconvened at the Eastside Warehouse to map our updated routing architecture. Producer Mira Okonkwo demonstrated how a 16-channel analog summing mixer feeds directly into the Avid HDX system without introducing more than 0.02 ms of round-trip latency. We agreed that maintaining this transparent path is essential when tracking live drums alongside synthesized bass lines generated from modular rigs.

## Microphone Choices and Placement

Engineer Theo Brennan brought three specific capsules to compare: the Neumann U 87 Ai, the Shure SM7B, and a vintage AKG C414 EB from 1986. Each microphone was positioned at 18 inches from the source, angled 30 degrees off-axis to reduce sibilance while preserving the chest resonance we need for the upcoming EP. Brennan emphasized that distance is not arbitrary; it is a compositional decision.

## The 48-Hour Rule

We adopted a new collective policy effective April 2, 2025. All raw multitrack sessions must be backed up to two separate LTO-9 tapes and one cloud vault within 48 hours of recording. The 48-hour rule applies regardless of session length, whether we captured 12 minutes or 120 minutes of material. Failure to archive within this window triggers a peer review at the next monthly meeting.

## Monitoring and Translation

- Calibrate all nearfields to 83 dB SPL using pink noise
- Reference completed mixes on Auratone 5C cubes and consumer earbuds
- Document every EQ adjustment with a timestamp in the session log
- Verify low-end coherence with a real-time analyzer running at 1/48 octave resolution
- Schedule a 15-minute listening break every 90 minutes to reset auditory fatigue
