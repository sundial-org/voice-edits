# Solar Installation Lecture Notes — Q3 Field Update

## Site Assessment Protocols
Before loading the racking, verify roof age and structural load paths. For asphalt shingles older than 15 years, advise replacement prior to array mounting. Use the Solmetric SunEye at 10:00 a.m. local solar time to capture accurate azimuth and tilt data. Record measurements in the project folder named after the lead tech, e.g., Martinez-2847.

## Racking and Flashing
Unirac RM5 rails remain our standard for residential pitches between 15 and 45 degrees. Maintain 4-inch setbacks from ridge and eaves per NEC 2023 Article 690. Maintain consistent 72-inch rail spans unless engineering stamps require 60-inch intervals on 40 psf snow zones.

## Electrical Integration
- Confirm inverter firmware is updated to version 7.4.2 before commissioning
- Label all DC homeruns with UV-resistant tags showing circuit 1 through 8
- Torque lug connections to 35 in-lb using a calibrated torque wrench
- Ground the array frame with continuous 6 AWG bare copper bonded to two ground rods

## Safety and Compliance
OSHA 1926.501 requires 100% tie-off above 6 feet. All crew must complete the NABCEP refresher by August 14, 2025. Inspect harnesses for fraying and check expiration dates stamped on the dorsal D-ring.

## Commissioning Checklist
Perform IV curve tracing with the Fluke 393 FC on circuits 3, 5, and 7. Compare Voc and Isc against the module datasheet within ±3%. If deviation exceeds 5%, isolate the string and inspect for reversed polarity or shading from the oak tree noted in the drone survey dated July 9.

## Wrap-Up and Next Steps
File the as-built photos and torque logs within 24 hours. Schedule the utility interconnection inspection for Tuesday, September 3, 2025. Update the customer portal with production estimates based on the 8.4 kW DC nameplate rating.
