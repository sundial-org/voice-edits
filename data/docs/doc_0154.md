# Draft: Launching StackTrace 2.0 — Search, Context, Speed

## The Bottleneck We Couldn't Ignore

In March 2024, our team at StackTrace noticed a pattern: engineers were losing 45 minutes per incident just jumping between logs, traces, and Slack threads. We built StackTrace 1.0 to collapse that chaos into a single pane, but the feedback was clear—context was still fragmented.

## What Changes in StackTrace 2.0

Today, on October 14, we are releasing StackTrace 2.0. This isn't a feature dump; it is a rethinking of developer flow. Key improvements include:

- Unified telemetry search across 12 different data sources with sub-100ms latency
- Context-aware annotations powered by the Meridian LLM introduced in Q2
- One-click incident timelines that link commits, deploys, and error spikes automatically
- A redesigned CLI that runs natively on macOS, Linux, and Windows ARM

We also reduced our ingestion overhead by 38%, which lets us pass savings directly to customers on the Team plan.

## Built for Real Teams

We tested the beta with three design partners—Novara Systems, Helix Labs, and Kinto—between July and September. Their on-call rotation times dropped by an average of 22 minutes. That is not a vanity metric; it is sleep regained and incidents resolved faster.

## Looking Ahead

StackTrace 2.0 is available immediately for all existing users. We are hosting a live demo on October 22 at 10:00 AM PT. If you are tired of stitching together context manually, join us. Our mission remains simple: make debugging the fastest part of shipping software, not the slowest.
