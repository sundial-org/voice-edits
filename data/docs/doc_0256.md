# Q3 Infrastructure Roadmap: Behind the Firewall at Riverside General

## The Scope of the Upgrade

On August 14, the Clinical IT team at Riverside General began Phase 2 of our EMR modernization. This is not a simple patch cycle. We are migrating 340 virtual servers, updating the PACS gateway, and reconfiguring network segmentation across the East Wing data center. Lead Architect Dr. Elena Vasquez has mapped every dependency, and her 18-page risk assessment is now the baseline for all sprint planning.

## Key Changes for Clinical Staff

Starting September 3, downtime windows will shift to 02:00–04:30 to minimize disruption to emergency intake. Nurses and physicians should expect brief authentication redirects during this window.

Notable adjustments include:
- A new MFA prompt for remote access via the Citrix portal
- Faster load times for radiology images following the PACS cache upgrade
- Temporary read-only mode for the legacy lab interface until October 12
- Updated workstation certificates pushed automatically on September 5

## Security and Compliance Milestones

Our HIPAA audit review is scheduled for November 9. The infrastructure team must close all critical vulnerabilities flagged in the July scan before that date. Marcus Chen, our CISO, has set a hard deadline of October 30 for remediation of the 23 open CVEs in the pharmacy dispensing network.

We have also implemented zero-trust policies on three subnets handling ePHI, reducing lateral movement risk without impacting clinical workflows.

## What Comes Next

The go-live for Phase 3 is targeted for January 8, 2026. Until then, please direct all outage reports to the IT Service Desk at extension 4100. We will publish weekly status updates every Friday by 16:00.

Thank you for your patience as we strengthen the systems that support patient care.
