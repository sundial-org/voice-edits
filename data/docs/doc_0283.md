# Aether Carbon — Open Source Climate Intelligence

Aether Carbon is a climate-tech company building open-source forecasting tools for grid-scale decarbonization. Founded in 2021 and headquartered in Oslo, our mission is to make carbon intensity data transparent, verifiable, and actionable for energy operators, policy analysts, and grid managers across Europe.

## The Problem

Electricity grids are cleaner than ever, yet carbon accounting remains opaque. Most forecasting models rely on proprietary emissions factors that lag by months, making real-time dispatch decisions nearly impossible. In 2023, the EU reported a 9% gap between estimated and verified grid carbon intensity during peak winter loads. We are closing that gap.

## Our Platform

Aether Core is an open-source pipeline that ingests live SCADA streams, satellite aerosols, and market settlement data to produce 15-minute carbon intensity forecasts. Key capabilities include:

- Real-time forecasting with 94.3% accuracy across the Nordic bidding zones
- Open API access under the Apache 2.0 license
- Integration modules for Python, Julia, and Rust
- Historical backtesting dataset spanning 2018 through Q1 2024
- Verified by the European Energy Observatory in March 2024

## Impact Metrics

Since our public launch in June 2022, the platform has supported over 140 institutional users and avoided an estimated 14,000 metric tons of CO₂ through optimized dispatch scheduling. Our research partner, Dr. Elena Vasquez at TU Delft, validated the forecasting model in a peer-reviewed study published in Energy Systems in January 2024.

## Roadmap

We are expanding coverage to the Iberian Peninsula and the Baltic states by Q3 2024. Upcoming releases will include probabilistic scenario generation and a native dashboard for non-technical grid operators.
