# Clinical Network Resilience: Q3 Infrastructure Update

## Session Overview
These notes summarize the August 14 briefing led by Dr. Elena Vasquez, Chief Clinical Informatics Officer, for the Hospital IT Infrastructure Group. The primary goal was aligning network redundancy protocols with the Joint Commission’s updated digital-health standards before the October 1 compliance deadline. Vasquez emphasized that downtime in the East Wing telemetry cluster is no longer an acceptable single point of failure.

## Critical Infrastructure Changes
Starting September 3, we will migrate the legacy VLAN backbone supporting radiology PACS and laboratory LIS interfaces. The migration targets 340 endpoints across three campuses and requires a staged cutover during the 02:00–05:00 maintenance window every Tuesday and Thursday. Network Operations confirmed that redundant fiber paths through Building C are now active, reducing failover latency from 14 seconds to under 2 seconds.

Key priorities include:
- Validating TLS 1.3 encryption on all gateway appliances by September 10
- Replacing aging core switches in Building B with models supporting 100 Gbps uplinks
- Testing automated failover scripts with the clinical engineering team during the September 12 drill

## Compliance and Risk Assessment
The audit trail must document 99.99% uptime for critical care telemetry streams. Any deviation during the migration window must be logged in ServiceNow within 15 minutes and escalated to Vasquez and the on-call network architect. We also need to preserve legacy packet captures for 90 days post-migration to support forensic review if clinical workflows are interrupted.
