# Postmortem: MetroLine Red Signal Cascade — March 14, 2024

## Executive Summary
On March 14, 2024, at 07:42 EDT, a cascading signal failure on the MetroLine Red corridor disrupted service across 14 stations from Union Central to Northgate. The incident lasted 4 hours and 17 minutes, affecting approximately 38,000 morning commuters. Chief Engineer Diana Alvarez led the response. No injuries occurred, but the agency incurred $1.2 million in emergency contractor costs and lost fare revenue.

## Timeline of Events
- 07:42 — Track circuit TC-19 reported false occupancy at Milepost 8.4
- 07:51 — Central Dispatch activated single-track protocols between Union Central and Parkside
- 08:15 — Backup relay relay R-77 failed to reset, triggering a corridor-wide stop
- 09:30 — Field technicians Marcus Chen and Sofia Reyes isolated the fault to a water-damaged junction box at Milepost 8.4
- 11:59 — Full bi-directional service restored after relay replacement and signal verification

## Root Cause Analysis
The primary failure was corrosion in junction box JB-204, installed during the 2019 track upgrade. Seasonal freeze-thaw cycles allowed moisture ingress, degrading insulation resistance below 0.5 megohms. The false occupancy propagated through the legacy SCADA layer, which lacked redundant validation checks for adjacent track blocks. Secondary relay R-77 then latched into an unrecoverable fault state.

## Impact Assessment
- 38,000 passengers experienced delays averaging 52 minutes
- 14 stations affected; shuttle buses deployed at 6 locations
- Estimated direct costs: $1.2 million
- Customer satisfaction surveys dropped 18 points in the week following

## Actions and Remediation
Immediate fixes include replacing JB-204 and inspecting 312 similar boxes by May 30, 2024. The engineering division will deploy redundant track circuit validators by Q3 2024. Training for dispatch staff on cascade protocols will be updated before June 1, 2024, with Director Alvarez overseeing certification.

## Lessons Learned
We underestimated aging infrastructure risks in underground segments built before 1995. Faster isolation of false-occupancy signals could have reduced downtime by roughly 90 minutes. Cross-functional drills between dispatch and field crews will occur monthly starting April 2024.
