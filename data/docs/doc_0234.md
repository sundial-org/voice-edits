# Project Helix — Synthetic Promoter Library for Mammalian Stress Response

## Overview
Initiated in March 2023 by Dr. Elena Vasquez and the Genome Dynamics Unit at the Mendel Institute, Project Helix aims to engineer a modular promoter library that activates selectively under oxidative and thermal stress in HEK293-derived lines. Our objective is to reduce basal expression by 40 percent while maintaining rapid induction kinetics within 90 minutes of stimulus onset.

## Library Design & Construction
We synthesized 384 variant constructs using Golden Gate assembly with BsaI and BsmBI restriction sites. Each variant combines one of four minimal CMV backbones with 96 synthetic stress-response elements derived from the NRF2 and HSF1 pathways. Key design parameters include:
- Promoter core lengths of 180 bp, 220 bp, and 260 bp
- Spacer distances between enhancer and TATA box set at 12, 18, or 24 nucleotides
- Fluorescent reporters: mNeonGreen (excitation 506 nm) and mScarlet-I

## Screening Protocol
High-throughput characterization was performed on the CytoFLEX S flow cytometer in collaboration with the Bioimaging Core led by Marcus Chen. Cells were exposed to 500 µM H2O2 or 42 °C heat shock for defined intervals. We collected data from 12 independent biological replicates across three batch dates: 14 June 2024, 29 July 2024, and 03 September 2024.

## Key Results
The variant designated HX-217 showed a 6.3-fold induction ratio with basal fluorescence remaining below 280 arbitrary units. By contrast, the wild-type CMV control exhibited continuous leaky expression exceeding 900 AU under identical culture conditions in DMEM supplemented with 10 percent FBS.

## Data Availability & Collaboration
All sequencing data have been deposited at the European Nucleotide Archive under accession PRJEB61234. The plasmid kit is available through Addgene (catalog #218942). We welcome inquiries from groups working on inducible gene therapy vectors or synthetic biology circuits for mammalian systems.
