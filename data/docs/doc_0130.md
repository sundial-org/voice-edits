# Helios Residential Solar Installation — Product Requirements Document

## Overview
This document defines deliverables for the Helios 8.4 kW residential solar installation at 1420 Oak Ridge Lane, commissioned by homeowner Maria Chen. The project replaces an aging 3.2 kW legacy array and must achieve Permission to Operate (PTO) from the local utility no later than November 14, 2025.

## Scope & Deliverables
The installation covers a south-facing asphalt shingle roof with a 28-degree pitch. The system will use 21 Q.PEAK DUO BLK-G10+ 400 W modules paired with a SolarEdge SE7600H-US inverter and a 10 kWh LG RESU battery. Key deliverables include:
- Full structural and electrical engineering stamps
- Utility interconnection application filed by October 3, 2025
- Customer-facing monitoring portal with real-time production tracking
- 25-year manufacturer and 10-year workmanship warranties

## Technical Requirements
The array must produce an estimated 11,450 kWh annually, offsetting 92% of the homeowner’s historical usage. All DC conductors shall be ran in 1-inch EMT conduit with appropriate derating for rooftop ambient temperatures up to 65°C. Rapid shutdown compliance per NEC 2023 Section 690.12 is mandatory; module-level electronics must reduce voltage to below 30 V within 30 seconds of grid loss.

## Installation Timeline
Work begins October 20, 2025, with racking and module placement completed by October 24. Electrical commissioning and battery integration occur October 27–29. Final inspection is scheduled for November 4, with utility witness testing targeted for November 10. Any delay past the November 14 PTO deadline triggers liquidated damages of $150 per calendar day.

## Quality Assurance
Quality checks occur at three gates: pre-install roof survey, mid-install torque verification, and post-commissioning IV curve tracing. The crew lead, James Whitaker, must sign off on each gate. Aerial drone imagery will document pre- and post-install conditions for warranty registration.

## Risk & Compliance
Permitting is handled through County Building Services; solar-specific amendments must reference Ordinance 2024-18. If the roof deck requires reinforcement, the change order cap is $2,400. Lightning protection and grounding electrode conductors must bond to the existing UFER within 6 feet of the service entrance.
