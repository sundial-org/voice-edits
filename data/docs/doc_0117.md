# Product Requirements Document: BridgeLearn Literacy Platform v2.0

## Overview & Mission Context
BridgeLearn is a nonprofit edtech initiative serving Title I schools across the Pacific Northwest. This document defines the feature set and delivery timeline for the BridgeLearn Literacy Platform v2.0, scheduled for pilot deployment on September 15, 2024. The platform must support 12,000 active students and 450 educators by the end of Q1 2025 without compromising accessibility standards or data privacy commitments made to partner districts.

## Target Users & Stakeholders
Primary users include K-5 reading specialists, multilingual learners, and classroom teachers in the Seattle, Tacoma, and Spokane districts. Dr. Amara Osei, our Director of Curriculum Partnerships, will oversee content alignment with state literacy frameworks. The product team must also accommodate district technology coordinators who manage single sign-on integrations and FERPA-compliant reporting dashboards.

## Functional Requirements
The v2.0 release introduces adaptive phonics pathways, real-time oral fluency assessment, and family-facing progress portals available in English, Spanish, Somali, and Vietnamese. Core system capabilities include:
- Adaptive lesson sequencing driven by a diagnostic benchmark administered every 30 days
- Offline-first mobile architecture supporting devices with 2 GB RAM or lower
- Automated IEP goal tracking with export formats compatible with the Washington State reporting system
- Multilingual family notifications via SMS and email with 48-hour response tracking

## Technical & Accessibility Constraints
Engineering must maintain WCAG 2.1 AA compliance across all interfaces. The backend will run on AWS Educate credits through June 2025, with database encryption at rest and in transit. Load testing must confirm stability at 8,000 concurrent sessions before the October 1, 2024 public beta. No student biometric data may be stored on-device; all audio analysis for fluency scoring happens server-side with 72-hour retention limits.

## Success Metrics & Milestones
We will measure impact through literacy growth percentiles, teacher retention of platform usage, and family engagement rates. Key milestones include:
- Closed beta with 80 educators: August 22, 2024
- Full pilot in 14 schools: September 15, 2024
- Mid-pilot review with Dr. Osei and district stakeholders: November 1, 2024
- Scale decision for the 2025-2026 academic year: January 30, 2025

Target outcomes include a 15 percent average improvement in oral reading fluency within one semester and 85 percent weekly active teacher usage sustained over 12 consecutive weeks.
