# Roastery Technical Design Document — Horizon Batch Works

## Facility Overview
The Horizon Batch Works facility in Portland, Oregon operates a 2,400-square-foot production floor designed for a peak throughput of 480 kilograms of green coffee per day. The layout separates green storage, roasting, and packaging into discrete thermal zones to prevent cross-contamination and maintain stable ambient conditions at 68 °F with 45% relative humidity.

## Roasting System Architecture
We selected a 15-kilogram Probat P25 drum roaster paired with a dual-burner modulating gas train rated for 85,000 BTU/hr. The machine feeds into a 3.2-meter cooling tray with forced-air extraction that drops bean temperature to 85 °F within 90 seconds.

Key design parameters:
- Batch size range: 10 kg to 14.5 kg
- Target development time ratio: 22% to 26%
- Exhaust duct velocity: 850 feet per minute
- Afterburner retention: 4.5 seconds at 1,250 °F

## Workflow and Safety Protocols
Green coffee arrives in 60-kilogram GrainPro bags and is staged for 72 hours of acclimation before roasting. Every batch receives a unique lot code tied to the harvest date—most recently the 14 February 2024 delivery from Finca El Mirador.

Operational checkpoints:
- Pre-roast moisture verification (target 10.5% to 11.2%)
- ColorTrack Agtron reading within 45 seconds of drop
- Packaging under nitrogen flush with residual O2 below 2%

## Quality and Scale Targets
By Q3 2024, Horizon Batch Works aims to process 12 metric tons monthly while maintaining a defect rate below 0.3%. Lead roaster Mara Delgado oversees profile development, with cupping scores evaluated against the Specialty Coffee Association protocol every Tuesday and Thursday.
