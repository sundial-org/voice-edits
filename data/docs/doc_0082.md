# IT Infrastructure Resilience: Q3 Hospital Network Briefing

## Incident Recap and Timeline
On August 14, 2024, at 02:17, monitoring flagged packet loss exceeding 12% across the East Wing radiology VLAN. By 03:45, Dr. Elena Vasquez confirmed three critical PACS nodes were unreachable. The root cause was a failed core switch firmware update pushed on August 12. We executed the rollback protocol at 05:10, restoring 98% throughput by 06:30. No patient data was exposed, but 47 diagnostic studies experienced delays.

## System Hardening Measures
We are replacing legacy switch stacks with redundant Cisco Catalyst 9300 pairs in every clinical wing. Network segmentation will isolate imaging, EHR, and guest traffic by October 1, 2024. Key actions include:
- Deploying dual-homed fiber links to Radiology and Cardiology by September 15
- Enforcing TLS 1.3 on all internal APIs serving the Epic integration
- Conducting quarterly failover drills led by Marcus Chen every second Tuesday
- Maintaining offline backup snapshots with a 72-hour recovery point objective

## Compliance and Audit Preparation
Joint Commission surveyors will arrive November 4, 2024. We must demonstrate 99.9% uptime logs for critical care networks and present updated access control matrices. Sarah Okonkwo has completed the HIPAA risk assessment revision, identifying 8 medium-risk gaps in vendor remote-access policies. Remediation must close before the audit window opens.

## Training and Operational Readiness
All IT staff must complete the updated incident-response certification by September 30. The curriculum covers network isolation, clinical communication trees, and evidence preservation.
