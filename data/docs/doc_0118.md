# Sonic Commons Collective: Equipment & Residency Grant Proposal

## Collective Overview
Sonic Commons Collective is a Brooklyn-based music production cooperative founded in 2019 by engineers Mara Ellis and Devon Park. Our 14-member collective operates out of a converted warehouse in Bushwick, providing affordable studio access, mentorship, and collaborative recording spaces for emerging producers from underrepresented backgrounds. Since our incorporation, we have hosted 340 recording sessions and released 27 projects through our in-house label imprint.

## Project Description: The 2025 Residency Program
We are requesting $48,000 to launch a six-month production residency running from March 1 to August 31, 2025. The program will select four resident artists—two electronic producers and two beatmakers—who will receive dedicated studio time, mentorship from collective members, and a $1,200 production stipend each. Residents will work toward a collaborative EP released on vinyl and all major streaming platforms.

## Budget & Resource Allocation
The requested funding breaks down as follows:
- $22,000 for upgrading our analog mixing console and outboard gear (Neve 1073LB preamps, UAD Satellite)
- $14,000 for resident stipends and travel support
- $8,000 for vinyl pressing, artwork, and promotional materials for the collaborative release
- $4,000 for accessibility accommodations and childcare support during session blocks

## Impact & Community Engagement
This residency extends beyond individual artists. We will host four free public workshops on synthesis and mixing at the Brooklyn Public Library, reaching an estimated 120 participants. All resident sessions will be documented through our podcast, *Signal Path*, and archived at the Center for Brooklyn History. By investing in shared infrastructure and direct artist support, Sonic Commons strengthens the local ecosystem that has already nurtured voices like producer Juno Reyes and sound designer Kai Whitmore.
