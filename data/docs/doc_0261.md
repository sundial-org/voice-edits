# Postmortem: Batch R-09 Roast Contamination Incident — Ember & Bean Roastery

## Incident Summary
On March 14, 2024, at 06:17, lead roaster Marcus Chen detected an acrid, metallic off-aroma during the cooling phase of Batch R-09, a 42 kg run of our single-origin Guatemala Huehuetenango. The batch had reached first crack at 09:42 and was entering the development phase when a malfunctioning bearing in Drum 2 released lubricant aerosol into the roasting chamber. The contamination affected approximately 38 kg of green coffee, rendering it unsellable and triggering a full production halt.

## Impact and Timeline
The incident caused a 4-hour shutdown of Roasting Line B. We lost the entire 42 kg batch valued at $1,240, plus an additional $380 in delayed packaging labor for the morning shift. No contaminated product reached customers; Marcus identified the anomaly before bagging began at 07:05. However, three wholesale accounts—Blue Bottle Cafe, Northwind Bakery, and The Daily Grind—experienced delivery delays of 24 to 36 hours as we rerouted orders through our backup Drum 3.

## Root Cause Analysis
Investigation traced the failure to a grease seal in the drum drive assembly that had exceeded its 1,200-hour service interval by 340 hours. The seal degraded under sustained heat cycles above 210°C, allowing synthetic lubricant to vaporize and deposit on the coffee surface. Our preventive maintenance logs showed the last inspection was on January 22, 2024, but the replacement was deferred due to parts backorder.

- Replace drum drive seals every 1,000 operating hours, not 1,200
- Install inline air-quality sensors at Drum 2 exhaust by April 5
- Require secondary sensory verification by a second roaster before bagging
- Maintain a 50 kg emergency green-coffee reserve for rerouting

## Remediation Actions
We replaced the bearing and seal assembly on March 15 at 09:00 and validated Drum 2 with a 15 kg test roast of Sumatra Mandheling. All air ducts were deep-cleaned with food-grade degreaser, and we updated our maintenance dashboard to flag service intervals automatically. Marcus led a refresher training session on March 16 for all four roasters, emphasizing early-stage aroma deviation protocols.

## Lessons Learned and Follow-Up
This incident revealed that single-point sensory checks are insufficient for high-value batches. Going forward, every batch over 30 kg requires dual sign-off. We have also ordered redundant seal kits and committed to a 48-hour maximum parts backlog. The Guatemala Huehuetenango reorder is scheduled for delivery on March 22, with replacement batches shipping to affected wholesale partners by March 20.
