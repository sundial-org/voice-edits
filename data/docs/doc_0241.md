# Robotics Team Field Deployment: Project Mantis

## Event Overview
The Lunar Robotics Collective will deploy the Mantis rover at the 2026 Desert Ridge Challenge in Albuquerque on March 14. Team lead Dr. Elena Vasquez has secured a 48-hour testing window within the arena's eastern quadrant.

## Pre-Deployment Checklist
- Calibrate LiDAR sensors and verify 12V battery banks
- Pack replacement servo motors (part #MR-774)
- Confirm satellite uplink with mission control at 09:00 MST

## Schedule and Milestones
Day one focuses on terrain mapping. By 16:00, the rover must complete three autonomous loops. Day two targets sample retrieval; we need four soil specimens collected before the 11:00 cutoff.

## Safety and Communication Protocol
All operators wear high-visibility vests. Vasquez monitors telemetry from Station Alpha. If temperatures exceed 42°C, operations pause immediately and equipment returns to the shaded hangar.

## Post-Event Review
Within 72 hours of return, the team will compile logs, clean actuators, and submit findings to the National Robotics Foundation by April 2.
