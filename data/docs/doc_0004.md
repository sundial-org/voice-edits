# Postmortem: Route Optimization Service Outage — Apex Freight Lines

## Summary
On March 14, 2024, Apex Freight Lines experienced a 4-hour degradation of the RouteWise optimization engine. The failure cascaded into delayed dispatch for 312 vehicles across the Midwest hub in Columbus, Ohio, and resulted in an estimated $84,000 in contractual penalties.

## Timeline
- 06:12 ET — Monitoring alerts fired for elevated latency on the route-calculation API.
- 06:45 ET — On-call engineer Priya Mehta acknowledged the page and began investigating.
- 07:30 ET — Full service outage declared; fallback to manual routing protocols activated.
- 10:15 ET — Root cause identified and hotfix deployed to production cluster `route-engine-7`.
- 10:45 ET — All 312 affected dispatches restored to automated optimization.

## Impact
The outage directly affected 312 trucks and 47 contracted drivers in the Ohio-Illinois corridor. Customer SLAs for same-day delivery dropped to 71%, and two high-value clients—Meridian Grocers and Lakeview Distribution—invoked penalty clauses totaling $84,000.

## Root Cause
A misconfigured memory limit in Kubernetes deployment v2.4.1 triggered an out-of-memory cascade in the graph solver. The deployment had been promoted by the CI pipeline without the required load-test gate.

## Action Items
- Increase load-test gate coverage to 100% of route-engine deployments by April 30, 2024.
- Add memory-limit alerts with a 5-minute lead time before OOM kills.
- Require two senior approvals for any change to cluster `route-engine-7`.
- Conduct a disaster-recovery drill with manual fallback protocols by May 15, 2024.

## Lessons Learned
The incident revealed that our manual fallback protocols, last tested in November 2023, were sufficient but slow. We need faster escalation paths and stricter deployment gates for critical logistics infrastructure.
