# Solar Project Research Notes — Riverside Array

## Site Survey Overview
Conducted preliminary assessment at 442 Linden Drive on March 14, 2024. The property is a 2,100 sq ft ranch-style home with a south-facing roof plane measuring approximately 28 feet by 42 feet. Homeowner, Marcus Chen, requested a 9.6 kW system to offset 110% of annual usage. Tree shading from the eastern oak line is minimal before 10:30 AM, but dense by 2:00 PM. Roof pitch calculated at 6:12, which is favorable for racking.

## Equipment and Layout Options
After comparing string inverter versus power optimizer architectures, I recommended Enphase IQ8 microinverters for panel-level monitoring and rapid shutdown compliance. The proposed layout uses 24 REC Alpha Pure 400W modules in two rows of twelve.

Key considerations for the design:
- Module spacing to maintain 18-inch access pathways per local fire code
- Two 4-foot by 12-foot racking zones separated by a central ridge vent
- Total DC system size of 9.6 kW with an estimated AC output of 8.4 kW
- Conduit run estimated at 65 linear feet from array to main service panel

## Utility and Incentive Review
Pacific Gas & Electric (PG&E) rate schedule EV2-A applies here. Net metering 3.0 interconnection requires UL 1741-SA certified inverters, which the IQ8 series satisfies. Marcus plans to apply for the federal Investment Tax Credit at 30% and the Self-Generation Incentive Program (SGIP) Step 4 battery rebate if we pair the system with a 10 kWh Tesla Powerwall.
