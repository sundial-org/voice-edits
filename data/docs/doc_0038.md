# Onboarding Guide — Meridian BioLabs, Cambridge

Welcome to Meridian BioLabs. This guide covers your first 30 days in the Cell Engineering Group.

## Safety and Certification

Before entering BSL-2 suites, complete the institutional biosafety course. Dr. Elena Vasquez oversees certification audits every quarter. You must wear a disposable lab coat (Cat. No. 4421) and nitrile gloves rated for 15-minute ethanol exposure. Emergency eyewash stations are tested on the first Monday of each month.

## Lab Access and Equipment

Your keycard activates at 06:00 and expires at 22:00. The shared equipment calendar, managed via LabArchives, requires 48-hour advance booking for the Zeiss LSM 900 confocal. Do not leave cultures in incubator B3 overnight without a dated label. Centrifuge rotor FA-45-24-11 is restricted to trained staff only.

## Daily Protocol Checklist

Every shift begins with documentation:
- Verify incubator CO2 is at 5% and temperature reads 37.0 °C
- Record lot numbers for all media batches, especially FBS Lot 892-B
- Update the contamination log if any flask shows turbidity
- Sign out reagents from the -20 °C freezer using the barcode scanner

## Training Schedule

Your onboarding follows a structured timeline:
- Week 1: Shadow senior researcher Marcus Chen on tissue culture workflows
- Week 2: Complete the PCR contamination workshop by March 14
- Week 3: Pass the independent competency assessment for cryopreservation

Mentorship meetings occur every Tuesday at 10:00 in Conference Room 4.

## Closing Notes

Report any near-misses to the Safety Committee within 24 hours. Your first performance review is scheduled 90 days after your start date. Welcome to the team.
