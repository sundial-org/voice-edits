# OpenLearn Literacy Hub

The OpenLearn Literacy Hub is an open-source digital classroom platform built by the Meridian Education Foundation to serve rural and under-resourced K-8 schools. Since our pilot launch in March 2022, the project has reached over 14,000 students across 38 districts, providing adaptive reading pathways, offline-first lesson caches, and multilingual family dashboards without requiring proprietary hardware.

## Project Overview

Meridian Education Foundation developed this repository to consolidate our curriculum APIs, teacher-facing analytics, and student assessment engines into a single maintainable codebase. The stack combines a React frontend, a Django backend, and PostgreSQL data stores, all containerized for low-bandwidth deployment. Our core philosophy is that equitable technology should run on aging infrastructure: every release is tested on hardware equivalent to a 2018 Chromebook with 4 GB of RAM.

## Key Features

- Adaptive literacy pathways calibrated for grades K through 5, supporting English, Spanish, and Haitian Creole
- Offline-first service worker that caches up to 90 days of lesson content for intermittent connectivity
- Real-time progress telemetry sent via compressed JSON payloads under 50 KB per session
- Administrator dashboard for district-level reporting, including attendance correlation and growth percentile tracking
- Parent portal with automated SMS notifications in 12 languages, reducing communication barriers for non-English-speaking households

## Installation & Deployment

District IT staff can deploy the hub using our published Docker Compose configuration or Kubernetes manifests maintained in the `deploy/` directory. A minimal production instance requires 2 vCPU cores, 4 GB of memory, and 20 GB of persistent storage. We recommend provisioning via the Meridian Foundation's hosted mirror if local server maintenance is limited; the mirror offers free tier hosting for verified nonprofit partners through 2026.

## Community & Contributions

We welcome contributions from educators, translators, and software engineers. All pull requests are reviewed by the Meridian technical committee, led by Dr. Aisha Patel and senior engineer Marcus Okonkwo. Before submitting code, please consult our Contributor Covenant and open an issue describing your proposed change. Translation efforts are coordinated through the Global Literacy Volunteers network, which has delivered localized content in 22 languages since 2023.

## Roadmap & Impact Metrics

By the end of Q3 2025, we aim to expand the assessment engine to cover mathematics foundations for grades 6 through 8 and integrate with state-level data standards adopted in 2024. Early outcome studies conducted with the University of Vermont indicate that students using the hub for at least 120 instructional minutes per week showed 18 percent greater reading fluency growth compared to control groups. Our long-term goal is to make the entire platform available under an MIT license by January 2027, ensuring that no school is ever locked behind a paywall.
