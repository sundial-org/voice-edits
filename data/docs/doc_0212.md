# BridgeEd Foundation: Spring Equity in EdTech Summit Plan

## Event Overview
The BridgeEd Foundation will host the Spring Equity in EdTech Summit on March 15, 2025, at the Meridian Community Center in Denver. This single-day convening targets 120 K-12 educators, district technology coordinators, and nonprofit partners. Our goal is to align open-source learning tools with classroom equity frameworks and distribute $50,000 in micro-grants to pilot schools.

## Program Design
Dr. Amara Okonkwo, our Director of Learning Innovation, will deliver the opening keynote at 9:00 a.m. Following her address, participants will rotate through three concurrent tracks:
- Accessible hardware lending libraries for rural districts
- Data privacy protocols for student-facing AI tutors
- Community Wi-Fi mapping and low-bandwidth curriculum design

Each track runs for 75 minutes and includes a working session with tangible deliverables.

## Logistics and Partnerships
Registration opens February 1, 2025, and closes March 8, 2025, to allow catering and accessibility accommodations. We have secured sponsorships from OpenLearn Labs and the Denver Education Cooperative. The venue provides ADA-compliant spaces, real-time captioning, and multilingual interpretation in Spanish and Somali.

## Budget and Impact Metrics
We have allocated $18,500 for venue and AV, $12,000 for participant travel stipends, and $8,000 for accessibility services. Success will be measured by:
- 100% of attendees completing a post-summit action plan
- At least 15 schools applying for pilot funding by April 30, 2025
- A 20% increase in BridgeEd’s open-resource downloads within 30 days

## Next Steps
The planning committee—Maria Chen, James Whitfield, and Dr. Amara Okonkwo—will finalize the speaker roster by January 20, 2025. We will publish the agenda publicly by February 10, 2025, and open scholarship applications for educators from Title I schools immediately thereafter.
