# MetroLink Unveils Route Optimization Plan for Eastside Corridor

## A New Chapter for Eastside Transit

Starting in January 2025, MetroLink will launch a comprehensive overhaul of the Eastside Corridor bus network, marking the largest service restructuring in our agency’s 42-year history. The initiative, led by General Manager David Okonkwo and approved by the Board of Directors on September 3, aims to reduce average wait times while expanding access to three major employment hubs along Riverside Drive.

For years, riders on Route 14 and Route 22 have navigated overlapping schedules and inconsistent frequencies. After analyzing 18 months of ridership data—covering more than 2.4 million boardings—we identified clear patterns: peak-hour crowding east of Meridian Station and underutilized midday loops near the warehouse district. This plan addresses both without cutting overall service hours.

## Key Service Changes

The redesigned network simplifies transfers and introduces timed connections at Central Terminal. Beginning Monday, January 13, 2025, passengers can expect the following adjustments:

- Route 14 will shift to a 12-minute frequency during weekday peaks, up from 20 minutes, with direct service to Riverside Medical Center.
- Route 22 is being split into two distinct lines—22A serving the university district and 22B connecting the industrial park—eliminating the current 35-minute loop that frustrated commuters.
- A new express shuttle, Route 88X, will run every 15 minutes between 6:00 a.m. and 8:30 p.m., linking Eastside Station directly to downtown in under 22 minutes.
- Late-night service on all affected lines will extend to 1:30 a.m. on Fridays and Saturdays, responding to requests from hospitality workers.
- Real-time arrival displays will be installed at 14 high-ridership stops by December 10, 2024.

These updates reflect direct feedback gathered during our summer outreach campaign, including 34 community meetings and over 1,200 survey responses.

## Community Input Shaped the Plan

No redesign succeeds without listening. Between June and August 2024, MetroLink staff held listening sessions at the Eastside Library, the Meridian Community Center, and the Riverside Farmers Market. Residents consistently asked for faster connections, safer waiting environments, and clearer route maps—not just more buses.

Mayor Elena Vasquez emphasized this point during the September board meeting: “Public transit is public infrastructure. It should respond to the people who use it every morning, not just to spreadsheets.”

We incorporated that philosophy into the final design. For example, we preserved the popular stop near Clayton Elementary after parents and teachers raised concerns about student walking distances. We also added enhanced lighting and shelter improvements at eight stops along the 22B corridor.

## Timeline and Next Steps

The rollout will occur in three phases to minimize disruption. Phase one, beginning November 4, 2024, includes driver training and new signage installation. Phase two launches the revised schedules on January 13. Phase three, targeted for March 2025, completes the real-time tracking rollout and evaluates ridership shifts for potential further adjustments.

Riders can preview the new maps at metrolink.gov/eastside or pick up printed brochures at any transit center. Customer service representatives will also host informational tables at Central Terminal every Tuesday and Thursday through the end of the year.

We recognize that change takes time, but we are committed to making the Eastside Corridor faster, clearer, and more reliable for everyone who depends on it.
