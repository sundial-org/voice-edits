# Phase-I SBIR Proposal: AAV-Mediated RPE65 Replacement for Inherited Retinal Dystrophies

## Abstract
Principal Investigator Dr. Elena Vasquez proposes an 18-month preclinical program at Helion Biosciences to advance AAV8-RPE65 into IND-enabling studies. Inherited retinal dystrophies affect approximately 1 in 4,000 individuals worldwide; current standard-of-care gene therapy costs exceed $850,000 per patient. Our engineered capsid demonstrates 40% improved photoreceptor transduction in non-human primates compared to benchmark AAV2. We request $2.4 million to complete GLP toxicology, GMP vector manufacturing, and regulatory filing with the FDA by Q3 2026.

## Background and Significance
Mutations in RPE65 disrupt the visual cycle and cause Leber congenital amaurosis type 2. While voretigene neparvovec received approval in 2017, durability concerns and immune responses limit long-term efficacy. Helion’s proprietary directed-evolution platform generated capsid variant HV-201, which shows reduced neutralizing-antibody prevalence in human sera. Preliminary data in the RPE65-null canine model indicate sustained functional rescue for 24 months post-injection. A next-generation vector with enhanced retinal pigment epithelium specificity could expand the treatable population and reduce surgical risk.

## Specific Aims
We will execute three integrated milestones:
- Complete GLP ocular toxicology and biodistribution studies in cynomolgus macaques (n=36) under ICH S6 guidelines
- Manufacture three GMP-grade AAV8-RPE65 batches at 5.0 × 10^13 vg/mL using our 200 L stirred-tank bioreactor suite
- Submit the IND package—including CMC, pharmacology, and clinical protocol—by September 30, 2026

## Research Design and Methods
The toxicology study will evaluate single and repeat intravitreal doses at 1×, 3×, and 10× the proposed clinical dose. Ocular assessments include spectral-domain OCT, full-field ERG, and histopathology at 13 and 26 weeks. Vector potency will be confirmed via quantitative PCR and in vitro RPE65 enzymatic activity assays. All work will be conducted under ISO 13485 and 21 CFR Part 211 quality systems at our Cambridge, Massachusetts facility.

## Budget and Timeline
The $2.4M award is allocated as follows: 45% GMP manufacturing ($1.08M), 30% GLP toxicology ($720K), 15% regulatory and CMC consulting ($360K), and 10% project management ($240K). Milestone 1 begins January 2026; Milestone 3 targets IND submission in Q3 2026. Progress reports will be delivered quarterly to the NIH SBIR program office.

## Conclusion
This proposal addresses a critical unmet need in ophthalmic gene therapy. By leveraging Dr. Vasquez’s prior experience with AAV manufacturing and Helion’s proprietary capsid technology, we are positioned to deliver a safer, more durable RPE65 replacement therapy. We respectfully request full funding to advance HV-201 into first-in-human trials by 2027.
