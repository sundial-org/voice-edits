# Grant Proposal for Meridian Commerce: Scaling Ethical Digital Retail

## Executive Summary
Meridian Commerce seeks $450,000 in funding from the Horizon Digital Equity Fund to expand its B2B marketplace infrastructure. Founded in March 2022 by Dr. Elena Vasquez, the platform has already onboarded 312 sustainable vendors across North America. Our objective is to reduce supply chain friction by 40 percent before Q3 2025.

## Problem Statement
Small-scale producers face prohibitive listing fees and opaque logistics networks on legacy platforms. Meridian Commerce addresses this by providing flat-rate 3.2 percent transaction costs and real-time carbon-footprint tracking. Without expansion capital, however, vendor growth will stall at current levels.

## Proposed Solution
We will deploy three integrated modules by January 2026:
- A predictive inventory engine using open-source forecasting algorithms
- A peer-to-peer logistics broker connecting regional carriers
- A vendor verification portal audited by the Fair Trade Certification Board

These improvements will allow us to serve 1,200 vendors within eighteen months while maintaining sub-two-second page loads.

## Budget and Timeline
The $450,000 award will be allocated as follows: $180,000 for engineering salaries, $95,000 for cloud infrastructure on AWS, and $75,000 for compliance auditing. Remaining funds cover outreach and contingency. Milestones are set for June 2025 and December 2025.

## Expected Impact
By lowering barriers to digital trade, we project a 25 percent revenue increase for participating vendors within one fiscal year. The platform will also publish an annual transparency report verified by the Global E-Commerce Trust Initiative.

## Conclusion
With support from the Horizon Digital Equity Fund, Meridian Commerce can transform ethical retail from a niche option into a scalable standard. We invite reviewers to examine our pilot data and join this next phase of growth.
