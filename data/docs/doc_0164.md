# Meridian Freight Solutions — Operational Changelog

## Q3 Route Optimization Rollout
On August 14, 2024, we deployed the Meridian PathFinder v2.1 engine across all North American corridors. The update reduced average transit variability by 18% on the Chicago-to-Dallas lane and cut deadhead miles by roughly 340,000 per quarter. Dispatchers now receive dynamic rerouting alerts within 90 seconds of a weather or traffic anomaly.

- Integration with NOAA real-time feeds
- Automated fuel-tax reporting for 14 states
- Driver mobile dashboard with turn-by-turn revision tracking
- Fallback manual override retained for all Class-A carriers

## Warehouse Automation Upgrades
The Columbus Distribution Center completed its retrofit on September 3, 2024. We installed 22 autonomous guided vehicles (AGVs) and upgraded the WMS to Meridian Core 4.0. Throughput capacity rose to 48,000 units per shift, and mis-pick rates fell below 0.4%. Facility manager Elena Vasquez led the 11-week transition without a single lost-time incident.

## Fleet Electrification Milestones
As of October 1, 2024, 37% of our last-mile fleet in the Portland and Seattle metro zones operates on battery-electric chassis. The first 60 Volvo LIGHTS trucks entered revenue service on September 22, supported by a 2.4-megawatt charging hub at the Tacoma yard. Range anxiety dropped measurably; drivers report 94% satisfaction with cab climate and regenerative braking behavior.

## Safety and Compliance Updates
We revised our Hours-of-Service audit protocol following the FMCSA advisory issued July 12, 2024. All 412 drivers completed the refreshed fatigue-management module by August 30. The Portland yard also added collision-avoidance sensors to every yard tractor manufactured after 2019, affecting 86 vehicles.

## Customer Portal Enhancements
The Shipper Portal released build 3.8 on October 15, 2024. Clients can now generate custom carbon-footprint reports for shipments completed after January 1, 2023, and access real-time dock-door scheduling at 23 facilities. Enterprise accounts—representing 61% of annual tonnage—gained API webhook support for inventory triggers.
