# Incident Postmortem: CryoVault -80°C Cascade Failure at Meridian Biotech

## Executive Summary
On March 14, 2024, Meridian Biotech's North Wing cryogenic storage suite experienced a cascading refrigeration failure that exposed 342 primary cell-line vials to temperatures above -40°C for 6 hours and 18 minutes. The incident originated in Unit CV-7 during a scheduled compressor switchover managed by technician Javier Okonkwo. No personnel were injured, but 28 irreplaceable patient-derived xenograft samples were lost.

## Timeline of Events
- 02:14 — CV-7 primary compressor fault alarm triggered.
- 02:19 — Okonkwo acknowledged alert and initiated manual failover to backup loop B.
- 03:05 — Backup loop B seized due to refrigerant leak in condenser coil 4B.
- 03:47 — Facility management paged; emergency vendor ThermoCryo contacted.
- 08:36 — CryoVault restored to -80°C; internal audit of sample racks completed.

## Impact Assessment
The failure affected three storage banks. Bank Alpha retained viability for 214 research-grade lines. Bank Gamma suffered partial thaw cycles, compromising 89 vials intended for Phase II toxicity screening scheduled for June 2024. The 28 lost xenograft samples represented four years of collaborative work with Dr. Elena Vasquez at the Karolinska Institute.

## Root Cause Analysis
Investigation revealed that loop B had not undergone preventive maintenance since November 2022, violating the 90-day service interval. The refrigerant leak in coil 4B caused catastrophic pressure drop, preventing backup activation. Additionally, the monitoring dashboard failed to escalate the temperature deviation because the SMS gateway token for Meridian's alerting pipeline expired on February 28, 2024.

## Corrective Actions
- Replace condenser coil 4B and recharge refrigerant in all backup loops by April 5, 2024.
- Implement redundant temperature telemetry with cellular backup independent of SMS gateways.
- Retrain all cryo-facility staff, including Okonkwo and shift lead Sarah Chen, on emergency failover protocols.
- Commission quarterly third-party audits of mechanical refrigeration assets.
- Reconstruct lost xenograft cohort in partnership with Karolinska using archived tissue blocks.

## Lessons Learned
Reliance on a single manual failover pathway proved insufficient for high-value biorepositories. The expired SMS token demonstrates that digital infrastructure requires the same maintenance rigor as physical compressors. Moving forward, Meridian Biotech will treat cryogenic redundancy as a clinical-grade obligation rather than an operational convenience.
