# MetroLink Transit Agency Spring Service Launch Event Plan

## Event Overview
MetroLink Transit Agency will host the official unveiling of the expanded Red Line Corridor on Saturday, April 12, 2025, at the Central Station Plaza. The celebration marks the completion of Phase Two infrastructure upgrades and introduces the new fleet of 14 electric articulated buses. Mayor Elena Vasquez and Executive Director James Okonkwo will deliver remarks at 10:00 a.m., followed by a ceremonial first ride to the new Eastside Terminal.

## Logistics and Venue Setup
The plaza will open to the public at 9:00 a.m., with seating arranged for approximately 300 guests. Staff should complete audio-visual checks by 8:30 a.m. Security checkpoints will be positioned at the north and south entrances.

- Confirm stage delivery with Apex Event Rentals by March 28
- Install temporary signage directing pedestrians away from Platform 4
- Distribute 500 commemorative transit cards to the first attendees
- Coordinate with Metro Police for traffic control along 5th Avenue

## Programming Schedule
The agenda balances official protocol with community engagement.

- 9:30 a.m. — Welcome remarks by Communications Director Sarah Lin
- 10:00 a.m. — Keynote address from Mayor Elena Vasquez
- 10:25 a.m. — Ribbon cutting and first boarding
- 10:45 a.m. — Live music from the Riverside High School Jazz Band
- 11:30 a.m. — Public tour of Bus 2047, the lead vehicle in the zero-emission fleet

## Marketing and Outreach
Promotion begins March 17 through digital billboards, social media, and onboard posters on all 38 active rail cars. Partnerships with the Downtown Business Alliance and Eastside Community Council will drive attendance. Press kits must be finalized by April 5.

## Risk Management and Accessibility
Weather contingencies include tent coverage for the stage and a rain-date option of April 19, 2025. ASL interpreters will be present on stage. All pathways meet ADA width requirements, and quiet zones will be available near the information tent for guests with sensory needs.

## Post-Event Follow-Up
Within 72 hours, staff will compile attendance metrics, media coverage summaries, and public feedback surveys distributed via QR code. A debrief meeting is scheduled for April 15 with representatives from Operations, Public Affairs, and Facilities.
