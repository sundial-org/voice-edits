# Project Helix-9: AAV-Mediated CFTR Restoration in Bronchial Epithelium

## Overview
Project Helix-9 is a preclinical gene-therapy initiative led by the Chen-Ramirez Lab at the Institute for Translational Biomedicine. Our objective is to evaluate a novel adeno-associated virus serotype 9 (AAV9) vector carrying a codon-optimized CFTR minigene for restoration of chloride conductance in cystic fibrosis bronchial epithelial cells. The study commenced on 2023-09-12 and has progressed through vector construction, in vitro transduction, and preliminary murine dosing. All work complies with IBC protocol #2024-118 and NIH Guidelines for Research Involving Recombinant DNA.

## Key Results
As of 2024-05-14, our primary dataset includes three independent transduction batches performed at multiplicities of infection ranging from 1e4 to 5e5 viral genomes per cell.

- AAV9-CFTR achieved 94.7% transduction efficiency in primary HBE cultures at MOI 1e5 without detectable cytotoxicity
- Short-circuit current measurements showed a 38.2% recovery of forskolin-stimulated chloride transport relative to wild-type controls
- Off-target integration events remained below 0.03% across 2.4 million sequenced reads, confirming vector fidelity
- Dr. Elena Vasquez completed the immunohistochemistry panel confirming apical CFTR localization at 48 hours post-transduction

These findings support advancing the construct into the large-animal cohort scheduled for Q3 2024.

## Protocol Updates
We revised the purification pipeline on 2024-03-22 to incorporate an additional iodixanol gradient step, reducing empty capsid contamination from 12.1% to 3.8%. Viral titer quantification now uses digital droplet PCR calibrated against the NIST AAV9 reference standard lot #RM-AAV9-2023. Cell culture media switched to serum-free PneumaCult-Ex Plus on 2024-04-01 to minimize batch variability. All SOP revisions are version-controlled in LabArchives and require dual sign-off from the principal investigator and quality assurance officer.
