# Helios Residential Solar — Internal Crew FAQ

## Safety & PPE Requirements
Every tech must wear Class 0 rubber gloves and arc-rated face shields when working inside live combiner boxes. Hard hats are mandatory on all roof pitches over 4:12, regardless of weather. In March 2024 we updated our fall-protection protocol: anchor points must now be inspected by a certified lead—currently Marcus Chen or his designated alternate—before anyone clips in. If you spot frayed lanyards or corroded carabiners, tag them out immediately and log the issue in the Field Safety app. Remember, no shortcuts on 600V DC circuits.

## Installation Standards & Code Compliance
We follow NEC 2023 for all residential arrays. That means rapid-shutdown devices are required within 1 foot of the array boundary, not just at the inverter. Use only MC4-compatible connectors rated for 1,500V; mixing manufacturers voids our workmanship warranty. When flashing penetrations on composite shingle roofs, apply a 6-inch overlap of peel-and-stick membrane under the base bracket. For tile roofs, always pre-drill pilot holes to prevent cracks—replacement tiles cost $18 each and delay close-out by 48 hours.

## Equipment & Inventory Check
- Tesla Powerwall 3 units must stay in climate-controlled storage until 24 hours before installation
- Verify that every iron ridge rail shipment includes 3/8-inch stainless bolts; we had a bad batch in February 2024
- Microinverter serial numbers must be scanned into the project folder before leaving the warehouse
- Keep two spare 15A fuses in your truck kit at all times
- Ladders over 28 feet require a second crew member for stabilization

## Project Timeline & Client Communication
Standard installs run two days: rough-in on day one, commissioning on day two. If weather pushes the schedule, notify the customer by 7:00 PM the night before—never leave them guessing. Our average interconnection turnaround with the local utility is 14 business days after inspection approval. Do not promise clients a specific PTO date; instead, reference the typical 10-to-21-day window we see with GreenGrid Utilities.

## Warranty & Service Escalations
Labor warranty is 10 years; equipment warranties vary by manufacturer. If a homeowner reports a ground-fault error within 90 days of activation, dispatch a senior tech, not an apprentice. Document every service call with photos of the inverter display and the breaker panel labeling. For emergency shutdown requests after hours, route through the on-call manager—Sarah Delgado handles the rotation this quarter. Keep service reports concise; they feed directly into our quarterly reliability audit due every January 15.
