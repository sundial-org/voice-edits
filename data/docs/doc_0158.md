# Internal Research Notes — Q3 Direct Air Capture Pilot

## Project Overview
This memo consolidates findings from the Meridian Carbon Labs pilot facility in Reykjavik, operational since March 2024. Our objective is to validate sorbent regeneration cycles under Nordic humidity profiles before scaling to the 4,000-tonne annual module planned for 2026.

## Key Metrics & Observations
Dr. Elena Vasquez led the field trials between 14 June and 29 August 2024. The unit processed 312 tonnes of atmospheric CO2, exceeding the 250-tonne baseline by 24.8 percent. Energy draw stabilized at 1,450 kWh per tonne, still 18 percent above the target set by the 2023 feasibility study.

- Regeneration thermal profile peaked at 92 °C, within acceptable bounds.
- Sorbent degradation rate measured 0.3 percent per cycle after 800 runs.
- Humidity spikes above 78 percent correlated with a 12 percent drop in capture efficiency.
- Maintenance windows required every 340 operating hours, shorter than the 500-hour design spec.
- Local grid carbon intensity averaged 28 g CO2/kWh, confirming favorable lifecycle accounting.

## Technical Adjustments
We are adjusting the desorption loop to accommodate faster moisture release. The engineering team recommends retrofitting the heat exchanger with a titanium-grade alloy by October 2024 to mitigate corrosion observed in Module B.

## Next Steps
Validate revised cycle parameters in October, finalize vendor contracts by 15 November 2024, and present board-level CAPEX estimates—currently $42 million for Phase II—before year-end. Dr. Vasquez will coordinate with the Reykjavik municipal energy office to secure fixed-rate geothermal supply for 2025.
