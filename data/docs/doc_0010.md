# St. Mercy Hospital IT Department — Change Log

## Overview
This log covers all production changes, maintenance windows, and security patches deployed across St. Mercy Hospital’s clinical and administrative networks from March 1 through March 31, 2025. The Information Systems team completed 14 scheduled updates, resolved 8 critical incidents, and supported the go-live of the new telemetry ward in Building C. Coordinators should reference ticket numbers when reporting downstream issues.

## Infrastructure Updates
On March 14 at 02:00, engineers upgraded the core switching fabric in Data Center East to support 40 Gbps uplinks between the radiology PACS cluster and the electronic health record (EHR) backbone. The maintenance window lasted 2.5 hours, 30 minutes under the projected downtime. Backup DNS resolution was rerouted through the secondary node in Building D to preserve connectivity for ambulatory clinics. Network latency for imaging transfers improved by approximately 18 percent following the cutover.

## Clinical Application Patches
The clinical applications group released three targeted patches for the Cerner Millennium environment running version 2024.3. Key fixes included:
- Resolution of the medication administration record (MAR) timeout affecting pediatric units since February 28
- Restoration of barcode scanning compatibility for infusion pumps running firmware 4.2.1
- Correction of a calculation error in the insulin dosing protocol module used by endocrinology
All patches were validated in the QA sandbox for 72 hours before promotion to production on March 22.

## Security & Compliance
Responding to advisory CVE-2025-1182, the cybersecurity team applied kernel-level updates to 312 virtual endpoints and 24 physical servers hosting patient data. Dr. Elena Vasquez signed off on the risk assessment on March 18, confirming no disruption to HIPAA audit logging. Multi-factor authentication was enforced for all remote access to the clinical VPN, affecting 89 staff members who previously relied on single-factor tokens. The team also rotated encryption certificates for the laboratory interface engine ahead of the April 1 expiration deadline.

## Known Issues & Next Steps
Two intermittent issues remain under active monitoring. The wireless access points in the surgical pavilion experience brief drops during peak hours; vendor support is scheduled for April 4. Additionally, the billing interface to payer gateway Meridian Health shows delayed acknowledgment messages of roughly 4 minutes, though no data loss has been recorded. The April maintenance calendar will prioritize storage array firmware updates and the migration of legacy file shares from server NAS-07 to the new cluster. Senior IT Director Marcus Chen will review resource allocations at the planning meeting on April 2.
