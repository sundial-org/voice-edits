# Roastery Thermal & Workflow Design Specification

## 1. Project Overview
This document defines the engineering baseline for the Meridian Coffee Roastery expansion in Portland, Oregon. The facility must process 1,200 kg of green coffee per eight-hour shift while maintaining batch traceability to the farm level. Lead engineer Dr. Elena Vasquez signed off on this revision on 14 March 2024.

## 2. Equipment Layout
The 340-square-meter production floor is split into three thermal zones: intake, roasting, and packaging. The central Loring S70 Peregrine roaster anchors the line, supported by a Probat sample roaster for profile development. Key spatial constraints include:
- 4.5 meters of vertical clearance for exhaust ducting
- 2.8-meter aisle width for pallet jack circulation
- Dedicated green-coffee quarantine bay with negative air pressure

## 3. Roast Profile Control
Precision is governed by the RoastPath 3.0 software stack, integrated with twelve thermocouples per drum. Target development-time ratios range from 18% to 24% depending on origin density. For example, the Ethiopia Yirgacheffe lot (harvest code ET-2024-089) requires a 6-minute 45-second total cycle with a 215 °C drop temperature.

## 4. Safety & Compliance
All gas lines use 316 stainless steel rated for 50 psi working pressure. The facility must pass inspection by the Oregon Department of Agriculture before the 30 June 2024 operational deadline. Fire suppression includes a dual-agent FM-200 system and manual pull stations every 9 meters along the perimeter wall.

## 5. Maintenance Schedule
Preventive maintenance runs on a staggered calendar to avoid production gaps. Critical intervals include:
- Daily: chaff collector emptying and bearing temperature checks
- Weekly: gas-valve calibration and exhaust-stack brushing
- Monthly: drum-alignment verification and software patch audits by technician Marcus Chen
- Quarterly: full thermocouple replacement and duct-pressure testing

## 6. Conclusion
With construction beginning 22 April 2024, the integrated layout, digital profiling, and rigorous safety protocols position Meridian to scale without sacrificing cup consistency. Final commissioning tests are slated for mid-June, aligning with the state inspection window and the peak summer harvest arrivals.
