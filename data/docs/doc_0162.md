# Fintech App Research Notes — Q3 2024

## Project Overview
This document captures findings for the NovaPay mobile wallet redesign, led by product researcher Elena Vasquez. Our primary goal is to reduce onboarding friction for users aged 25-40 while maintaining PCI-DSS Level 1 compliance. The current build (v2.4.1) processes roughly 840,000 monthly active transactions, yet our conversion funnel leaks significantly at the identity verification stage.

## User Feedback & Pain Points
We conducted 18 moderated usability sessions between August 12 and August 29, 2024. Participants consistently reported confusion around the KYC flow.

- 63% of users abandoned verification after encountering the document upload screen
- Average time-to-complete for successful users was 4 minutes 12 seconds
- Support tickets referencing "blurry photo" errors increased by 22% since July 15
- Users under 30 expected instant approval, not the current 24-hour review window
- The biometric fallback option was hidden behind two nested menus
- Spanish-language users noted missing translations in the consent modal

## Competitor Benchmarking
We evaluated Stripe Link, Cash App, and Revolut. Stripe Link achieves identity verification in under 90 seconds using live camera guidance. Cash App uses progressive disclosure, while Revolut leverages open banking APIs to skip manual uploads in 14 supported regions. We should adopt similar progressive patterns without compromising our risk-score thresholds.

## Technical Constraints
Engineering lead Marcus Chen confirmed that our current OCR vendor, ScanTrust, caps processing at 3 concurrent requests per device. Migrating to the newer VisionAPI pipeline (scheduled for October 3, 2024) should resolve latency spikes observed during peak hours (18:00-21:00 EST). Additionally, the backend must store verification logs for 5 years per regulatory mandate.

## Recommended Next Steps
Prioritize the progressive KYC redesign for sprint 14. Allocate two UX designers to prototype the camera-guidance module by September 20, 2024. Run a closed beta with 500 invited users before broad rollout in November. Monitor abandonment rate, targeting a reduction from 63% to below 35% within 60 days of launch.
