# Build Season Log: Tuning the 2025 Drive Train

## Where We Started
On January 7, our lead mechanical engineer, Marcus Chen, unboxed the 2026 Kit of Parts chassis and immediately noticed the weight distribution was off by nearly four pounds. We had committed to a swerve drive this year, but the initial CAD model from December underestimated the battery tray footprint by 1.2 inches.

## The Mechanical Fix
The fabrication sub-team spent the weekend of January 11-12 cutting a new aluminum belly pan. Key adjustments included:
- Shifting the gearbox mounts 0.75 inches aft
- Replacing the 4-inch omni wheels with 3.5-inch Colsons for ground clearance
- Adding a 3/16-inch polycarbonate guard around the gyroscope
- Re-torquing every bolt to 22 lb-in after the first stress test

By Sunday evening, the frame was square within 0.03 inches across the diagonal.

## Software and Controls
Dr. Alvarez, our programming mentor, pushed a new odometry update on January 13. The swerve kinematics now account for the corrected wheelbase of 23.5 inches. During Tuesday's practice match against Team 4499, we logged a 94.2 percent autonomous accuracy rate—up from 78 percent the week prior.

## What Comes Next
We still need to finish the intake mechanism before the February 1 bag-and-tag deadline. The plan is to run two full endurance cycles on January 25 and finalize our pit checklist by January 28. If the drive motors stay under 140°F during the 15-minute stress run, we are confident this platform will hold up through the regional in March.
