# Quarterly Logistics Research Notes — Meridian Freight

## Executive Summary

This document summarizes operational research conducted between February 1 and March 14, 2024, across Meridian Freight's Midwest corridor. Our primary objective was to evaluate route density, warehouse utilization, and last-mile delivery variance following the Q4 2023 network restructuring. The findings indicate that while long-haul efficiency improved by 8.2 percent, regional hub congestion remains the dominant bottleneck. Lead researcher Dr. Elena Vasquez and logistics analyst Marcus Chen compiled data from 4,200 active shipments, 18 distribution nodes, and 312 contracted carriers.

## Route Performance Analysis

Data extracted from the Meridian Freight Telemetry System (MFTS) reveals significant divergence between planned and actual transit times. The Chicago-to-Detroit corridor averaged 11.4 hours, exceeding the projected 9.8-hour benchmark by 16.3 percent. Conversely, the Indianapolis-to-St. Louis lane performed 4.1 percent under budget, largely due to reduced toll-gate congestion after the I-70 widening project completed on January 22.

Key observations include:
- Carrier reliability scores dropped to 87.4 percent in February, down from 91.2 percent in December
- Fuel surcharge adjustments added $0.14 per mile on refrigerated loads exceeding 28 pallets
- Weather-related delays accounted for 34 percent of all missed delivery windows in the study period
- Night-shift dispatch accuracy improved to 96.7 percent following the new scanning protocol

## Capacity Planning

Warehouse space utilization at the Toledo hub reached 94.6 percent by March 1, triggering overflow protocols that rerouted 312 trailers to the secondary Gary facility. The Gary site, originally designed for 1,200 pallets, currently holds 1,487 pallets, pushing floor-load density beyond recommended OSHA guidelines for aisle clearance.

We recommend phased expansion of cold-storage bays 7 through 12 by Q3 2024. Preliminary cost modeling suggests a $2.4 million capital outlay, with break-even projected within 18 months if current volume growth of 6.8 percent per quarter holds steady. Dr. Vasquez has scheduled vendor negotiations with ThermoVault Systems for March 28.

## Risk Factors and Mitigation

Supply chain volatility remains elevated. The Port of Cleveland experienced a 72-hour labor action beginning February 19, delaying 18 inbound containers and cascading through three downstream delivery cycles. Insurance premiums for international intermodal cargo increased 11.5 percent year-over-year, impacting margins on cross-border lanes to Windsor and Hamilton, Ontario.

Additionally, driver retention at the regional level fell to 78.9 percent, below the national industry median of 82.1 percent. Marcus Chen proposes a tiered incentive program tied to on-time percentages rather than mileage alone, with pilot rollout targeted for April 15.

## Next Steps

Research will continue through June 30, 2024, with emphasis on automation feasibility at the Gary overflow site and predictive modeling for seasonal demand spikes. All stakeholders should review the attached appendix containing carrier scorecards, fuel-price hedging contracts, and the revised routing matrix effective April 1. Final recommendations will be presented to the Meridian Freight executive board on July 10, 2024.
