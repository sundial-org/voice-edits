# Research Notes: Meridian Pay UX Audit

## Context and Scope
This document captures findings from the Q3 usability study for Meridian Pay, our B2B invoice-automation platform. The audit ran from 14 August 2024 through 28 August 2024, covering 42 participants across treasury, AP, and vendor-relations roles.

## Methodology
We conducted moderated remote sessions using Figma prototypes tied to the v2.4.1 build. Each session lasted 45 minutes and included a task-based flow for batch approvals, dispute flagging, and real-time FX exposure checks.

## Key Findings
- 67% of users failed the multi-currency reconciliation task within the 90-second benchmark
- The primary CTA ("Confirm Settlement") lacks contrast against the slate-700 background, violating WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines
- Error messages for failed ACH pulls reference internal error code 0x8F3A without user-facing remediation steps
- Navigation between the Ledger view and the Cash-Position dashboard requires four discrete clicks instead of one

## Competitive Benchmarking
We evaluated Stripe Treasury, Brex Cash, and Ramp against our settlement-speed metrics. Ramp processes same-day wires in under 3 hours, while Meridian Pay averages 5.2 hours for domestic transactions under $50,000.

## Recommended Actions
- Redesign the settlement confirmation modal by 15 September 2024
- Add contextual help tooltips for error codes 0x8F3A and 0x9021
- Reduce navigation depth between Ledger and Cash-Position views from four clicks to two

## Next Steps
Engineering will prioritize the contrast fix in sprint 22. Design owes updated wireframes by 12 September 2024, followed by a second round of testing with 20 participants from the original cohort.
