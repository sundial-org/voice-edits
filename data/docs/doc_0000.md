# Lecture Notes: Roast Profiling at Ember & Oak Roastery

## Origin Context
Our lecture series draws from the 2019 harvest at Finca San José in Huehuetenango. Roaster-in-chief Marco Delgado developed the core curriculum after cupping 340 micro-lots that season. Understanding origin density is essential before adjusting gas flow on our 15-kilogram Probat.

## Green Grading & Moisture
Always verify green coffee specs before loading. Target moisture should sit between 10.2% and 11.4%. If readings exceed 12%, extend the drying phase by 45 seconds to avoid scorching the silver skin.

- Screen size 15/16 or larger for uniform heat transfer
- Density tests using the 100-gram graduated cylinder method
- Defect counts kept below 5 per 300-gram sample

## Roast Development
We apply a 3-phase curve: drying at 380°F, Maillard from 302°F to 388°F, and development after first crack around 401°F. For the Guatemala Antigua lot roasted on 14 March 2023, we hit first crack at 7 minutes 12 seconds and dropped at 8:45 for a 19% development ratio.

## Cupping Protocol
Post-roast rest is 24 hours minimum. Use 8.25 grams per 150 milliliters at 200°F. Score acidity, body, sweetness, and finish on the SCA sheet. Last Tuesday’s panel rated the Ethiopia Yirgacheffe 87.5 points, noting bergamot and black tea.

## Safety & Maintenance
Clean the chaff collector every 4 roasts. Check the drum bearings monthly and log temperatures in the binder. Never leave the gas valve open past 6:00 PM; the night manager, Elena Torres, conducts the final lockup.
