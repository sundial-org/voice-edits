# Orion Ridge Astronomy Club

Founded in 1987, the Orion Ridge Astronomy Club has spent nearly four decades bringing stargazers together across the Pacific Northwest. Our mission is simple: make the night sky accessible to everyone, whether you are peering through binoculars for the first time or calibrating a 14-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope.

## About Us

We are a volunteer-run nonprofit with over 320 active members ranging from high school students to retired aerospace engineers. The club maintains a dark-sky site on Mount Halloway, equipped with a roll-off roof observatory completed in March 2019. Monthly board meetings occur on the first Thursday of each month at the Ridge Community Center, and all are welcome to attend regardless of experience level.

## Equipment & Facilities

Our shared assets include:
- A 14-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain telescope with a computerized GoTo mount
- Three 8-inch Dobsonians available for member checkout
- Solar viewing filters and H-alpha telescopes for daytime sessions
- A climate-controlled library of over 400 star charts and reference texts

Members must complete a brief orientation, led by our equipment director Dr. Elena Vasquez, before borrowing any gear.

## Events & Outreach

Every clear Saturday, we host public star parties at the Ridge Observatory. In 2023 alone we welcomed more than 1,200 visitors. Our annual Messier Marathon, scheduled for March 15, 2025, draws participants from across three states. We also partner with Lincoln High School to run an after-school astronomy program for grades 9 through 12.

## Membership

Dues are $45 per year, or $25 for students and educators. New members receive a welcome packet, access to our private Slack workspace, and priority registration for the winter astrophotography workshop hosted by guest lecturer Marcus Chen.
