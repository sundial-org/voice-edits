# Project Atlas — Autonomous Navigation Platform

## Overview
Project Atlas is the 2024 competition entry developed by the University of Michigan Robotics Team (UMRT). Designed for the RoboCup@Home Open Challenge, Atlas integrates computer vision, manipulation, and natural-language interaction into a single mobile base. Development began in January 2024 with a core group of twelve undergraduate engineers and two graduate advisors.

## Hardware Architecture
The robot uses a modified TurtleBot4 chassis paired with a custom aluminum spine. Key specifications include a 14.2 kg total mass, a 1.2-meter reach arm, and dual RealSense D455 cameras mounted on a pan-tilt unit. Power is delivered by a 24 V LiFePO4 battery pack providing approximately four hours of continuous runtime. All mechanical parts were machined in-house at the Wilson Student Team Project Center.

- TurtleBot4 differential-drive base with upgraded 180 W motors
- 6-DOF Kinova Gen3 Lite arm with custom 3D-printed parallel gripper
- Onboard NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano running ROS 2 Humble
- Emergency-stop relay integrated into the motor controller loop

## Software Stack
Perception runs on YOLOv8 for object detection and RTAB-Map for 3D scene reconstruction. The navigation layer uses Nav2 with a custom costmap plugin that weighs social proximity. Speech processing is handled locally by a Whisper-based pipeline, allowing Atlas to respond to commands without cloud dependency.

## Testing and Validation
Over 340 hours of logged testing occurred between March and June 2024. Notable milestones include a 97 percent object-grasp success rate in cluttered tabletop scenarios and consistent sub-30-second task-completion times during mock trials. Dr. Elena Vasquez oversaw safety certification prior to the July 2024 competition.

## Roadmap
Next-generation work targets a lighter carbon-fiber spine, improved haptic feedback for the gripper, and expanded multilingual command support for the 2025 competition cycle. Documentation and CAD files are released under the MIT license on our GitHub repository.
