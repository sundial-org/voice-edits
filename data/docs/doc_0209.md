# Helios Ridge Solar — Commercial Roof-Mount Installation

## Project Overview
The Helios Ridge Solar initiative is a 482 kW DC rooftop photovoltaic array serving the Meridian Logistics Center in Denver, Colorado. Awarded to SunPeak Installers in March 2024, the project spans 62,000 square feet of TPO membrane roofing across two warehouse buildings constructed in 2018. Our goal is to offset approximately 78 percent of the facility’s annual electricity consumption while maintaining full warehouse operations throughout the installation period.

## System Specifications
We are deploying 1,104 monocrystalline modules rated at 435 W each, paired with four SolarEdge SE80KUS inverters and a rapid-shutdown-compliant power control system. The racking uses Unirac RM5 low-profile ballasted mounts to minimize roof penetrations. Key design targets include:

- Annual energy production of 712,000 kWh in year one
- A 25-year performance warranty backed by the module manufacturer
- Integration with the existing 480 V three-phase service via a 600 A bus connection
- Real-time monitoring through the SolarEdge monitoring portal for facility managers

## Installation Timeline
Work began with structural verification and pull-testing on April 8, 2024. Module delivery is scheduled for May 20, 2024, followed by four weeks of mechanical and electrical installation. We anticipate substantial completion by July 3, 2024, with utility interconnection testing and PTO targeted for August 15, 2024. All crane lifts and material staging occur during weekend windows to avoid disrupting Meridian’s 24-hour shipping schedule.

## Safety and Compliance
Every crew member holds OSHA 30 certification and receives site-specific fall-protection training before stepping onto the roof. We maintain a 100 percent tie-off policy above six feet and conduct daily safety briefings led by our on-site supervisor, Carlos Mendez. The project complies with Denver Building Code 2023, NEC 2020 Article 690, and the National Electrical Safety Code. Fire-setback pathways and ventilation clearances exceed local amendments by an additional 18 inches.
