# Research Notes — Hospital IT Group: EHR Integration & Network Resilience

## Background & Scope
On March 14, 2024, the clinical informatics team at St. Mary’s General requested a feasibility review for integrating the legacy LabConnect v2.1 module with the new Epic Hyperdrive release scheduled for Q3. The primary driver was reducing manual result-entry errors observed during the January audit, where 14.3 percent of microbiology reports required correction after initial posting. Dr. Amina Patel, Chief of Health Informatics, emphasized that any middleware must preserve HL7 FHIR R4 compatibility and maintain sub-200ms latency for critical-care units.

## Vendor Evaluation Findings
We evaluated three integration platforms—Redox Engine, Rhapsody Core, and Mirth Connect—against security, scalability, and support criteria. Key observations:

- Redox Engine offered the fastest deployment timeline at six weeks but required a $48,000 annual licensing fee.
- Rhapsody Core demonstrated superior mapping tools for LOINC and SNOMED CT, critical for the pathology lab’s 2025 accreditation cycle.
- Mirth Connect remained open-source with zero licensing costs, yet internal testing revealed a 0.8-second delay during peak concurrent loads of 350 messages per minute.
- All vendors confirmed HIPAA-compliant encryption at rest (AES-256) and support for OAuth 2.0.

## Security & Compliance Review
The cybersecurity subgroup led by Marcus Chen completed a penetration test on the proposed VLAN segmentation on April 2. Findings included two medium-severity misconfigurations in the firewall rules governing port 8443. Remediation patches were applied within 48 hours. We recommend enforcing multi-factor authentication for all service accounts accessing the integration bus by June 30, 2024, aligning with the hospital’s updated Information Security Policy v4.2.

## Next Steps & Timeline
Pending board approval at the May 17 meeting, we will initiate a 90-day pilot in the cardiology and emergency departments. Success metrics include reducing interface downtime below 0.5 percent, achieving 99.9 percent message acknowledgment within 10 seconds, and completing staff training for 42 clinical analysts before go-live. A final report is due August 12, 2024.
