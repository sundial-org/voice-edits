# Onboarding Guide: Meridian Biotech Wet Lab

Welcome to Meridian Biotech. This guide outlines the essential protocols, safety expectations, and operational rhythms for all new research associates joining our Cambridge facility.

## Safety and Certification

Before entering the BSL-2 suite, every associate must complete the institutional biosafety course led by Dr. Elena Vasquez. You will also need to schedule your respirator fit test by August 12 and keep your immunization records updated in the portal. Personal protective equipment is mandatory at all times; lab coats remain in the designated clean corridor and never enter the break room.

## Lab Layout and Equipment Access

The wet lab is divided into four zones: molecular biology, cell culture, analytical chemistry, and cold storage. New hires receive badge access to Zones A and B on their first day, with Zone C clearance granted after 30 days of supervised work. Please reserve shared instruments—such as the Thermo Fisher Q Exactive and the Agilent 1260—through the Calenda system at least 24 hours in advance.

## Daily Workflow and Documentation

A typical shift runs from 07:30 to 16:30, though tissue culture feeding requires weekend coverage on a rotating basis. Your responsibilities include:

- Logging all reagent lot numbers in the electronic batch record
- Calibrating pipettes every Monday using the ISO 8655 reference weights
- Submitting waste manifests to the safety office by 15:00 each Friday
- Archiving raw data to the server within 48 hours of collection

## Compliance and Quality

Meridian operates under ISO 17025 guidelines and maintains GLP compliance for all client-facing studies. If you observe a deviation—temperature excursions, contamination, or protocol drift—file a report within 2 hours using Form Q-104. Quarterly audits occur in March, June, September, and December; be prepared to present chain-of-custody documentation for any active project.

## Mentorship and Next Steps

Your direct supervisor is Dr. Marcus Chen, who will conduct your 90-day review on the first Friday of your third month. Attend the orientation seminar on September 4 at 10:00 in Conference Room 3B. By the end of your first week, confirm your training checklist in the LMS and reach out to HR if you have questions about benefits enrollment. Welcome to the team.
