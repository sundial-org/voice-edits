# Meridian Astronomy Club — Monthly Meeting Notes

## Attendance and Call to Order
President Dr. Elena Vasquez called the meeting to order at 7:00 PM on October 14, 2024, in the Riverside Observatory classroom. Twelve members were present, including new recruit Marcus Chen and returning observer Sarah Okonkwo. Treasurer James Whitfield distributed the updated membership roster and confirmed that dues for the 2024–2025 season remain $35 per individual and $50 per family.

## Upcoming Observing Nights
The club will host three public viewing sessions before winter:
- November 2, 2024 — Leonids meteor shower peak viewing at Black Ridge Park
- November 18, 2024 — Deep-sky targets with the 14-inch Schmidt-Cassegrain
- December 9, 2024 — Holiday star party with hot chocolate and telescope tours

Members must sign up for setup shifts by October 28 to secure parking access.

## Equipment and Library Updates
We approved the purchase of a new 8-inch Dobsonian reflector using $420 from the equipment fund. Librarian Nina Patel donated two volumes on cosmology and requested that borrowers return books within 21 days. The club’s 12.5-inch primary mirror is undergoing recoating at Optical Works in Denver, with an expected return by January 15, 2025.

## Outreach and Education
Vice President Tom Reed reported that our September school visit reached 87 students at Lincoln Middle School. We discussed expanding the program to include a solar viewing workshop in March 2025, pending approval from the district board. A proposal to partner with the county planetarium was tabled until the November meeting.

## Next Steps
Dr. Vasquez reminded everyone that nomination forms for the 2025 board are due by November 30. The next regular meeting will be held on November 11, 2024, at 6:30 PM in the same classroom. Please bring ideas for winter lecture topics and observe responsibly.
