# Weekly Status Update — GenFlux Therapeutics, R&D Division

## Executive Summary
This week the team advanced three parallel workstreams in the Immuno-Oncology pipeline. Dr. Elena Vasquez presented interim data at the Tuesday steering committee, and we received confirmation that the Q3 milestone for vector optimization remains achievable by October 14.

## Cell Line Development
The CHO-K1 master cell bank underwent passage 14 expansion in bioreactor B-07. Viability held at 96.4%, exceeding the 95% gate. We initiated glycan profiling on lots 2219-A and 2219-B to compare Fc-effector profiles ahead of the IND-enabling tox study.

- Completed fed-batch runs for clone GX-442 and GX-443
- Measured titer at 3.2 g/L and 2.9 g/L respectively
- Frozen 48 vials per clone in vapor-phase nitrogen storage
- Scheduled stability testing to begin September 9

## Analytical & QC Milestones
Validated the new SEC-HPLC method for aggregate quantification per ICH Q6B guidelines. The system suitability criteria passed with RSD below 1.1% across six injections. Meanwhile, the endotoxin assay for formulation F-202 returned 0.04 EU/mL, well within release specifications.

## In Vivo Pharmacology
The xenograft study in NSG mice reached day 28 post-dosing. Tumor volume inhibition averaged 62% for the 10 mg/kg cohort versus vehicle, with no clinical signs of cytokine release syndrome observed. Dr. Marcus Chen will finalize the pathology report by Friday.

## Operations & Safety
No reportable incidents this period. Liquid nitrogen supply was replenished on Wednesday, and the BSC-III certification audit is scheduled for September 22. All staff completed the updated biosafety refresher module.

## Upcoming Priorities
Next week we will freeze the final development candidate, initiate the 3-month accelerated stability study, and deliver the CMC update package to regulatory affairs by September 12.
