# Grant Proposal: LedgerLine — Inclusive Credit Building for Underserved Youth

## Executive Summary
LedgerLine is a mobile-first fintech platform designed to help young adults aged 18–26 establish healthy credit histories without predatory fees. Founded in 2023 by Dr. Amara Okonkwo and Marcus Chen, the app combines micro-savings targets with reporting to all three major bureaus. We seek $450,000 in seed funding to complete our pilot in partnership with the Urban Economic Development Fund and launch nationally by March 2025.

## The Problem
Over 26 million Americans are credit-invisible, and the burden falls disproportionately on first-generation college students and entry-level workers. Traditional secured cards require upfront deposits averaging $300, which excludes the very populations who need credit access most. Meanwhile, payday lending cycles trap users in APRs exceeding 400 percent, erasing months of progress. Without intervention, this gap widens intergenerational wealth inequality.

## Our Solution
LedgerLine replaces the security deposit with behavioral milestones verified through open-banking APIs. Users link a checking account, set automated transfers as low as $10 per week, and unlock incremental credit lines only after demonstrating consistent cash flow. Key features include:
- Real-time credit-score tracking powered by FICO Score 10
- Bilingual financial coaching via in-app messaging with certified planners
- Automated dispute filing for erroneous negative marks within 48 hours
- A zero-fee structure funded through merchant interchange, not user debt

## Pilot Results & Metrics
Our closed beta ran from January 2024 to June 2024 with 840 participants across Detroit and Atlanta. Results exceeded benchmarks:
- Average credit score increase of 42 points within four months
- 91 percent of users maintained on-time payment streaks longer than 90 days
- Net Promoter Score of 68, compared to an industry average of 31 for neobanks

## Budget and Timeline
The requested $450,000 will be allocated over 18 months: $210,000 for engineering and compliance (SOC 2 Type II audit by November 2024), $140,000 for community outreach through 12 partner nonprofits, and $100,000 for regulatory licensing in 15 additional states. By Q2 2025, we project 12,000 active users generating $1.2 million in annualized interchange revenue.
