# Echoes of Meridian — Internal Research Notes

## Project Context
We are three months into pre-production for Echoes of Meridian, the atmospheric puzzle-platformer pitched at Tokyo Game Show 2023. Lead designer Mara Ellison wants a hand-painted aesthetic anchored by physics-driven narrative moments. Our target release window remains Q2 2026, but the milestone schedule is already compressed after the engine migration in January.

## Art Direction & Reference Material
The visual language draws heavily from 1970s Soviet space program photography and the watercolor studies of Elena Vasquez. We have catalogued 142 reference images in the shared drive, sorted by palette temperature and structural geometry. Key takeaways from last week’s review with art director Jiro Tanaka:
- Desaturated ochres and deep teals dominate the exterior sequences
- Interior lighting should reference Vasquez’s “Blue Corridor” series
- Particle density must stay under 340 instances per scene on Switch hardware

## Gameplay Mechanics — Water Memory System
The central mechanic—restoring fractured memories by manipulating water flow—requires tighter tuning. During the February playtest with 12 external participants, 9 users failed to understand the reflection puzzle in Sector 4 within the 90-second tutorial window.

Proposed adjustments:
- Add a subtle ripple distortion cue 0.4 seconds before the mirror activates
- Reduce the vertical drop in Sector 4 from 3.2 meters to 2.1 meters
- Introduce a ghost-trace replay feature triggered after three consecutive failed attempts
- Lock the advanced viscosity toggle behind the first boss encounter

We will validate these changes in the March 14 internal build.

## Technical Pipeline & Milestones
Engine migration to Godot 4.2 completed on January 28. Shader compilation times dropped by 34 percent, but we lost custom navmesh tooling that must be rebuilt by April 4. Programmer Leo Chen estimates 80 hours of work, which conflicts with the audio middleware integration scheduled for the same sprint.

Risk mitigation options include contracting external porting support for the Switch SKU, though budget allows only $4,200 for Q1 contractors.
