# Q3 Supply Chain Resilience: Internal Logistics Briefing

## Executive Overview
On October 14, 2024, Director of Operations Marcus Chen convened the quarterly logistics review at the Denver distribution hub. The session addressed rising freight volatility across North American corridors and the need to harden our last-mile network before the holiday surge begins on November 18.

## Network Performance Metrics
Third-quarter data shows measurable stress in three primary lanes:
- The I-70 corridor averaged 34 hours of delay per 100 miles in September.
- Cross-dock efficiency at Facility 7 dropped to 82%, down from 91% in Q2.
- Fuel surcharge exposure increased by 12% compared to the July baseline.

These figures suggest we are operating closer to capacity constraints than our contingency models predicted.

## Strategic Adjustments for Q4
To stabilize throughput before peak season, the committee approved four operational changes:
- Rerouting 18% of Midwest volume through the Kansas City rail intermodal terminal.
- Adding a second shift at the Omaha consolidation center effective October 28.
- Renegotiating the carrier contract with Meridian Freight Logistics to cap rate increases at 4.5% through March 2025.
- Deploying predictive load-planning software across all 14 regional hubs.

## Risk Monitoring and Next Steps
Marcus Chen will present updated contingency maps at the November board meeting. Until then, regional managers must submit weekly bottleneck reports every Friday by 16:00 MST. Any deviation exceeding 6% from forecasted transit times should trigger an immediate escalation to the central operations desk. The goal remains clear: maintain 96% on-time delivery through the end of the fiscal year while reducing per-unit logistics costs by 9%.
