# Internal Research Notes — Q3 Direct-Air Capture Pilot

## Project Overview
The Meridian pilot site in Reykjavik, Iceland, has been running continuously since March 14, 2024. Our objective is to validate the Gen-4 solid-sorbent module under real-world humidity swings. Dr. Elena Vasquez leads the field team, coordinating with Reykjavik Energy for grid baseload.

## Performance Metrics (June–August 2024)
Over the 92-day observation window, the system captured 847 metric tons of CO₂. Energy intensity held at 1,420 kWh per ton, roughly 18% below the Gen-3 baseline. Key observations:
- Morning dew cycles reduce sorbent regeneration efficiency by 6–9%
- Nighttime geothermal steam integration improves thermal recovery by 12%
- Filter degradation accelerated after day 67, requiring mid-cycle replacement
- Ambient temperature variance (8°C to 19°C) correlates weakly with capture rate (r² = 0.31)

## Engineering Adjustments
Based on Vasquez’s July 22 memo, we are revising the desorption temperature setpoint from 95°C to 102°C for the September run. The maintenance crew will install redundant moisture traps before the October 3 shutdown.

## Next Steps

- Independent audit of the 847-ton dataset by Nordic Carbon Labs
