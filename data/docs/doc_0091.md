# Solar Installation Research Notes — Q3 Field Review

## Project Overview and Site Context
This document summarizes preliminary findings for the proposed 14.2 kW residential array at the Morales property, located at 4821 Ridgeview Lane. Initial site assessment was conducted on September 14, 2024, with follow-up shading analysis completed by technician Elena Vasquez on September 18. The roof faces 195° southwest with a 28-degree pitch, and the structural survey indicates adequate load capacity for standard rail mounting. We are targeting a production estimate of 18,400 kWh annually based on local solar irradiance data.

## Equipment Selection and Specifications
We evaluated three module options before settling on the REC Alpha Pure 405 W panels. Key specifications include:
- Module efficiency: 21.6%
- Temperature coefficient: -0.34%/°C
- Warranty term: 25 years product, 25 years performance
- Frame dimensions: 68.1 in × 44.6 in
- Weight per panel: 48.5 lbs

Inverter pairing will use the SolarEdge SE11400H-US with integrated rapid shutdown and module-level monitoring. String sizing calculations confirm six panels per string across four strings, maintaining voltage within inverter limits across the expected temperature range of -12°C to 42°C.

## Shading and Performance Constraints
Drone imagery revealed partial obstruction from a mature oak tree on the eastern boundary. Between 8:00 AM and 10:30 AM, estimated losses reach 11% during winter months. We recommend either selective pruning or an optimized layout shifting the eastern array 1.2 meters westward. Net energy yield after shading adjustments is projected at 16,350 kWh per year, which still covers approximately 94% of the homeowner's reported 17,200 kWh annual consumption.

## Permitting and Timeline Notes
The City of Meridian requires structural stamps and electrical single-line diagrams prior to issuance. Based on recent submissions, the review period averages 14 business days. We should submit by October 4, 2024, to secure interconnection approval before the November rate adjustment takes effect on November 1. Utility interconnection agreement must reference the existing 200 A service panel, which will require a 60 A breaker backfeed with a derated bus calculation.

## Installation Logistics
Roof access is straightforward via the rear driveway, but the garage eave limits crane placement within 6 feet of the east wall. Material delivery is scheduled for October 9 with installation spanning three days: October 10 for racking and flashing, October 11 for module and inverter mounting, and October 12 for commissioning and inspection scheduling. Weather contingency should be monitored closely given the 35% precipitation forecast for that week.
