# MetroLink Transit Authority — New Operator Onboarding Guide

Welcome to the MetroLink Transit Authority. This guide outlines the essential steps, policies, and resources you need before operating revenue service on the Red Line, Blue Line, or Express Bus Network.

## Pre-Service Requirements

Before your first assigned shift, complete all documentation and background verification through Human Resources at 400 Transit Plaza, Suite 200. All new operators must finish the 40-hour classroom orientation and pass the Department of Transportation physical examination. Your employee identification badge, issued on Day 1, grants access to depots at Westfield Yard and Northgate Terminal.

## Training Schedule and Certification

The formal training program runs for 6 consecutive weeks, beginning the first Monday of each month. Cohort 47 starts on September 8, 2025. You will rotate through simulator labs, route familiarization, and customer service modules supervised by Lead Instructor Maria Delgado. By Week 4, you must demonstrate competency in emergency braking procedures and ADA-compliant boarding protocols.

## Safety and Operational Standards

Safety is our highest priority. Review the following protocols before signing your operator agreement:
- Conduct a full pre-trip inspection using Checklist 12-B; report defects immediately to Dispatch.
- Adhere to the 25 mph speed limit in all downtown corridors and school zones.
- Maintain a minimum 3-second following distance in all weather conditions.
- Wear the approved high-visibility vest and steel-toe boots provided in your starter kit.

## Benefits and Payroll Details

Your pay rate begins at Grade 3, Step 1, with biweekly direct deposit on Fridays. Health coverage through the City Municipal Plan becomes active after 30 calendar days. You accrue 1 hour of sick leave per 30 hours worked. For retirement, enroll in the Transit Pension Fund within 90 days of hire; contributions are matched up to 5 percent.

## First 90 Days and Mentorship

During your probationary period, you will be paired with a senior mentor—typically an operator with at least 5 years of accident-free service. Attend the monthly safety meeting held every third Thursday in the Northgate Training Room. By the end of your 90-day review, you should complete 120 hours of supervised revenue service and submit your self-assessment form to your depot supervisor.

If you have questions, contact the Onboarding Team at onboarding@metrolink.gov or call extension 4420. Welcome aboard.
