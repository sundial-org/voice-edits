# Product Requirements Document: Equinox Dark Roast — Spring 2026 Release

## Overview
Meridian Coffee Roasters will launch Equinox Dark Roast as a limited seasonal offering sourced exclusively from the Finca La Esperanza cooperative in Huehuetenango, Guatemala. The product targets wholesale accounts and direct-to-consumer subscribers seeking a full-bodied profile with low acidity. Head roaster Elena Vasquez will oversee profile development, with quality validation performed by Q Grader Marcus Chen. The blend must achieve a minimum cupping score of 85 points and ship in 12-ounce recyclable canisters by March 15, 2026.

## Objectives
- Establish a premium seasonal SKU that differentiates Meridian from commodity dark roasts.
- Maintain consistent batch sizes of 12 kilograms to preserve small-batch traceability.
- Secure 200 pre-orders from existing subscription members before the public release on April 2, 2026.
- Ensure all packaging carries the new carbon-neutral certification seal verified by GreenTrace Auditing.

## Functional Requirements
The roasting team must complete profile locking within 14 days of green coffee arrival. Requirements include:
- Target development time of 18 percent post-first crack, with a drop temperature of 432°F.
- Grind calibration for espresso, drip, and French press using the Mahlkönig EK43.
- Sensory benchmarks: dark chocolate, toasted walnut, and black cherry with a finish score no lower than 8.0.
- Packaging must include a roast date stamp, origin map, and brewing guide printed with soy-based inks.

## Timeline and Milestones
Green coffee arrives at the Portland facility on February 10, 2026. Profile development runs through February 24, followed by production cupping on March 1. Packaging artwork approval is due March 5. Full production begins March 10, with warehouse fulfillment completed by March 13 to meet the March 15 ship date. Marketing assets, including photography by Juno Wright, will be delivered by February 28.

## Risks and Mitigations
Supply delay from Finca La Esperanza remains the highest risk; we will maintain a backup lot of 500 kilograms from the same harvest. If cupping scores fall below 85, the batch will be redirected to the house blend program rather than released as Equinox. Finally, canister supply chain disruptions will be mitigated by confirming inventory with Pacific Container by January 20, 2026.
