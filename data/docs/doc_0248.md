# BrightFuture EdTech Nonprofit: Annual Impact Summit 2025 Event Plan

## Overview and Objectives
BrightFuture EdTech Nonprofit will host its Annual Impact Summit 2025 on March 14, 2025, at the Oakland Convention Center. The event gathers 320 educators, district administrators, and technology partners to showcase how open-source learning platforms reduce achievement gaps in underserved communities. Our goal is to secure $480,000 in renewed partner commitments, recruit 45 new volunteer mentors, and launch the pilot rollout of the "AccessNow" digital literacy toolkit in 12 Bay Area middle schools.

## Program Schedule
The summit runs from 8:30 a.m. to 4:00 p.m. Keynote speaker Dr. Aisha Patel, Superintendent of Riverside Unified, opens at 9:15 a.m. with data from the 2024-2025 academic year. Breakout tracks cover adaptive assessment tools, student data privacy, and community broadband access. Lunch, sponsored by the Chen Family Foundation, features presentations from three student ambassadors from Lincoln Middle School.

## Logistics and Venue
The Oakland Convention Center Hall B accommodates up to 400 attendees with full AV integration. Registration opens February 3, 2025, via the BrightFuture portal. The venue contract requires final headcount by February 28, 2025. We will provide live Spanish interpretation, wheelchair-accessible seating, and childcare for 25 families through our partnership with Oakland Early Learning Cooperative.

## Marketing and Outreach
Campaign launch targets January 20, 2025, across email, LinkedIn, and local NPR sponsorships. Messaging highlights outcomes: 18,000 students served, 92% teacher retention in partner districts, and 34% gains in digital competency scores. Marketing lead Javier Morales will coordinate with district communications officers by February 10, 2025.

## Sponsorship and Budget
- Platinum sponsor tier ($25,000): exclusivity for keynote branding and three exhibition booths
- Gold sponsor tier ($12,000): logo placement in program guide and one breakout session sponsorship
- Community partner tier ($5,000): digital recognition and admission for four staff members
The total event budget is $142,000, with 65% covered by sponsors and 35% from the BrightFuture program reserve.

## Volunteer Coordination and Risk Management
We need 55 volunteers divided into registration, tech support, accessibility, and hospitality teams. Volunteer training occurs March 7, 2025, via Zoom. Safety protocols include first-aid stations staffed by certified EMTs, clear emergency exit signage, and a weather contingency plan if attendance exceeds indoor capacity. Event director Maria Santos holds final authority on all day-of adjustments.
