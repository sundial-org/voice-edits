# Grant Proposal: OpenLedger — Democratizing Small-Business Credit Through Real-Time Cash-Flow Intelligence

## Executive Summary
OpenLedger is a fintech platform founded in March 2023 by Dr. Amara Okonkwo and Marcus Chen that replaces static credit scores with live operational data. We seek $1.2 million in non-dilutive funding to scale our pilot with the Community Development Financial Institutions Fund (CDFIF) and deploy our API across 400 regional banks by Q4 2025.

## Problem Statement
Traditional underwriting excludes 42% of American small businesses because legacy models rely on tax returns that are 18 months stale. In 2024 alone, this gap denied an estimated $82 billion in working capital to viable enterprises, particularly those led by women and minority founders.

## Proposed Solution
OpenLedger integrates directly with accounting software, point-of-sale terminals, and treasury accounts to generate a dynamic “Liquidity Health Index” updated every 24 hours. Our core innovations include:
- Real-time cash-flow forecasting with 94% accuracy over a 90-day horizon
- Privacy-preserving tokenization that never stores raw bank credentials
- Automated compliance reporting aligned with the CFPB’s 2025 open-banking rules

## Implementation Timeline
Phase 1 (January–June 2025): Complete SOC 2 Type II audit and onboard 50 pilot merchants.
Phase 2 (July–December 2025): Launch with CDFIF partner institutions and process $50 million in underwriting volume.

## Budget and Impact
The $1.2 million grant covers engineering (55%), regulatory compliance (20%), and community outreach (25%). We project that every dollar invested will unlock $18 in previously inaccessible credit for underserved entrepreneurs by the end of 2026.
