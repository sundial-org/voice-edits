# Biotech Lab Notes — Mammalian Cell Culture Protocols

## Introduction and Scope

These notes summarize standard operating procedures established by Dr. Elena Vasquez for the Spring 2024 term. All experiments use HEK293T suspension lines maintained in our BSL-2 facility. Review the updated risk assessment from March 12, 2024 before handling viral vectors.

## Materials and Reagents

Prepare the following stocks fresh each Monday:

- DMEM high-glucose with 10% FBS (heat-inactivated, 56 °C for 30 minutes)
- Penicillin-streptomycin at 100 U/mL final concentration
- TrypLE Express enzyme for dissociation
- Phosphate-buffered saline, pH 7.4, sterile-filtered through 0.22 µm membranes
- Cryovials rated for liquid nitrogen storage (Nunc 2.0 mL, catalog #375418)

## Cell Passaging Protocol

Maintain cultures at 37 °C, 5% CO2, and 85% humidity. When confluency reaches 80%—typically every 48 to 72 hours—perform a 1:6 split. Count viable cells using the Countess II automated counter; record density and viability in the lab notebook. Do not exceed passage number 25 for transfection-grade work.

## Transfection and Analysis

Use Lipofectamine 3000 at a 3:1 reagent-to-DNA ratio. For reporter assays, plate 2.5 × 10⁵ cells per well in a 24-well format 18 hours prior to transfection. Harvest lysates at 24 and 48 hours post-transfection. Quantify luciferase signal on the GloMax Discover instrument; normalize to Renilla internal control.

## Safety and Waste Handling

All liquid waste containing viral particles must be treated with 10% bleach for 30 minutes before drain disposal. Sharps go into red puncture-resistant containers. Report any spills exceeding 50 mL to the biosafety officer immediately. Lock freezers at -80 °C every evening; log temperatures in the digital system.
