# Notes from the Tuesday Listening Session — Frequency Collective

## Context and Setup
On March 14, 2024, the Frequency Collective gathered in Studio B to dissect the mixing philosophy behind Juno Rivera’s 1998 underground release *Glass Threshold*. Our goal was to map analog saturation techniques onto modern digital workflows without losing the warmth that defined the era.

## Key Observations
Rivera relied on a 12-track reel-to-reel running at 30 ips, which introduced a gentle roll-off above 14 kHz. We noticed three distinct behaviors:
- The kick drum was high-pass filtered at 42 Hz, not 30 Hz, preserving punch without mud.
- A 3 dB boost at 2.4 kHz on the lead synth created presence without harshness.
- Reverb sends were panned 15 percent left to maintain mono compatibility.

## Action Items for Next Week
We agreed to rebuild one reference mix using only stock plugins and compare it against Rivera’s original masters by April 2. Members should bring stems rendered at 48 kHz / 24-bit.

## Closing Thoughts
The collective left convinced that limitation breeds creativity. Rivera’s four-stage compression chain—each stage contributing no more than 2 dB of gain reduction—proved more transparent than a single aggressive plugin. Next session, we tackle drum bus saturation.
