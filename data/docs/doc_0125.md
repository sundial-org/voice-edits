# Q3 Incident Response & Product Strategy Sync — ApexShield Security

## Attendees & Context
Held on October 14, 2024, in Conference Room B and via Zoom. Present were Marcus Chen (VP Engineering), Dr. Elena Vasquez (Threat Intel Lead), Priya Nair (Product Manager), and Julian Torres (CISO). The session focused on aligning the Sentinel One EDR rollout with recent adversary behavior observed in the healthcare vertical.

## Threat Landscape Update
Dr. Vasquez presented data from the past 90 days. Ransomware affiliates have increased lateral-movement speed by 34%, with median dwell time dropping to 72 hours. Two named clusters—LockBit 3.0 and Akira—are actively exploiting unpatched VPN concentrators using CVE-2024-21762. Our telemetry shows 18 confirmed customer incidents tied to this vector since September 1. Vasquez emphasized that static IOCs are insufficient; we need behavioral heuristics deployed by Q4 2024.

## Product Roadmap Decisions
The team agreed to fast-track the "GhostTrace" memory-forensics module. Nair confirmed engineering resources are available after the October 28 release of v4.2.1. Key priorities:
- Integrate real-time YARA-X scanning into the endpoint agent by November 15
- Reduce false-positive rates on behavioral detections to under 4% through model retraining
- Deliver an automated containment playbook that isolates hosts within 90 seconds of high-confidence detection

Marcus Chen noted that the 90-second SLA requires kernel-level driver updates, which will need extended QA on Windows Server 2019 and 2022.

We will reconvene on October 28 following the v4.2.1 launch to review deployment metrics. The Q4 board update should highlight the 72-hour dwell-time reduction target and the November 15 feature milestone.
