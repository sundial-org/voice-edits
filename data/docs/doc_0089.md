# Grant Proposal: Solar-Powered Micro-Roastery Expansion for Meridian Coffee Collective

## Executive Summary
Meridian Coffee Collective, founded in 2019 by head roaster Elena Vasquez, seeks $85,000 to expand our Portland facility with a solar-powered Loring S35 Kestrel roaster and direct-trade sourcing partnerships in Huehuetenango, Guatemala. Our 2024 production reached 14,200 pounds, yet demand exceeds capacity by 40 percent. This grant will eliminate our fossil-fuel dependency, create three full-time jobs, and double output by Q2 2026.

## Project Goals and Timeline
We will complete equipment installation by March 15, 2025, followed by staff certification in April. The Loring S35 reduces emissions by 80 percent compared to our current 2017 Probat drum. By June 2025, we aim to roast 28,000 pounds annually, supplying 18 regional cafés and our flagship shop on Hawthorne Boulevard.

Key milestones include:
- January 2025: Secure permits and solar array contracts
- March 2025: Install roaster and ventilation upgrades
- May 2025: Launch direct-trade contracts with Finca El Injerto
- August 2025: Achieve carbon-neutral roasting certification

## Community and Economic Impact
Meridian employs eight people at $22 per hour, well above Oregon’s minimum wage. Expansion will add a green-coffee buyer, a quality-control technician, and an apprentice roaster from the Portland Youth Employment Program. We commit to purchasing 6,000 pounds of green coffee through fair-trade cooperatives by December 2025, returning an estimated $48,000 directly to Guatemalan producers.

## Budget and Sustainability
The $85,000 allocation breaks down as follows: $42,000 for the Loring roaster, $18,000 for rooftop solar panels, $15,000 for building modifications, and $10,000 for training and travel. Energy savings of $620 monthly will recover solar costs within 30 months. All packaging will shift to compostable bags printed with soy ink by July 2025, completing our zero-waste roasting cycle.

## Conclusion
With this investment, Meridian Coffee Collective transforms from a neighborhood roastery into a regional model for sustainable craft coffee. We invite the Foundation to partner in a project grounded in measurable climate action, equitable labor, and exceptional quality.
