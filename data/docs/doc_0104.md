# Incident Postmortem: EHR Outage at Mercy General — March 14, 2024

## Executive Summary
On March 14, 2024, at 02:17 EST, the Epic EHR environment serving Mercy General Hospital experienced a total service interruption lasting 4 hours and 12 minutes. The root cause was an untested database patch applied during the 01:00 maintenance window by engineer David Chen. Patient registration, pharmacy verification, and telemetry feeds went offline. No protected health information was breached. All clinical operations were restored by 06:29 EST with full data integrity confirmed.

## Timeline of Events
- **01:00 EST** — Scheduled maintenance begins; David Chen deploys patch v9.4.2 to the primary SQL cluster.
- **02:17 EST** — Monitoring alerts trigger for connection pool exhaustion; EHR login fails across all 14 nursing stations.
- **02:45 EST** — Incident commander Dr. Sarah Whitmore activates the downtime protocol and notifies the clinical shift supervisor.
- **04:30 EST** — Rollback to v9.4.1 completed; replication lag observed at 8 minutes.
- **06:29 EST** — Full service restored; pharmacy queue of 312 orders begins reconciliation.

## Impact Assessment
The outage affected 847 active patient records in the acute care tower. Critical impacts included:
- Delayed medication administration for 23 ICU patients, mitigated by paper MAR backups.
- Cancellation of 6 elective surgeries scheduled between 05:30 and 08:00 EST.
- Temporary loss of telemetry for 41 monitored beds, requiring manual vital sign checks every 15 minutes.
- Zero data corruption detected; audit logs verified 100% transaction consistency post-recovery.

## Root Cause Analysis
The patch v9.4.2 introduced an undocumented query-plan change under high concurrency. The staging environment only simulated 120 concurrent users, whereas production routinely handles over 2,400. No rollback script was pre-staged. The change advisory board approved the deployment on March 12, 2024, without requiring load-test evidence.

## Corrective Actions
- Rebuild staging cluster to mirror production concurrency of 2,400 users by April 30, 2024.
- Mandate pre-staged rollback scripts for all database patches; enforce 48-hour observation window.
- Require load-test reports signed by both engineering and clinical informatics before CAB approval.
- Conduct quarterly downtime drills with nursing leadership; next drill scheduled for June 10, 2024.

## Lessons Learned
Technology failures in clinical environments cascade faster than in standard enterprise settings. The 4-hour and 12-minute window revealed strengths in paper-based backup protocols but exposed gaps in pharmacy integration. Going forward, every production change must pass through the revised CAB gate with empirical performance data, not just vendor release notes.
