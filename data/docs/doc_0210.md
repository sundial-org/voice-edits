# Incident Postmortem: EHR Outage at Mercy General — March 14, 2024

## Summary
On March 14, 2024, at 02:17 EDT, the Epic EHR cluster at Mercy General Hospital experienced a cascading failure following a storage-area network (SAN) firmware update on node SAN-07. The outage lasted 4 hours and 23 minutes, affecting 312 clinical workstations across the ED, ICU, and surgical wards. Dr. Elena Vasquez, Chief of Clinical Informatics, authorized downtime procedures at 02:34.

## Impact
- 847 patient records were inaccessible in read-only mode until 06:40 EDT
- 12 scheduled surgical cases were delayed; 3 were rescheduled to March 15
- Pharmacy dispensing reverted to paper logs for 196 medication orders
- No PHI was exposed; zero data corruption detected in post-incident validation

## Root Cause
The 3.2.1 firmware patch applied at 01:55 EDT introduced a memory-leak regression in the multipath I/O driver. Under peak morning batch load—approximately 14,000 concurrent database queries—the storage controllers on SAN-07 and SAN-08 entered a deadlock loop. Redundancy failed because the cluster heartbeat timeout was misconfigured to 90 seconds instead of the standard 30 seconds, preventing automatic failover.

## Timeline
- 01:55 EDT — Firmware update completed by engineer Marcus Li
- 02:17 EDT — First alerts triggered; paging on-call team
- 02:34 EDT — Dr. Vasquez approves paper-downtime protocol
- 03:12 EDT — SAN-07 isolated; read-only mirror activated
- 06:40 EDT — Full EHR service restored; validation complete

## Remediation
- Roll back firmware to version 3.1.9 on all 8 SAN nodes by March 18, 2024
- Adjust cluster heartbeat to 30 seconds and add redundant watchdog monitors
- Conduct tabletop exercise with nursing and pharmacy leadership by April 5
- Implement automated rollback window of 15 minutes for all storage patches

## Lessons Learned
The incident revealed that change-management approvals for infrastructure updates lacked clinical-risk review. Moving forward, any SAN or database patch affecting Epic requires dual sign-off from IT Operations and Clinical Informatics, with a mandatory 48-hour observation window before production deployment.
