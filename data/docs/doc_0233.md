# SunCurrent Installers: Community Solar Access Grant Proposal

## Executive Summary
SunCurrent Installers seeks $450,000 from the Department of Energy’s Solar for All initiative to deploy 1.2 MW of rooftop solar across the Riverside Unified School District by August 2026. Founded in 2014, our certified team has completed over 850 residential and commercial installations, but rising equipment costs now block low-income adoption. This funding closes that gap.

## Project Scope and Goals
We will design, permit, and install arrays on four campuses—Lincoln High, Washington Middle, Kennedy Elementary, and the District Maintenance Yard—creating a 1.2 MW portfolio. Expected annual generation is 1.85 million kWh, offsetting 62% of district electricity use. The project includes job-training partnerships with Riverside Community College to hire 12 local apprentices for 18 months.

## Implementation Timeline
- Q1 2025: Engineering surveys and interconnection applications with Southern California Edison
- Q2 2025: Apprenticeship recruitment and procurement of 3,200 Tier-1 modules
- Q3 2025 to Q2 2026: Staged construction across all four sites
- August 2026: Commissioning, monitoring handover, and final DOE reporting

## Budget and Impact Metrics
The $450,000 grant leverages $1.1 million in district bonds and $280,000 in federal tax credits. Key allocations:
- $210,000 for module and inverter procurement
- $95,000 for apprentice wages and safety certification
- $85,000 for grid upgrades and net-metering equipment
- $60,000 for project management and 20-year monitoring

We project $3.4 million in energy savings over 25 years and 1,240 metric tons of CO₂ avoided annually.

## Conclusion
With DOE support, SunCurrent Installers will deliver measurable climate equity, skilled workforce growth, and long-term fiscal relief to Riverside families before the 2026 academic year begins.
