# Mothlight Interactive

## About the Studio

Mothlight Interactive is a four-person indie studio based in Portland, Oregon. Founded in March 2022 by lead designer Elara Voss and programmer Kaelen Reed, we build atmospheric narrative games that prioritize emotional resonance over spectacle. Our philosophy is simple: small teams can tell enormous stories. After leaving AAA development, Voss and Reed spent 14 months in a shared warehouse space prototyping the core systems that would become our debut title. Today, the studio includes audio director Marisol Chen and environment artist Jonas Park. We operate without publishers, funding development through regional arts grants and a small Patreon community of approximately 1,200 supporters.

## Our Projects

We released our first commercial game, *Velvet Drift*, in October 2023. The title is a 6-hour exploration puzzle game set across a flooded archival library, and it has sold over 40,000 copies across Steam and itch.io. We are currently deep in development on our second project, codenamed *Signal Bloom*, which we plan to announce at PAX West in 2025.

Our portfolio also includes:
- *Midnight Census* (2022) – a free narrative prototype about census workers in a fading town
- *Glass Harp* (2023) – an experimental sound-toy released during our winter residency
- *Velvet Drift* (2023) – our debut commercial release
- *Signal Bloom* (TBA) – an unannounced narrative mystery in active production

## Technical Stack

We develop primarily in Godot 4.2, with custom shader pipelines written in GLSL. Our audio middleware relies on Wwise combined with hand-authored field recordings. Because our team is distributed—Reed works remotely from Auckland—we maintain a strict version-control workflow using Git with LFS enabled for all 3D assets and audio banks. Build automation runs on a self-hosted GitLab CI pipeline that deploys nightly Windows and Linux builds to our internal QA server.

## Roadmap

The next 18 months are critical for Mothlight. By Q2 2025, we intend to complete vertical slice production for *Signal Bloom* and secure a limited publishing deal for console ports of *Velvet Drift*. We are also committed to open-sourcing our Godot dialogue plugin once it reaches version 1.0, currently targeted for late 2025.
