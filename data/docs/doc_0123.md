# MetroLink Service Restructuring: Q3 Operational Briefing

## Executive Summary
These notes summarize the internal briefing delivered by Director Elena Vasquez on September 14, 2024, regarding the phased restructuring of east-corridor bus operations. The primary objective is to reduce average headway variance on Route 42 and Route 88 from 9.2 minutes to under 6 minutes by November 1, 2024, without expanding the active fleet beyond the current 18 articulated buses assigned to Division 4.

## Route Adjustments
Starting October 2, 2024, the express overlay on the Lakefront Corridor will be suspended during midday hours (10:00 to 14:30). Local service will be reinforced using the existing loop pattern between Central Terminal and Harbor Point.

Key modifications include:
- Elimination of the temporary shuttle bridge at Canal Street, replaced by a protected left-turn lane completed in August
- Addition of two new timed stops at Riverside Park and Maritime Museum to improve accessibility metrics
- Adjustment of the Route 42 terminus from Dockside Plaza to the relocated Bayfront Transit Center

## Fleet & Maintenance
The maintenance window for the articulated fleet will shift to overnight blocks beginning October 9. This change is necessary because daytime bay availability at Division 4 has dropped to 34 percent, down from 61 percent in June.

Mechanics will prioritize brake-system inspections and HVAC filter replacements on units 4401 through 4418. Any bus exceeding 42,000 revenue miles without a full axle service will be pulled for a 48-hour corrective cycle.

## Safety & Compliance
All operators must complete the updated fatigue-management module by October 15, 2024. Incident data from the second quarter shows 7 reportable events tied to shift overruns exceeding 11 hours. The revised scheduling algorithm caps continuous duty at 10.5 hours, with mandatory 30-minute breaks after 5 hours of revenue service.

Supervisors will conduct unannounced ride-alongs on 10 percent of all shifts during the first 30 days of implementation.

## Next Steps
Division managers should submit revised rosters to Operations Control by September 27. The pilot evaluation period runs from October 2 through December 13, 2024, with a public report scheduled for release on January 8, 2025. Questions should be directed to the Office of Service Planning.
